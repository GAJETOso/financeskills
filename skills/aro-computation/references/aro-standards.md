# ARO / Decommissioning Standards: IAS 37 & ASC 410-20

## Recognition (IAS 37 / IFRIC 1; US GAAP ASC 410-20)
Recognize a decommissioning/restoration provision when: present obligation (legal or constructive) from a past event, probable outflow, reliable estimate. For oil & gas: obligation arises when the well is drilled / facility installed — capitalize the same amount into the related asset's cost (IAS 16).

## Initial Measurement
`ARO = Estimated future restoration cost × (1 + inflation)^n ÷ (1 + discount rate)^n`
- IFRS: discount at pre-tax rate reflecting current market assessment of time value + liability-specific risks (risk-adjust cash flows OR rate, not both).
- US GAAP: credit-adjusted risk-free rate (entity's own credit standing); fair-value framework with market participant assumptions + ASC 410 requires probability-weighted scenarios.

## Subsequent Measurement
- **Accretion (unwinding)**: `Opening balance × discount rate` → finance cost (IFRS) / accretion expense (US GAAP). Never restate prior accretion.
- **Estimate revisions (IFRIC 1)**: change in cost estimate, timing, or discount rate → adjust BOTH the provision and the related asset (cost model). US GAAP: upward revisions use current rate (new "layer"); downward revisions unwind oldest layers at their original rates.
- Depreciate the capitalized ARO asset over the asset's useful life (units of production common in E&P).

## Oil & Gas Specifics
- Estimates from AFE-style engineering studies: well P&A, facility removal, site remediation; update at least annually and on regulation change (e.g., NUPRC decommissioning guidelines).
- Joint ventures: recognize only your working-interest share.
- Funds set aside (escrow/decommissioning fund) are a separate asset — no netting.

## Disclosure
Rollforward: opening, additions, accretion, revisions, settlements, closing; plus discount rates, expected timing, and uncertainties.
