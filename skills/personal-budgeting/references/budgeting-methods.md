# Budgeting Methods

A comparison of the four most widely used personal budgeting frameworks.

## 1. 50/30/20 Rule
Popularised by Senator Elizabeth Warren in *All Your Worth* (2005).
- **50% Needs**: rent, utilities, groceries, transport, minimum debt payments.
- **30% Wants**: dining, entertainment, subscriptions, hobbies.
- **20% Savings & extra debt repayment**.

Best for: beginners who want a simple, low-maintenance system.

## 2. Zero-Based Budget
Every dollar of income is assigned a category until Income − Expenses = $0.
- Forces intentional spending decisions.
- Requires monthly rebuilding of the budget.

Best for: people with irregular spending or those aggressively paying off debt.

## 3. Envelope Method
Cash is divided into physical (or digital) envelopes per category. When the envelope is empty, spending stops.
- Eliminates overspending in variable categories.
- Works well paired with zero-based budgeting.

Best for: people who overspend on discretionary categories.

## 4. Pay-Yourself-First
Savings are automated on payday before any other spending occurs. The remainder is spent freely.
- Savings target is guaranteed regardless of discipline.
- Does not control spending, only ensures savings.

Best for: high earners who save enough but want more structure.

## Recommended Starting Point
Begin with the 50/30/20 rule to establish a baseline. Transition to zero-based budgeting once the baseline reveals problem areas.
