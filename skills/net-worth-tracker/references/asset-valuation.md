# Asset Valuation Guide

How to assign realistic values to assets for a personal balance sheet.

## Liquid Assets (Easy — Use Account Balances)
- Chequing, savings, money market: use the current account balance.
- Cash on hand: include if material.

## Investment Accounts
- Brokerage and retirement accounts: use the current market value shown on the statement.
- Do NOT use cost basis (what you paid) — use market value.

## Real Estate
Use current market value, not purchase price or mortgage balance.
Estimation methods (in order of reliability):
1. Recent appraisal (most accurate).
2. Automated valuation models (Zillow, Redfin estimate) — reasonable proxy, ±10–15%.
3. Comparable sales (comps) from a local real estate agent.

Subtract nothing for mortgage here — that is captured on the liabilities side.

## Vehicles
- Use Kelley Blue Book or similar guide for current private-party value.
- Depreciation is fast in the first 3 years; update annually.

## Business Equity
Difficult to value without a formal appraisal. Common shortcuts:
- **Revenue multiple**: 0.5–1× annual revenue for service businesses.
- **Earnings multiple**: 3–5× EBITDA for stable small businesses.
- Use conservatively and note the uncertainty.

## Collectibles & Valuables
- Use recent auction results or certified appraisals.
- Only include if you would realistically sell.
- Do not include sentimental items unless they have clear market value.

## What NOT to Include
- Future earnings or bonuses (not yet received).
- Value of pension promises (present value is complex — note separately).
- Life insurance death benefit (only include cash surrender value if applicable).
