# Berkus Method and Pre-Revenue Valuation

## Berkus Method
Assign value (0 to cap, classically $0.5M each, commonly stretched to $1M in hot markets) across five risk-reduction factors:
| Factor | Risk Addressed | Max (classic) |
|---|---|---|
| Sound idea | Basic value/market risk | $0.5M |
| Prototype | Technology risk | $0.5M |
| Quality management team | Execution risk | $0.5M |
| Strategic relationships | Market/competitive risk | $0.5M |
| Product rollout / early sales | Production risk | $0.5M |
Pre-money = sum (classic ceiling $2.5M pre-revenue). Calibrate the per-factor cap to local market norms — Lagos ≠ Bay Area.

## Scorecard (Payne) Method
Start from median pre-money of comparable local seed deals (M), multiply by weighted factor comparison vs. the "average" startup:
- Team 30%, Market size 25%, Product/tech 15%, Competition 10%, Marketing/partnerships 10%, Need for funding 5%, Other 5%.
`Valuation = M × Σ(weight × company rating / average)` where rating 1.0 = average.

## Risk Factor Summation
Median comp valuation ± $250K per factor across 12 risks (management, stage, political, manufacturing, sales, funding, competition, technology, litigation, international, reputation, exit), each rated −2 to +2.

## Usage Notes
- These methods price the ROUND, not the company — they exist to anchor negotiation where DCF is meaningless.
- Always triangulate ≥ 2 methods + recent comparable rounds.
- Document ratings rationale; investors will challenge each factor.
