# Startup Financial Metrics

## Burn and Runway
- **Gross burn**: total monthly cash outflows.
- **Net burn**: gross burn − cash collections. The runway number.
- **Runway**: months until cash < safety floor. Investors expect 18–24 months post-raise.
- **Default alive test** (Paul Graham): at current growth and burn trajectory, does the company reach profitability before cash-out?

## Efficiency Metrics
- **Burn multiple** = Net burn / Net new ARR. < 1 excellent, 1–1.5 good, > 2 concerning (Sacks framework).
- **CAC payback** = CAC / (new ARR per customer × gross margin), in months. < 12 strong, < 18 acceptable B2B.
- **LTV:CAC** ≥ 3 healthy (but meaningless pre-churn-data).
- **Magic number** = Net new ARR × 4 / prior-quarter S&M spend. > 0.75 → keep investing in sales.
- **Rule of 40**: revenue growth % + FCF margin % ≥ 40 (later stage).

## Revenue Quality
- **NRR (net revenue retention)**: > 110% B2B good, > 120% excellent.
- **Gross margin**: SaaS benchmark 70–85%; services-heavy mix drags it.
- **ARR vs. recognized revenue vs. billings**: never conflate (see SaaS rev rec).

## Fundraise Planning Inputs
- Months-to-milestone vs. runway: raise must cover milestone + 6-month buffer.
- Dilution math: target 15–25% per priced round.
- Bridge triggers: < 9 months runway without a started process = act now.

## Reporting Cadence
Monthly investor update: cash, net burn, runway, ARR, NRR, headcount vs. plan — consistency beats polish.
