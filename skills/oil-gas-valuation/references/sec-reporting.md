# SEC Oil & Gas Reporting Requirements

## Reserve Categories (SEC Modernized Rules, Reg S-X 4-10)
- **Proved (1P)**: reasonable certainty (≥ 90% for probabilistic); economically producible under EXISTING economic conditions. Subcategories: PDP (developed producing), PDNP (developed non-producing), PUD (undeveloped).
- **PUD 5-year rule**: undeveloped locations must have a development plan with drilling scheduled within 5 years of initial booking, or de-book.
- Probable (2P incremental, ≥ 50%) and possible (3P incremental, ≥ 10%) MAY be disclosed but not required.

## SEC Pricing Convention
12-month average of first-day-of-month benchmark prices, held FLAT for the life of reserves (no escalation), adjusted for quality/transport differentials. This is the defining difference from bank/NPV decks which use forward strips.

## Standardized Measure (ASC 932)
PV of future net revenues from proved reserves: SEC prices × production schedule − development costs − production costs − income taxes, all discounted at a MANDATED 10% — the "standardized measure." **PV-10** is the non-GAAP pre-tax cousin (reconcile when disclosed).

## Required Disclosures (annual)
- Reserve quantities rollforward by product and geography: revisions, extensions/discoveries, improved recovery, purchases/sales, production.
- Capitalized costs, costs incurred (acquisition/exploration/development), results of operations for O&G activities.
- Standardized measure rollforward.
- Third-party reserve engineer involvement and internal qualified person credentials.

## Accounting Method Interplay
- Successful efforts vs. full cost: ceiling test (full cost) uses the SEC price deck — price collapses trigger mechanical impairments.
- Nigeria note: NUPRC reserve reporting and local content rules sit alongside; reconcile SEC-basis vs. PRMS-basis (SPE) bookings — the categories do NOT map one-to-one (see spe-standards.md).
