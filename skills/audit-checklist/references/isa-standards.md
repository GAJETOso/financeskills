# International Standards on Auditing (ISA)

Overview of the professional standards for the performance of financial audits of historical financial information.

## 🏛️ Core Principles

### ISA 200: Overall Objectives
The objective of an audit is to obtain reasonable assurance about whether the financial statements as a whole are free from material misstatement, whether due to fraud or error.

### ISA 240: Auditor’s Responsibilities Relating to Fraud
Distinguishes between fraud (deliberate) and error (unintentional). Requires auditors to maintain professional skepticism throughout the audit.

### ISA 315: Identifying Risks of Material Misstatement
The auditor must understand the entity and its environment, including internal controls, to identify and assess the risks of material misstatement.

---

## 🔍 The Audit Opinion Types
1.  **Unmodified (Unqualified)**: The "Clean" opinion. Statements are presented fairly.
2.  **Qualified**: Material but not pervasive misstatement found. "Except for [X], everything is fair."
3.  **Adverse**: Material and pervasive misstatement. The statements are misleading.
4.  **Disclaimer**: The auditor is unable to obtain sufficient evidence to form an opinion.
