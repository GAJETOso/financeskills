# Internal Control Standards: COSO Framework

The gold standard for designing, implementing, and assessing internal control for financial reporting.

## 🏛️ The Five Components of COSO
The COSO cube identifies five integrated components:

### 1. Control Environment
The set of standards, processes, and structures that provide the basis for carrying out internal control across the organization.
- **Key Signal**: The "Tone at the Top" set by management.

### 2. Risk Assessment
A dynamic and iterative process for identifying and analyzing risks to achieving objectives.
- **Key Signal**: Regular SWOT analyses and risk registries.

### 3. Control Activities
The actions established through policies and procedures that help ensure that management’s directives to mitigate risks to the achievement of objectives are carried out.
- **Examples**: Approvals, authorizations, verifications, reconciliations, and segregation of duties.

### 4. Information & Communication
The internal and external communication of information necessary to support the functioning of other components of internal control.

### 5. Monitoring Activities
Ongoing evaluations, separate evaluations, or some combination of the two used to ascertain whether each of the five components of internal control is present and functioning.

---

## 🔍 Common Control Gaps
- **Lack of Segregation of Duties**: The same person can initiate, approve, and record a transaction.
- **Inadequate Documentation**: No audit trail for approvals.
- **Override of Controls**: Management bypassing established procedures.
