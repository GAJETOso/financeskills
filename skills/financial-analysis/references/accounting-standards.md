# Accounting Standards: IFRS vs US GAAP

Detailed comparison of the two primary financial reporting frameworks. Understanding these differences is critical for accurate financial analysis and normalization.

## 🏛️ Framework Overview

### IFRS (International Financial Reporting Standards)
- **Governance**: International Accounting Standards Board (IASB).
- **Usage**: Used in 140+ countries (EU, Canada, Australia, most of Africa).
- **Philosophy**: Principles-based. Focuses on the "substance over form" of a transaction.

### US GAAP (Generally Accepted Accounting Principles)
- **Governance**: Financial Accounting Standards Board (FASB).
- **Usage**: Mandatory for all US public companies.
- **Philosophy**: Rules-based. Highly detailed with specific guidance for various industries.

---

## 🔍 Key Differences

### 1. Inventory Valuation
- **IFRS**: LIFO (Last-In-First-Out) is prohibited. Only FIFO and Weighted Average are allowed.
- **GAAP**: LIFO is allowed and commonly used for tax benefits.
- **Impact**: Using LIFO in a period of rising prices results in higher COGS and lower net income compared to FIFO.

### 2. Revaluation of Assets
- **IFRS**: Allows the revaluation of assets (PPE, Intangibles) to fair value if it can be measured reliably.
- **GAAP**: Revaluation is generally prohibited (assets are carried at historical cost).

### 3. Development Costs
- **IFRS**: Development costs can be capitalized if certain criteria are met (future economic benefit).
- **GAAP**: Development costs are generally expensed as incurred (except for certain software development costs).

### 4. Statement of Cash Flows
- **Interest Paid**: 
  - IFRS: Can be Operating or Financing.
  - GAAP: Must be Operating.
- **Dividends Received**:
  - IFRS: Can be Operating or Investing.
  - GAAP: Must be Operating.
