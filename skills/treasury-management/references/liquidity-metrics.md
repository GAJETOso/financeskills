# Liquidity Metrics and Cash Management

## Core Metrics
- **Daily cash position** = Σ available balances (by currency, by bank) + committed undrawn facilities − same-day obligations.
- **Cash conversion cycle (CCC)** = DSO + DIO − DPO. Shorter = less funding needed. Track quarterly by business unit.
  - DSO = AR / Revenue × days; DIO = Inventory / COGS × days; DPO = AP / COGS × days.
- **Minimum liquidity threshold**: ≥ (1 month opex + debt service + a stress buffer); board-approved, never breached silently.
- **Liquidity headroom** = unrestricted cash + undrawn committed facilities − minimum threshold.

## Forecasting Discipline
- **13-week rolling forecast** (weekly buckets): receipts from AR aging × collection curve; disbursements from AP, payroll calendar, tax calendar, debt service, capex schedule.
- Direct method for 13 weeks; indirect (P&L-derived) for 12 months.
- Variance discipline: weekly actual-vs-forecast, investigate > 10% line variances; rolling re-forecast every week, not month.

## Concentration & Structure
- Cash pooling: physical sweep vs. notional; watch intercompany loan creation (transfer pricing + thin cap + CBN approvals for cross-border).
- Counterparty limits: max % of cash per bank, by rating tier; government securities ladder for excess.
- Trapped cash map: balances by jurisdiction with repatriation constraints (FX controls, dividend capacity) — Nigeria: CBN documentation requirements make this a real planning item.

## Stress Testing
Run quarterly: top customer non-payment (30/60/90-day), 20% FX devaluation, revolver unavailable, 1-month revenue stop. Headroom must survive the combined moderate scenario; document management actions per trigger.

## Early-Warning Indicators
DSO creep > 5 days vs. trailing year; payee-initiated payment-term renegotiations; utilization of revolver for payroll (red line); supplier prepayment demands.
