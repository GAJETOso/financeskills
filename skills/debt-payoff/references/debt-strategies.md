# Debt Payoff Strategies

## Avalanche Method
Target the debt with the **highest interest rate** first while paying minimums on all others.

**Why it wins mathematically**: High-rate debt accrues interest fastest. Eliminating it first reduces the total interest pool immediately.

**Research backing**: A 2012 study by Amar et al. (*Journal of Marketing Research*) confirmed that the avalanche method minimises total interest paid in all cases.

## Snowball Method
Target the debt with the **lowest balance** first while paying minimums on all others.

**Why it wins behaviourally**: Clearing a debt account produces a concrete "win" that boosts motivation. Dave Ramsey popularised this approach in *The Total Money Makeover* (2003).

**Research backing**: Keinan & Kivetz (2011) found that psychological momentum from small wins can improve follow-through on long-term financial plans.

## When Avalanche Beats Snowball by the Most
- Large spread between the highest and lowest interest rates (e.g., 22% vs. 4%).
- Large balances on high-rate debts.
- User has strong discipline and doesn't need the motivational boost.

## When to Consider Snowball Instead
- Multiple small balances that can be cleared within 1–3 months.
- User has previously abandoned debt payoff plans.
- The interest rate difference between debts is small (<3%).

## Rollover Principle (Both Methods)
When a debt is paid off, add its former minimum payment to the payment for the next target debt. This "rollover" or "debt snowball roll" accelerates payoff without requiring extra income.

## Sources
- Amar, M., Ariely, D., Ayal, S., Cryder, C. E., & Rick, S. I. (2011). Winning the battle but losing the war: The psychology of debt management. *Journal of Marketing Research*.
- Ramsey, D. (2003). *The Total Money Makeover*. Thomas Nelson.
