# The Fraud Triangle: Why People Steal

A model for explaining the factors that cause someone to commit occupational fraud.

## 🏛️ The Three Elements

### 1. Pressure (Incentive)
The person has a "non-sharable" financial problem.
- **Examples**: High personal debt, gambling addiction, or pressure to meet aggressive earnings targets.

### 2. Opportunity
The person sees a way to abuse their position of trust with low perceived risk of detection.
- **Examples**: Weak internal controls, lack of segregation of duties, or management override.

### 3. Rationalization
The person justifies the act to themselves to maintain their self-image as a "good person."
- **Examples**: "I'm only borrowing the money," "I'm underpaid," or "Everyone else is doing it."

---

## 🔍 Mitigation Strategy
Management can mostly control **Opportunity** by hardening internal controls. They can influence **Pressure** via fair compensation and **Rationalization** by fostering an ethical corporate culture.
