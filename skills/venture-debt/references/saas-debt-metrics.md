# SaaS Lending Metrics and Covenants

## Borrowing Base Concepts
- **ARR-based facilities**: commitment = multiple of ARR (0.3–0.8x), stepping with NRR and growth.
- **MRR lines**: borrowing base = 3–6x MRR with retention haircuts; recalculated monthly.

## Metrics Lenders Track (and covenant)
| Metric | Typical Threshold | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Minimum cash / runway | ≥ 6 months runway or fixed floor | The covenant that bites first |
| ARR growth | > 0–20% YoY minimum | Tested quarterly |
| Net revenue retention | ≥ 90–100% | Below 90% = churn problem, expect default discussion |
| Gross margin | ≥ 60–70% | Confirms it's actually software |
| Burn covenant | actual burn ≤ 110–120% of plan | Plan attached to credit agreement |
| Performance-to-plan | ≥ 70–80% of board plan revenue | Subjective danger zone |

## Churn and Cohort Analysis for Debt Capacity
- Gross revenue churn > 2%/month makes MRR-based borrowing bases unstable — lenders haircut aggressively.
- Logo vs. revenue churn divergence reveals dependence on a few expanding whales (concentration covenant: top customer < 15–20% of ARR).

## Negotiation Priorities (borrower side)
1. Kill or objectify MAC/investor-abandonment defaults.
2. Cure rights and equity-cure provisions for financial covenants.
3. Interest-only period through the next planned raise + 2 quarters.
4. Prepayment penalty step-downs (3/2/1) and no make-whole after month 18.
5. Warrant coverage on drawn (not committed) amounts.
