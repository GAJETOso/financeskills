# Venture Lending Fundamentals

## What It Is
Term debt to VC-backed, typically pre-profit companies, underwritten on enterprise value, investor support, and runway extension logic — not cash flow coverage. Sized at 25–35% of the last equity round.

## Standard Structure
- **Term**: 36–48 months; 6–12 month interest-only period, then straight-line amortization.
- **Pricing**: 10–15% all-in — coupon (prime/SOFR + 400–700) + final payment fee (3–8% of principal) + facility fee.
- **Warrants**: 5–15% warrant coverage (= warrant value / loan amount), struck at last round price, 7–10 year exercise window.
- **Security**: all-asset lien; IP often via negative pledge rather than direct lien.
- **MAC/investor-support clauses**: subjective default triggers — negotiate hard.

## All-In Cost (model it, don't trust the coupon)
IRR of all cash flows: draw − interest − amortization − final fee − warrant value. A "12% coupon" deal with 5% final fee and warrants commonly prices 16–20% all-in.

## Lender Underwriting Lens (useful when negotiating)
- Quality of the cap table (will insiders bridge?).
- Runway with the debt ≥ next milestone + 6 months.
- Loan-to-enterprise-value < 10–20%.
- Repayment source: next round, not operations — refinancing risk is THE risk.

## When It Makes Sense
Extend runway between rounds without dilution; finance hardware/inventory working capital. When it doesn't: substituting for unrentable equity, or when amortization starts before revenue inflects — debt service accelerates the death spiral.
