# Compound Growth Guide

## The Time Value of Money

A dollar today is worth more than a dollar in the future because it can be invested and earn a return.

**Future Value formula (lump sum):**
`FV = PV × (1 + r)^n`

**Future Value with regular contributions (monthly):**
`FV = PV × (1 + r/12)^(12n) + PMT × [((1 + r/12)^(12n) − 1) / (r/12)]`

where `r` = annual rate, `n` = years, `PMT` = monthly contribution.

## Why Starting Early Matters

| Start Age | Monthly Contribution | Balance at 65 (7%) |
|---|---|---|
| 25 | $300 | $906,000 |
| 35 | $300 | $436,000 |
| 45 | $300 | $189,000 |

Starting at 25 vs. 35 yields more than double the outcome with the same contribution.

## The Cost of Stopping Contributions

Stopping contributions for just 5 years in your 30s costs more in lost growth than the contributions themselves, because early dollars have the longest compounding runway.

## Inflation Adjustment

To express a future sum in today's purchasing power:
`Real FV = Nominal FV / (1 + inflation_rate)^n`

At 2.5% inflation, $1M in 30 years has the purchasing power of ~$477,000 today.
