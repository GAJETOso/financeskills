# Accruals and Deferrals

## The Four Period-End Adjustment Types
| Type | Cash vs. Recognition | Example | Entry |
|---|---|---|---|
| Accrued expense | Expense before cash out | Unbilled legal fees | Dr Expense / Cr Accrued Liability |
| Accrued revenue | Revenue before cash in | Unbilled services delivered | Dr Accrued Receivable / Cr Revenue |
| Deferred (prepaid) expense | Cash out before expense | Annual insurance premium | Dr Prepaid Asset at payment; amortize Dr Expense / Cr Prepaid |
| Deferred revenue | Cash in before revenue | Annual SaaS subscription billed upfront | Dr Cash / Cr Deferred Revenue; release Dr Deferred Revenue / Cr Revenue |

## Estimation Standards for Accruals
- Base on invoices received post-close, PO/GRNI reports, contracts, and run-rates.
- Document the basis; an accrual without support fails audit.
- Reverse auto-reversing accruals on day 1 of the next period; true-up against actuals and track accrual accuracy (target: |true-up| < 5% of accrual).

## Cut-Off Rules
- Goods: recognize when control transfers (shipping terms — FOB shipping point vs. destination).
- Services: recognize as performed, supported by timesheets/completion evidence.
- Three-way match exceptions at close → GRNI accrual.

## Red Flags
- Accruals flat period-over-period (stale, not re-estimated).
- Deferred revenue declining while billings grow (release schedule error).
- Round-number accruals with no support.
