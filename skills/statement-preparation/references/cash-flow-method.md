# Cash Flow Statement: Indirect Method

## Structure (IAS 7 / ASC 230)
1. **Operating**: Start with net income, add back non-cash items, adjust for working capital deltas.
2. **Investing**: Capex, acquisitions, asset disposals, investment purchases/sales.
3. **Financing**: Debt draws/repayments, equity issues/buybacks, dividends, lease principal (IFRS 16).

## Operating Section Walk
```
Net Income
+ Depreciation & Amortization
+ Impairment losses
+ Share-based compensation
+/- Deferred tax expense/(benefit)
+/- Loss/(gain) on asset disposal
- Increase in receivables      (+ if decrease)
- Increase in inventory        (+ if decrease)
+ Increase in payables         (- if decrease)
+ Increase in deferred revenue (- if decrease)
= Cash Flow from Operations
```

## Classification Traps
- **Interest paid**: Operating under US GAAP; operating OR financing under IFRS (be consistent).
- **Interest/dividends received**: Operating (US GAAP); operating or investing (IFRS).
- **Lease payments (IFRS 16)**: Principal → financing; interest → per interest policy.
- **Capitalized development costs**: Investing, not operating.

## Tie-Out Checks
1. Net change in cash = closing cash − opening cash (including FX effect line).
2. D&A added back must equal D&A in the fixed asset rollforward.
3. Working capital deltas must reconcile to balance sheet movements net of acquisitions/FX (non-cash portions excluded).
