# Non-Controlling Interest (NCI)

## Measurement Choice at Acquisition (per transaction, IFRS 3)
- **Fair value method ("full goodwill")**: NCI at fair value → goodwill includes NCI's share. Required under US GAAP.
- **Proportionate method ("partial goodwill")**: NCI = NCI% × identifiable net assets at fair value → goodwill is parent's share only. IFRS option.

Worked example — Parent buys 80% for 800; identifiable net assets FV = 700; NCI fair value = 190:
- Full goodwill: (800 + 190) − 700 = **290** (NCI = 190)
- Partial goodwill: 800 − 80% × 700 = **240** (NCI = 20% × 700 = 140)

## Subsequent Measurement
`NCI closing = NCI opening + NCI% × sub's comprehensive income − dividends paid to NCI ± NCI share of equity transactions`

Allocate losses to NCI even if it goes negative (IFRS 10).

## Profit Attribution
P&L shows single consolidated profit, then attribution: "attributable to owners of the parent" and "attributable to NCI". NCI share is of the subsidiary's PAT adjusted for fair-value depreciation, unrealized profit eliminations where the sub is the seller (upstream), and intragroup interest.

## Changes in Ownership Without Loss of Control
Equity transactions — no P&L gain/loss, no goodwill remeasurement. Difference between consideration and NCI adjustment goes to parent equity.

## Common Errors
1. Eliminating only the parent's share of intragroup profit (must eliminate 100%).
2. Forgetting fair-value adjustment depreciation in NCI's profit share.
3. Recording gain on buying out NCI (it's an equity transaction).
