# Consolidation Standards: IFRS 10 / ASC 810 Essentials

## Control Test (IFRS 10)
Control = power over relevant activities + exposure to variable returns + ability to use power to affect returns. Consolidate ALL controlled entities; >50% voting rights is the usual but not the only path (de facto control, potential voting rights, structured entities).

## Method Selection
| Holding/Influence | Method |
|---|---|
| Control (usually >50%) | Full consolidation |
| Joint control — joint venture | Equity method (IFRS 11) |
| Significant influence (20%–50%) | Equity method |
| < 20%, no influence | FVTPL/FVOCI financial asset |

## Consolidation Mechanics
1. Combine 100% of subsidiary line items (even at < 100% ownership).
2. Eliminate parent's investment against subsidiary equity at acquisition; difference = goodwill (or bargain purchase gain).
3. Recognize NCI (see minority-interest.md).
4. Eliminate ALL intragroup balances, transactions, and unrealized profits (inventory, fixed assets).
5. Align accounting policies and reporting dates (gap ≤ 3 months with adjustments).
6. Translate foreign subs: assets/liabilities at closing rate, income at average rate, equity at historical; differences → FX translation reserve (OCI/CTA).

## Business Combination (IFRS 3) at Acquisition
- Identifiable assets/liabilities at fair value (including intangibles not on the target's books: customer relationships, brands).
- Contingent consideration at fair value; subsequent changes through P&L (if liability-classified).
- Transaction costs expensed, NOT capitalized.

## Loss of Control
Derecognize sub, recognize retained stake at fair value, recycle CTA, gain/loss to P&L.
