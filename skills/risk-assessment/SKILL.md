# ⚖️ Risk Assessment Skill

## When to Use
Use this skill to identify, quantify, and mitigate financial risks including market volatility, credit defaults, and liquidity gaps.

## Initial Assessment
- Define the scope (e.g., specific portfolio, enterprise-wide).
- Identify key risk metrics needed (e.g., VaR, Sharpe Ratio, Beta).
- Access historical price data and counterparty credit scores.

## Steps
1.  **Metric Calculation**: Use Python to calculate Value at Risk (VaR) using Historical Simulation or Monte Carlo methods.
2.  **Sensitivity Analysis**: Perform "Stress Tests" by simulating extreme market conditions (e.g., 2008 crash, COVID-19 shock).
3.  **Credit Risk Review**: Analyze counterparty data to detect increasing default probabilities.
4.  **Liquidity Audit**: Compare current assets vs. short-term liabilities to identify potential cash flow gaps.
5.  **Mitigation Strategy**: Recommend hedging instruments (options, swaps) or asset reallocation to bring risk within tolerance limits.

## Output Format
- **Risk Dashboard**: Visual summary of key metrics (VaR, Volatility).
- **Stress Test Results**: Outcomes of various "black swan" scenarios.
- **Hedging Recommendations**: Specific actions to lower risk exposure.

## ⚖️ Disclaimer
AI risk assessments are based on historical data and mathematical models. They cannot predict future events with certainty. Financial decisions should be reviewed by a risk management professional.
