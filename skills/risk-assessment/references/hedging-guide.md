# Hedging Guide: Managing Financial Exposure

Strategies for protecting an entity from adverse price movements in markets.

## 🏛️ Common Hedging Instruments

### 1. Options
- **Put Options**: Protect against falling prices (downside protection).
- **Call Options**: Protect against rising prices (for buyers of commodities).

### 2. Futures & Forwards
Contractual obligations to buy or sell an asset at a predetermined price on a future date. Used to lock in prices today.

### 3. Swaps (Interest Rate Swaps)
Exchanging floating-rate interest payments for fixed-rate payments to protect against rising interest rates.

---

## 🔍 The Goal of Hedging
The goal of hedging is **NOT** to make a profit. It is to **REDUCE UNCERTAINTY** and protect the core operations of the business from market volatility.
