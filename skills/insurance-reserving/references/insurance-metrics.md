# Insurance Financial Metrics

## Underwriting Ratios
- **Loss ratio** = Incurred losses / Earned premium. Include LAE/claims handling (be explicit which).
- **Expense ratio** = UW expenses / Written premium (trade basis) or earned (statutory basis — state which).
- **Combined ratio** = loss + expense. < 100% = underwriting profit. 95% is strong for P&C; > 105% needs investment income to survive.
- **Operating ratio** = combined − investment income ratio.

## Reserve Health Indicators
- **Reserve-to-premium ratio** trend by line: shrinking ratio in a growing long-tail book = likely under-reserving.
- **Paid-to-incurred ratio**: rising = reserves running off true; spiking = possible case reserve weakening.
- **Survival ratio** (reserves / avg annual paid): for latent exposures (asbestos-style), benchmark vs. peers.
- **One-year reserve development / opening reserves**: persistent favorable development > 5% = padding; persistent adverse = deficiency. Track 5 years.

## Premium Metrics
- GWP vs. NWP (after reinsurance) vs. earned; unearned premium reserve roll.
- Rate change vs. loss trend: margin expands only if rate > trend.

## Solvency / Capital
- Solvency II SCR coverage ≥ 100% (comfort > 150%); NAIC RBC ratio ≥ 200% of ACL avoids action levels; Nigeria: NAICOM minimum capital + solvency margin per Insurance Act.
- Premium-to-surplus ≤ 2–3x (P&C heuristic); reserves-to-surplus by line tail length.

## IFRS 17 Lens
- CSM balance and release pattern = future profit emergence.
- Loss component on onerous contracts hits P&L immediately — watch cohort-level onerous classifications.
- Risk adjustment confidence level disclosure: compare across peers (65th–85th percentile typical).
