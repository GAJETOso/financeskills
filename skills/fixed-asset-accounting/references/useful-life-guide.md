# Useful Life Benchmarks and Policy Guidance

## Typical Useful Lives (calibrate to entity experience — these are starting points)
| Asset Class | Life (years) | Method Notes |
|---|---|---|
| Buildings — shell | 30–50 | Straight-line; componentize |
| Buildings — roof, HVAC, lifts | 10–20 | Separate components |
| Leasehold improvements | Shorter of life and lease term | Include reasonably certain renewals only |
| Plant & machinery (general) | 8–15 | SL or UoP if usage-driven wear |
| Oil & gas production facilities | Field life | Units of production on proved developed reserves |
| Pipelines | 20–40 | SL; integrity-driven component reviews |
| Motor vehicles | 4–6 | Residual values meaningful — set and review |
| IT hardware | 3–5 | Zero residual typical |
| Servers/network infra | 4–6 | Hyperscalers have stretched to 6 — follow entity evidence |
| Office furniture & fittings | 5–10 | — |
| Software (purchased/internally developed) | 3–7 | Amortize from go-live; cloud config costs usually EXPENSED (SaaS arrangements — IFRIC agenda decision) |

## Componentization Triggers
Component is "significant" if its cost is a meaningful share (rule of thumb ≥ 10–20%) of the asset AND its life differs materially. Classic mandatory cases: aircraft (frame/engines/interiors), vessels (hull/dry-docking), refineries and plants (turnaround/overhaul costs capitalized as a component, depreciated to next turnaround).

## Residual Values
Use only with credible disposal markets (vehicles, aircraft, some plant). Review annually; residual ≥ carrying amount → depreciation stops. Document the evidence (auction data, dealer quotes).

## Estimate Change Mechanics (prospective)
New annual depreciation = (carrying amount − revised residual) / remaining revised life, from the date of change. No restatement, but disclose if material.

## Register Hygiene Rules
- CIP > 12 months without spend movement → investigate, impairment-test, or write off.
- Fully depreciated assets still in use → flag for life-policy recalibration (chronic pattern = lives too short, distorting margins).
- Ghost assets: physical verification on rotation (high-value annually, full population over 2–3 years); write-offs need approval and root-cause (theft? records?).
