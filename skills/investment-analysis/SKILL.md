# 📈 Investment Analysis Skill

## When to Use
Use this skill to evaluate the potential profitability and viability of an investment opportunity (stocks, real estate, or private equity).

## Initial Assessment
- Identify the target asset or company.
- Gather 3-5 years of historical financial statements (Income, Balance Sheet, Cash Flow).
- Identify relevant benchmark indices or peer companies.

## Steps
1.  **Fundamental Analysis**: Calculate key ratios (P/E, ROE, Debt/Equity) and compare against industry averages.
2.  **Valuation Modeling**: Build a Discounted Cash Flow (DCF) model to estimate the "Intrinsic Value" of the asset.
3.  **Technical Analysis**: (Optional) Review price charts for trends, support/resistance levels, and volume indicators.
4.  **Qualitative Review**: Analyze competitive advantages (Moats), management quality, and market tailwinds.
5.  **Investment Thesis**: Synthesize findings into a "Buy/Hold/Sell" recommendation based on the margin of safety.

## Output Format
- **Investment Memo**: One-page executive summary of the opportunity.
- **Valuation Table**: Breakdown of DCF assumptions and results.
- **Competitor Matrix**: Side-by-side comparison with top peers.

## ⚖️ Disclaimer
AI investment analysis is for educational and research purposes only. It does not constitute financial advice. All investments carry risk.
