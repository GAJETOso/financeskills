# Competitive Advantage: Identifying Moats

Why some companies earn high returns on capital for decades while others fail.

## 🏛️ The Five Types of Moats (Morningstar Framework)

### 1. Network Effect
The value of a service increases as more people use it (e.g., Visa, Facebook, Microsoft Windows).

### 2. Intangible Assets
Brands, patents, or regulatory licenses that allow a company to charge more or block competitors (e.g., Coca-Cola, Disney, Pfizer).

### 3. Cost Advantage
Producing goods or services at a lower cost than competitors due to scale, location, or unique processes (e.g., Walmart, Amazon, Geico).

### 4. Switching Costs
The time, money, or emotional cost of moving to a competitor is too high (e.g., Oracle Database, ERP systems, Bank accounts).

### 5. Efficient Scale
A market is so small that it only supports 1-2 profitable players; a new entrant would make everyone unprofitable (e.g., Regional airports, Pipeline companies).

---

## 🔍 Evidence of a Moat
- Consistent **Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)** > **WACC**.
- Stable or growing **Market Share**.
- **Pricing Power** (Ability to raise prices without losing customers).
