# Valuation Guide: DCF & Multiples

Standard methodologies for determining the intrinsic and relative value of a business.

## 🏛️ Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)
The DCF is a "First Principles" valuation based on the present value of future cash flows.

### The Formula
`Value = Σ [FCF_t / (1 + WACC)^t]`
- **FCF**: Free Cash Flow (Cash from Ops - CapEx).
- **WACC**: Weighted Average Cost of Capital (Discount rate).
- **t**: Time period.

### Key Assumptions
- **Terminal Growth Rate**: Usually 2-3% (aligned with long-term GDP growth).
- **Discount Rate**: Reflects the riskiness of the cash flows.

---

## 🔍 Relative Valuation (Multiples)
Comparing a company to its peers or its own historical history.

- **P/E (Price-to-Earnings)**: Most common for profitable companies.
- **EV/EBITDA**: Used for capital-intensive industries; accounts for debt.
- **P/S (Price-to-Sales)**: Used for high-growth companies with no net income.
