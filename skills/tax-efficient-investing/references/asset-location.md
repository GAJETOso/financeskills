# Asset Location Research

Asset location is the strategy of placing investments in the account type that minimises their tax cost.

## Core Principle
Different assets generate different types of taxable events:
- **Interest income** (bonds, CDs): taxed as ordinary income — highest rate.
- **Dividends**: qualified dividends taxed at capital gains rates; non-qualified at ordinary rates.
- **Capital gains**: long-term gains taxed at preferential rates (0/15/20%); short-term at ordinary rates.
- **REIT distributions**: mostly ordinary income — high tax cost.

## Optimal Asset Location

| Asset Class | Best Account | Reason |
|---|---|---|
| Bonds / Fixed income | Tax-advantaged (traditional) | Interest taxed as ordinary income |
| REITs | Tax-advantaged | High ordinary income distributions |
| High-dividend stocks | Tax-advantaged | Avoids annual dividend tax |
| Actively managed funds | Tax-advantaged | High turnover generates short-term gains |
| Broad index funds / ETFs | Taxable | Low turnover, qualified dividends, tax-loss harvesting |
| Municipal bonds | Taxable | Already tax-exempt; wasted in tax-advantaged |
| International stocks | Taxable | Foreign tax credit only available in taxable accounts |

## Tax-Loss Harvesting
Selling a position at a loss to realise the loss for tax purposes, then immediately buying a similar (but not "substantially identical") fund to maintain market exposure.

**Annual value**: Studies estimate 0.5–1.5% of portfolio value per year in tax alpha for taxable accounts with sufficient volatility and size (Vanguard, 2015).

**Wash-sale rule (US)**: Cannot repurchase the same or substantially identical security within 30 days before or after the sale.

## Quantified Value of Asset Location
Vanguard research (Jaconetti et al., 2018) estimates optimal asset location adds 0–0.75% per year in after-tax returns, depending on tax bracket and asset mix. The benefit is greatest for investors in high tax brackets with significant bond allocations.
