# Account Types Guide

## United States

### Tax-Deferred Accounts (Pay tax on withdrawal)
| Account | Who | 2024 Limit | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Traditional 401(k) | Employees | $23,000 (+$7,500 catch-up) | RMDs at age 73 |
| Traditional IRA | Anyone with earned income | $7,000 (+$1,000 catch-up) | Deductibility phases out with 401k |
| SEP-IRA | Self-employed | 25% of comp, max $69,000 | No catch-up |
| SIMPLE IRA | Small business | $16,000 (+$3,500 catch-up) | 2-year vesting rule |

### Tax-Free Accounts (Pay tax now, withdraw tax-free)
| Account | Who | 2024 Limit | Key Rule |
|---|---|---|---|
| Roth 401(k) | Employees | Same as 401(k) | No RMDs |
| Roth IRA | Income limit applies | $7,000 (+$1,000 catch-up) | Phases out at $146k–$161k (single) |
| HSA | HDHP enrollees | $4,150 individual / $8,300 family | Triple tax advantage |

### Taxable Brokerage
No contribution limits. Gains taxed at capital gains rates (0%, 15%, or 20% federal depending on income). Qualified dividends also receive preferential rates.

## United Kingdom
| Account | Annual Allowance | Tax Treatment |
|---|---|---|
| ISA | £20,000 | Tax-free growth and withdrawals |
| SIPP | £60,000 (annual allowance) | Tax-relieved contributions, taxed on withdrawal |
| Lifetime ISA | £4,000 (25% bonus) | First home or retirement only |

## General Principles (All Jurisdictions)
1. Maximise employer-matched accounts first (free money).
2. Prefer tax-free over tax-deferred if future tax rate expected to rise.
3. Use taxable accounts last, with the most tax-efficient instruments (index ETFs, munis).
