# GHG Protocol Essentials

## The Three Scopes
- **Scope 1 — direct**: owned/controlled sources: combustion (generators, fleet, gas processing), process emissions, fugitives (flaring, venting, methane leaks — dominant in oil & gas), refrigerants.
- **Scope 2 — purchased energy**: electricity, steam, heat. Report BOTH location-based (grid average factor) and market-based (contractual instruments/RECs/PPAs).
- **Scope 3 — value chain**: 15 categories; materiality varies wildly. O&G: category 11 (use of sold products) is typically 80%+ of total footprint. Banks: category 15 (financed emissions, PCAF methodology).

## Calculation Basics
`Emissions (tCO2e) = Activity data × Emission factor × GWP`
- Factors: IPCC, national inventories, IEA grid factors, API Compendium (O&G).
- GWP: use AR5/AR6 100-year values consistently (CH4 ≈ 28–30, N2O ≈ 265–273).
- Hierarchy of data quality: measured/metered > calculated (fuel × factor) > estimated (spend-based). Spend-based Scope 3 is a screening tool, not a reporting-grade method.

## Organizational Boundaries
Choose and disclose: equity share vs. operational control vs. financial control. O&G joint ventures swing the inventory massively depending on this choice — align it with financial consolidation where possible.

## Common Errors
1. Netting renewables purchases against the location-based figure.
2. Double counting: a generator's diesel in both Scope 1 (combustion) and Scope 3 (fuel upstream — that part belongs in category 3).
3. Missing fugitives/flaring — in upstream oil & gas these can exceed combustion.
4. Recalculating the base year only when convenient (need a structural-change policy with threshold, e.g., 5%).
5. Mixing GWP vintages across years without restating.
