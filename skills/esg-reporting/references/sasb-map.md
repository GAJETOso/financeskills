# SASB / ISSB Industry Mapping

## Framework Landscape (post-consolidation)
SASB standards now sit under the ISSB (IFRS Foundation). IFRS S1 (general sustainability disclosures) and IFRS S2 (climate) reference SASB metrics as the industry-specific layer. TCFD is absorbed into IFRS S2. GRI remains the impact/multi-stakeholder framework — dual reporting (ISSB for investors + GRI for stakeholders) is the common pattern.

## SASB's 77 Industries → 5-Factor Materiality
Each industry standard selects from: Environment / Social capital / Human capital / Business model & innovation / Leadership & governance. Examples:

| Industry | Most Material Topics (illustrative) |
|---|---|
| Oil & Gas E&P | GHG emissions, water management, biodiversity, community relations, reserves valuation & capex resilience, critical incident management |
| Commercial Banks | Data security, access & affordability, financed emissions, business ethics, systemic risk management |
| Insurance | ESG integration in investments, physical risk exposure, policyholder transparency |
| Software/IT | Energy of data centers, data privacy, employee recruitment/diversity, IP & competitive behavior |
| Food & Beverage | Energy/water, packaging, ingredient sourcing, health & nutrition, labor |

## Building the Disclosure Map
1. Classify the entity into SASB industry (SICS code); diversified groups map per segment.
2. Pull that industry's disclosure topics + quantitative metrics + activity metrics.
3. Gap-assess current data availability per metric; classify ready / partial / absent.
4. Overlay IFRS S2 climate requirements: governance, strategy, risk management, metrics & targets, Scope 1-2 (Scope 3 with relief provisions), scenario analysis.
5. Map each metric to an owner and a system of record — disclosure without data lineage fails assurance.

## Assurance Trajectory
Limited assurance on GHG (ISSA 5000) is becoming the baseline; build audit-trail discipline (methodology memos, evidence retention) from day one.
