# FASB Crypto Accounting: ASU 2023-08 (ASC 350-60)

## Scope Test (all six required)
1. Meets the definition of an intangible asset;
2. Holder has no enforceable claims on underlying goods/services/other assets (excludes most stablecoins and wrapped/asset-backed tokens);
3. Created/resides on a distributed ledger;
4. Secured by cryptography;
5. Fungible (excludes NFTs);
6. Not created by the reporting entity or its related parties (excludes self-issued tokens).

## Measurement
**Fair value through net income, every reporting period** (ASC 820). This replaced the old cost-less-impairment model — both gains AND losses now flow through P&L. Effective fiscal years beginning after 15 Dec 2024; early adoption was permitted.

## Presentation & Disclosure
- Balance sheet: crypto assets presented SEPARATELY from other intangibles.
- Income statement: remeasurement gains/losses presented separately from impairments of other intangibles.
- Disclosures: per significant holding — name, cost basis, fair value, units; rollforward (additions, dispositions, gains, losses); restrictions; method for cost basis (FIFO/specific ID).

## Out-of-Scope Items (still messy)
- **Stablecoins**: fail criterion 2 → financial asset analysis; often amortized cost if redeemable 1:1.
- **NFTs**: remain under legacy intangible model (cost less impairment) — non-fungible.
- **Self-issued tokens**: no recognized asset for the issuer; proceeds analysis → liability/revenue/equity question under contract terms.
- **Staking rewards**: recognize when control obtained, at fair value — revenue vs. other income classification judgment.

## IFRS Contrast
No crypto-specific standard: IAS 38 (cost or revaluation-through-OCI if active market) or IAS 2 at FV-less-costs-to-sell for broker-traders. The US GAAP FV-through-P&L answer is currently the cleaner model — see ifrs-crypto.md.
