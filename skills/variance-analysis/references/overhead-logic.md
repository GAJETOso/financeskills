# Overhead Variance Logic: Absorption vs. Variable Costing

## The Four-Way Overhead Variance Split
**Variable overhead:**
- Spending: `(Actual VOH rate − Standard rate) × Actual hours` — price of the overhead inputs.
- Efficiency: `(Actual hours − Standard hours allowed) × Standard VOH rate` — driven by the same hours as labor efficiency.

**Fixed overhead:**
- Spending (budget): `Actual FOH − Budgeted FOH` — the only "real" fixed overhead control variance.
- Volume: `Budgeted FOH − Applied FOH` where `Applied = Standard hours allowed × FOH rate` — an ARTIFACT of unitizing fixed costs, not a cost control signal. Unfavorable volume variance = produced below denominator level.

## Denominator Choice Matters
FOH rate = Budgeted FOH / denominator activity. Choosing theoretical vs. practical vs. normal vs. budgeted capacity changes the rate and the volume variance. Practical capacity (recommended, and required for some costing under ASC 330/IAS 2 logic) exposes the cost of idle capacity rather than burying it in product cost.

## Absorption vs. Variable Costing P&L Effect
- `Absorption income − Variable income = ΔInventory units × FOH rate`
- Production > sales → absorption income HIGHER (fixed cost parked in inventory). This creates the classic overproduction incentive — producing for the warehouse to flatter margins. Watch for it in bonus-driven plants.
- IAS 2 / GAAP external reporting REQUIRES absorption; use variable costing internally for CVP and control.

## Under/Over-Applied Overhead Disposal
Immaterial → close to COGS. Material → prorate across WIP, FG, COGS based on applied overhead in each. Abnormal idle capacity cost → expense immediately, never inventory.

## Interpretation Discipline
Volume variance tells you about CAPACITY UTILIZATION, spending variance about COST CONTROL. Reporting them as one number invites the wrong fix.
