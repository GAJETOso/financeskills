# Forecasting Best Practices

Building accurate financial projections requires a blend of historical data, market insight, and statistical discipline.

## 🏛️ Core Principles

### 1. Driver-Based Modeling
Identify the "Levers" that control the numbers. Instead of just guessing revenue, model it as:
`Traffic x Conversion Rate x Average Order Value = Revenue`.

### 2. Rolling Forecasts
Don't just set a budget once a year. Update the forecast monthly or quarterly as actual data comes in (e.g., 4+8, 9+3 reporting).

### 3. Variance Analysis (BvA)
Regularly compare Budget vs. Actual. Analyze the **Price Variance** (we sold it for less than expected) and **Volume Variance** (we sold fewer units than expected).

---

## 🔍 Common Pitfalls
- **Over-Optimism Bias**: Underestimating the time or cost to achieve growth.
- **Sandbagging**: Intentionally lowering targets to ensure they are beaten.
- **Ignoring Seasonality**: Assuming a straight-line growth in businesses with holiday peaks.
