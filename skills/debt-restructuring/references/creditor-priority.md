# Creditor Priority and the Waterfall

## Absolute Priority Order (typical)
1. Super-priority / DIP financing, admin expenses of the proceeding
2. Secured creditors — to the value of their collateral (deficiency claims drop to unsecured)
3. Priority unsecured (statutory: employees' wages, certain taxes — caps vary by jurisdiction)
4. Senior unsecured
5. Subordinated / mezzanine (per subordination agreements)
6. Preferred equity
7. Common equity

## Recovery Waterfall Mechanics
1. Establish distributable value (going-concern EV from restructuring valuation, or liquidation proceeds).
2. Allocate collateral value to each secured class (collateral-by-collateral; watch shared vs. exclusive liens).
3. Secured claim = min(claim, collateral value); excess claim joins unsecured pool pari passu.
4. Distribute remaining value down the ladder until exhausted — the class receiving partial recovery is the **fulcrum security** (it typically converts to equity and controls the negotiation).

## Worked Micro-Example
EV = 400. Secured claim 250 (collateral 300) → recovers 250 in full. Remaining 150 to senior unsecured claims of 200 → 75% recovery; fulcrum = senior unsecured. Sub debt and equity: zero.

## Complications
- **Structural subordination**: lenders at OpCo sit ahead of HoldCo creditors regardless of label — map the org chart and guarantees first.
- **Guarantee/upstream claims**: double-dip claims can recover from multiple estates.
- **Intercreditor agreements**: payment vs. lien subordination, standstill periods.
- **Cram-down**: junior classes get nothing until seniors are paid in full (absolute priority), subject to negotiated gifting.
- **Nigeria/Commonwealth**: employee claims and secured creditor enforcement rights per CAMA 2020 and applicable security interests (perfection status decides everything — verify registrations).
