# Debt Restructuring Types

## Out-of-Court (consensual)
| Type | Mechanics | When |
|---|---|---|
| Amend & extend | Push maturity, fee + margin bump | Liquidity timing issue, solvent business |
| Covenant waiver/reset | Temporary or permanent covenant relief | Technical breach, fundamentals intact |
| Forbearance | Lender agrees not to enforce for a period | Buys negotiation time post-default |
| Debt-for-debt exchange | New notes (longer/cheaper/junior-to-senior uptier) for old | Discounted capture, maturity wall |
| Debt-for-equity swap | Creditors take equity, debt extinguished | Over-levered but viable operations |
| New money + priming | Fresh capital senior to existing (uptiering/drop-down) | Liquidity crisis; expect lawsuits — check docs |
| Distressed buyback | Issuer repurchases debt below par | Cash-rich issuer, bonds trading low |

## In-Court
- **Chapter 11 (US)**: debtor-in-possession, automatic stay, plan needs one impaired accepting class + cram-down power; prepacks compress timeline to 1–3 months.
- **Scheme of arrangement (UK/Commonwealth)**: 75% by value per class binds holdouts; no automatic stay.
- **Nigeria**: CAMA 2020 introduces company voluntary arrangements and administration as rescue alternatives to receivership/winding-up; AMCON intervention for eligible bank debt.

## Accounting Consequences (debtor side)
- IFRS 9: terms "substantially different" (≥10% PV test or qualitative) → extinguish old, recognize new at FV, gain/loss to P&L. Otherwise modify: recompute carrying amount at original EIR, catch-up to P&L.
- TDR/concession analysis drives creditor-side impairment.

## Selection Logic
Liquidity problem → extend/waive. Solvency problem → haircut or equitize. Holdout risk high → court process. Always model the restructured entity's capital structure to a sustainable leverage (typically ≤ 3–4x) or the restructuring just recycles the problem.
