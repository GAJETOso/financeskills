# IFRS 15 / ASC 606: The Five-Step Model

## Step 1 — Identify the Contract
Criteria: approved, rights identifiable, payment terms identifiable, commercial substance, collection probable. Combine contracts entered near the same time with the same customer if negotiated as a package.

## Step 2 — Identify Performance Obligations (POs)
A promised good/service is distinct if: (a) customer can benefit from it alone or with readily available resources, AND (b) it is separately identifiable in the contract (not highly integrated, not significantly modifying another good/service).
- Software + critical customization = often ONE PO.
- Software + standalone support = TWO POs.

## Step 3 — Determine Transaction Price
- Variable consideration (rebates, penalties, usage tiers): estimate via expected value or most-likely amount, constrained so reversal is highly improbable.
- Significant financing component if timing gap > 12 months.
- Non-cash consideration at fair value.

## Step 4 — Allocate to POs
Pro-rata on standalone selling prices (SSP). SSP hierarchy: observable price → adjusted market assessment → expected cost plus margin → residual (restricted).

## Step 5 — Recognize Revenue
Over time if ANY: (a) customer simultaneously receives/consumes, (b) asset created/enhanced is customer-controlled, (c) no alternative use + enforceable right to payment for performance to date. Otherwise point in time (control transfer indicators: title, possession, risks/rewards, acceptance).

## Contract Modifications
- Adds distinct goods at SSP → separate contract.
- Adds distinct goods NOT at SSP → prospective (terminate & replace).
- Remaining goods not distinct → cumulative catch-up.

## Disclosures
Disaggregated revenue, contract balances (contract assets/liabilities), remaining performance obligations (backlog), significant judgments.
