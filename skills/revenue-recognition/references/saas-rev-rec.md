# SaaS Revenue Recognition Patterns

## Typical POs in a SaaS Contract
| Element | Distinct? | Pattern |
|---|---|---|
| Subscription/hosting | Yes (the core PO) | Ratable over term |
| Implementation/setup | Usually NOT distinct if no standalone value | Roll into subscription, recognize over term (or over expected customer life if material) |
| Premium support | Yes if sold separately | Ratable |
| Usage/overage fees | Variable consideration | Recognize as usage occurs (royalty-style exception often applies) |
| Professional services (config, training) | Often distinct | As delivered |

## Key Judgments
- **Setup fees**: Non-refundable upfront fees rarely transfer a distinct service → defer and amortize. Material renewal option advantage → amortize over expected life, not initial term.
- **Term licenses + cloud**: On-prem license is point-in-time; SaaS is over-time. Hybrid deals need SSP allocation.
- **Free trials/discounted first year**: Total contract price spread over total committed term.

## Metrics Bridge (for finance reviews)
- Billings = Revenue + ΔDeferred Revenue.
- ARR ≠ revenue: ARR is contracted run-rate; revenue is recognized.
- A growing gap between billings and revenue signals lengthening prepay terms, not necessarily growth.

## Common Errors
1. Recognizing setup fees on go-live (should be deferred).
2. Straight-lining usage revenue (should follow actual usage).
3. Ignoring material rights from renewal discounts.
4. Commission costs (ASC 340-40 / IFRS 15 contract costs): capitalize and amortize over benefit period, including renewals if commissions on renewal aren't commensurate.
