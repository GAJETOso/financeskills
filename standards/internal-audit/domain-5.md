# GIAS Domain V: Performing Internal Audit Services

## What It Addresses
The technical requirements for planning, performing, and communicating internal audit engagements — both assurance and advisory services.

## Engagement Planning
- **Risk-based scoping**: Audit scope must reflect the risk assessment; prioritise high-risk areas
- **Engagement objectives**: Clear statement of what the audit will assess (risk, control, compliance, performance)
- **Work programme**: Document planned procedures sufficient to achieve objectives

## Performing the Engagement
- Identify and assess controls; test operating effectiveness; use data analytics where appropriate
- Evaluate root cause of deficiencies — not just symptoms
- Criteria for findings: Condition (what is); Criteria (what should be); Cause; Effect; Recommendation

## Communicating Results
- **Audit report**: Objectives; scope; observations; conclusions; recommendations; management's response
- **Significant findings**: Report to board/audit committee directly if management does not act
- **Timeliness**: Reports issued promptly; management action plans tracked

## Follow-Up
- CAE must establish process to monitor and report on management's disposition of audit findings
- Unresolved significant findings escalated to board

## IT and Data Analytics
GIAS 2024 explicitly expects internal auditors to leverage data analytics, continuous monitoring, and automation. Technology risks (cybersecurity, AI governance, cloud) are priority audit areas.
