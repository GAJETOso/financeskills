# GIAS Domain III: Governing the Internal Audit Function

## What It Addresses
Establishes requirements for how the internal audit function is governed — primarily focused on the CAE's relationship with the board, audit committee, and senior management.

## Key Standards
- **Standard 9 (Board's Role)**: Board must approve and oversee the Internal Audit Charter; approve CAE appointment/removal; ensure adequate resources
- **Standard 10 (CAE Accountability)**: CAE functionally reports to the board; must communicate significant audit findings and resource limitations directly
- **Standard 11 (Independence)**: CAE must maintain independence from operational management; disclose actual or perceived independence impairments
- **Standard 12 (Objectivity)**: Individual auditors free from bias; conflicts of interest must be disclosed and managed
- **Standard 13 (Quality)**: Internal quality assurance and improvement program (QAIP); external assessment ≥ every 5 years by qualified independent assessor

## Internal Audit Charter Requirements
- Mandate; purpose; authority; responsibility
- Unrestricted access to records, personnel, and physical properties
- Reporting lines (functional to board; administrative to CEO)
- Scope — financial, operational, compliance, IT, risk management
