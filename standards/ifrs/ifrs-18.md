# IFRS-18: Presentation and Disclosure in Financial Statements

## What It Addresses
Replaces IAS 1. Introduces new requirements on the presentation of the income statement, adds requirements for management performance measures (MPMs), and clarifies aggregation/disaggregation principles.

## Scope
All entities preparing financial statements under IFRS (same as IAS 1).

## Key Requirements
- New income statement structure: five mandatory categories — operating, investing, financing, income tax, discontinued operations
- Operating profit subtotal: mandatory (currently no IFRS requirement to present operating profit)
- Defined categories: investing income (assets not related to main operations); financing (defined debt/equity structure items)
- Management Performance Measures (MPMs): non-IFRS measures prominently communicated to investors must be disclosed in notes with reconciliation to most directly comparable IFRS subtotal, explanation of why it provides useful information, and tax/NCI effects
- Aggregation and disaggregation: enhanced guidance; prohibits labelling items 'other' without adequate explanation

## Key Disclosures
- MPM disclosures: label, definition, reconciliation to IFRS equivalent, why useful
- Operating profit line on face of income statement
- Unusual income/expense items in notes (IASB removed 'extraordinary items' but requires specific disclosure of items with limited predictive value)

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 205-220): US GAAP already requires some income statement subtotals. MPM concept comparable to SEC's non-GAAP measures guidance (but IFRS 18 goes further for financial statements vs. SEC's focus on press releases/investor decks).
