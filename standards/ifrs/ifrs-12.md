# IFRS-12: Disclosure of Interests in Other Entities

## What It Addresses
Requires disclosure of information that enables users to evaluate the nature of, and risks associated with, interests in other entities, and the effects on financial position, performance, and cash flows.

## Scope
All interests in: subsidiaries; joint arrangements (joint operations and joint ventures); associates; unconsolidated structured entities.

## Key Requirements
- Significant judgements and assumptions in determining nature of interests
- Interests in subsidiaries: composition of group; interests of NCI; nature and extent of restrictions; risks from consolidated structured entities
- Interests in JVs and associates: summarised financial information for material interests; aggregate information for immaterial interests
- Interests in unconsolidated structured entities: nature, purpose, activities; maximum exposure to loss; financial support provided

## Key Disclosures
- For material subsidiaries: name, principal place of business, NCI %
- For material associates and JVs: reconciliation of summarised financial info to carrying amount
- Unrecognised share of losses of associates/JVs
- Commitments relating to JVs; contingent liabilities for associates/JVs

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 810, ASC 323): similar disclosure objectives but separate standards. US GAAP has specific VIE disclosure requirements (ASC 810-10-50) which are somewhat comparable to IFRS 12 structured entity disclosures.
