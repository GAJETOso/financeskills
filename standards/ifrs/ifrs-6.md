# IFRS-6: Exploration for and Evaluation of Mineral Resources

## What It Addresses
Specifies the financial reporting for the exploration for and evaluation of mineral resources. An interim standard that permits existing accounting policies to be maintained, pending completion of a comprehensive minerals standard.

## Scope
Expenditures incurred in connection with the exploration for and evaluation of mineral resources (oil, gas, minerals) before technical feasibility and commercial viability of extraction is demonstrable.

## Key Requirements
- Entity determines accounting policy: may capitalise or expense exploration and evaluation (E&E) expenditures
- Must apply chosen policy consistently
- E&E assets recognised at cost; classify as tangible or intangible
- After technical feasibility and commercial viability: reclassify from E&E; test for impairment at reclassification date
- Impairment: test when facts indicate carrying amount may exceed recoverable amount; present impairment loss per IAS 36; but CGUs may be larger than normal (aggregation of licences into areas of interest permitted)

## Key Disclosures
- Accounting policy for E&E expenditures and assets
- Amounts of assets, liabilities, income, and expense arising from exploration and evaluation
- Cash flows from exploration and evaluation activities

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 932): specific rules for oil and gas (successful efforts method vs. full cost method). IFRS 6 is deliberately permissive — entities can keep their existing method on transition to IFRS.
