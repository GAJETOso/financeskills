# IFRS-16: Leases

## What It Addresses
Introduces a single lessee accounting model requiring a lessee to recognise assets and liabilities for all leases (except short-term and low-value leases), eliminating the distinction between operating and finance leases for lessees.

## Scope
All leases (contracts conveying the right to control the use of an identified asset for a period in exchange for consideration). Excludes: short-term leases (≤12 months); low-value asset leases (~US$5k); leases of intangible assets (can apply by analogy); exploration rights (IFRS 6).

## Key Requirements
- Lessee: recognise right-of-use (ROU) asset and lease liability for all leases (except exemptions)
- Lease liability: PV of future lease payments (fixed, in-substance fixed, index/rate-linked variable, optional periods if reasonably certain, residual guarantees, purchase options if reasonably certain)
- ROU asset: initial lease liability + initial direct costs + prepayments + restoration estimate − lease incentives received
- Subsequent: liability — effective interest method; ROU asset — depreciate (SL over shorter of lease term and useful life)
- Reassessment: when significant event or change in circumstances that is within lessee's control affects whether reasonably certain
- Lessor: retains IAS 17-type operating/finance lease classification; finance leases — derecognise asset, recognise receivable; operating leases — keep asset, recognise income straight-line

## Key Disclosures
- ROU assets by class; depreciation charge; additions; carrying amount
- Lease liabilities: maturity analysis (undiscounted); interest expense
- Short-term and low-value lease expense
- Variable lease payments not included in lease liability
- Total cash outflow for leases
- Sale and leaseback transactions

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 842): retains operating vs. finance lease distinction for lessees. Operating leases: single straight-line expense (but ROU asset and liability recognised). Finance leases: like IFRS 16. Both require balance sheet recognition — major convergence. Difference in classification: affects P&L presentation (combined single line vs. depreciation + interest).
