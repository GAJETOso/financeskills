# IFRS-11: Joint Arrangements

## What It Addresses
Establishes principles for financial reporting by parties to a joint arrangement — classified as either a joint operation or a joint venture.

## Scope
Joint arrangements: contractual arrangements whereby two or more parties have joint control.

## Key Requirements
- Joint control: contractually agreed sharing of control, which exists only when decisions about relevant activities require unanimous consent of the parties sharing control
- Joint operation: parties have rights to assets and obligations for liabilities relating to arrangement; recognise assets, liabilities, revenues, expenses on line-by-line basis
- Joint venture: parties have rights to net assets; account using equity method (IAS 28); proportionate consolidation no longer permitted
- Classification depends on legal form, contractual terms, other facts and circumstances (not merely ownership %)

## Key Disclosures
- Name, principal place of business, proportion of ownership/participating share
- Method used to account for joint arrangements
- For joint ventures: summarised financial information for material JVs; unrecognised share of losses
- Commitments and contingent liabilities in relation to joint arrangements

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 323, ASC 808): proportionate consolidation sometimes permitted for unincorporated entities in specific industries (oil and gas, construction). Collaborative arrangements (ASC 808) may have different treatment. Equity method broadly similar.
