# IFRS-1: First-time Adoption of International Financial Reporting Standards

## What It Addresses
Provides guidance on how an entity should prepare and present its first IFRS financial statements and interim reports, ensuring high quality information is the starting point and is transparent and comparable.

## Scope
An entity's first IFRS financial statements — first annual period for which IFRS are used.

## Key Requirements
- Opening IFRS balance sheet at date of transition (beginning of the comparative period): apply all effective IFRS retrospectively
- Mandatory exceptions to retrospective application: derecognition of financial assets/liabilities; hedge accounting; non-controlling interests; classification/measurement of financial assets; impairment of financial assets; embedded derivatives; government loans
- Optional exemptions available (elect to use vs. full retrospective): business combinations before transition; deemed cost for PP&E/investment property/intangibles; cumulative translation differences; compound financial instruments; designation of financial instruments; share-based payment; insurance contracts; leases
- Reconciliations required from previous GAAP to IFRS for equity and total comprehensive income

## Key Disclosures
- Explanation of how transition to IFRS affected reported financial position, performance, and cash flows
- Reconciliation of equity at transition date and at end of latest period under previous GAAP
- Reconciliation of total comprehensive income
- Information about impairment losses recognised or reversed on transition

## Key Differences from US GAAP
Not directly applicable — IFRS 1 is the bridge standard. Entities transitioning from US GAAP to IFRS use IFRS 1 to manage the transition.
