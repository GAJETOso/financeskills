# IFRS-15: Revenue from Contracts with Customers

## What It Addresses
Establishes the principles for reporting information about the nature, amount, timing, and uncertainty of revenue and cash flows arising from a contract with a customer using the 5-step model.

## Scope
All contracts with customers except: leases (IFRS 16); financial instruments (IFRS 9); insurance contracts (IFRS 17); certain non-monetary exchanges between entities in same line of business.

## Key Requirements
- Step 1 — Identify the contract: written/oral/implied; commercial substance; approved by parties; rights and payment terms identifiable; probable collection
- Step 2 — Identify performance obligations: distinct goods/services (customer can benefit on own or with readily available resources AND distinct within context of contract)
- Step 3 — Determine transaction price: fixed/variable (constrain variable to highly probable amount not to reverse); non-cash; significant financing component; consideration payable to customer
- Step 4 — Allocate transaction price: based on relative standalone selling price (SSP); residual approach if SSP highly variable or uncertain
- Step 5 — Recognise revenue: point in time (control transferred) OR over time (criteria: customer simultaneously receives/consumes; creates/enhances asset customer controls; no alternative use + enforceable right to payment for performance to date)
- Contract assets, contract liabilities, receivables: presentation on balance sheet
- Incremental costs of obtaining a contract: capitalise if expected to recover; amortise on consistent basis

## Key Disclosures
- Disaggregation of revenue (type of good/service, geography, market/customer type, contract type, timing)
- Contract balances: opening/closing, revenue recognised from contract liabilities, performance obligations satisfied in prior periods
- Remaining performance obligations: aggregate transaction price allocated to unsatisfied POs
- Significant judgements (timing, transaction price, variable consideration)

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 606): identical 5-step model — fully converged. Minor differences: US GAAP has practical expedient for sales-based royalties; some industry-specific guidance retained under US GAAP (e.g., ASC 976 for real estate); US GAAP allows cost/completion percentage option for certain contracts.
