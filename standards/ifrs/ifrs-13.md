# IFRS-13: Fair Value Measurement

## What It Addresses
Defines fair value, sets out a framework for measuring fair value, and requires disclosures about fair value measurements. A single source of fair value measurement guidance across IFRS.

## Scope
Applies when another IFRS requires or permits fair value measurement or disclosure. Does not apply to: share-based payment (IFRS 2); leases (IFRS 16); net realisable value (IAS 2); value in use (IAS 36).

## Key Requirements
- Fair value: price that would be received to sell an asset or paid to transfer a liability in an orderly transaction between market participants at the measurement date (exit price)
- Principal market (or most advantageous market if no principal market)
- Highest and best use for non-financial assets
- Fair value hierarchy: Level 1 (quoted prices in active markets for identical assets/liabilities); Level 2 (observable inputs other than Level 1); Level 3 (unobservable inputs)
- Valuation techniques: market approach; cost approach; income approach
- Liabilities and own credit risk: measured from market participant perspective; consider credit risk

## Key Disclosures
- Level in fair value hierarchy for each class of asset/liability
- For Level 3: reconciliation of opening to closing balances; gains/losses in P&L and OCI; transfers into/out of Level 3; valuation technique and inputs; sensitivity analysis
- Highest and best use (if different from current use for non-financial assets)

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 820): substantially identical — converged standard. Both define fair value as exit price, use same three-level hierarchy, same valuation approaches.
