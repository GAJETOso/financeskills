# IFRS-3: Business Combinations

## What It Addresses
Establishes principles and requirements for how an acquirer: recognises and measures in its financial statements identifiable assets acquired, liabilities assumed, and non-controlling interest; recognises and measures goodwill; and determines what information to disclose.

## Scope
All business combinations except: common control transactions; formation of joint arrangements (IFRS 11); acquisition of assets; combination of mutual entities.

## Key Requirements
- Acquisition method only (pooling/uniting of interests prohibited)
- Identify the acquirer (entity that obtains control)
- Determine acquisition date (date control obtained)
- Recognise and measure identifiable assets/liabilities at fair value at acquisition date
- Non-controlling interest (NCI): measured at FAIR VALUE or PROPORTIONATE SHARE of net identifiable assets (choice per acquisition)
- Goodwill = consideration transferred + NCI + FV of previously held interest − net identifiable assets at FV
- Contingent consideration: measure at fair value at acquisition date; subsequent changes: equity (no remeasurement) or P&L (liability)
- Acquisition-related costs: expense as incurred (not part of cost)
- Bargain purchase: gain recognised in P&L immediately after reassessment

## Key Disclosures
- Name, description, acquisition date and percentage acquired of acquiree
- Primary reasons for business combination
- Fair values of each class of consideration; contingent consideration arrangements
- Amounts recognised for each class of asset and liability
- Goodwill or gain from bargain purchase
- Revenue and profit/loss of acquiree since acquisition date
- Pro-forma revenue and profit/loss as if acquisition had occurred at beginning of year

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 805): substantially converged. NCI: US GAAP requires full goodwill method (NCI at fair value — same as IFRS full goodwill option). Step acquisitions: both recognise remeasurement gain/loss on previously held equity interest. US GAAP has more guidance on in-process R&D; different treatment of reversals of valuation allowances on acquired deferred tax assets.
