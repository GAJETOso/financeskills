# IFRS-17: Insurance Contracts

## What It Addresses
Establishes principles for the recognition, measurement, presentation, and disclosure of insurance contracts. Replaces IFRS 4. Introduces a current value measurement model for insurance liabilities.

## Scope
Insurance contracts issued; reinsurance contracts held; investment contracts with discretionary participation features (DPF) issued by entities that also issue insurance contracts.

## Key Requirements
- General Measurement Model (GMM): fulfilment cash flows (probability-weighted expected present value of future cash flows; risk adjustment for non-financial risk) + contractual service margin (CSM = unearned profit; released to P&L over coverage period)
- Premium Allocation Approach (PAA): simplified model available when coverage period ≤1 year or reasonable approximation of GMM
- Variable Fee Approach (VFA): for direct participating contracts where insurer provides investment-related services
- Onerous contracts: recognise loss immediately when fulfilment cash flows > carrying amount of liability for remaining coverage
- Insurance revenue: presented separately from finance income/expense; reflects services provided in period
- Insurance finance income/expense: choice of recognising in P&L or disaggregating between P&L and OCI

## Key Disclosures
- Reconciliation of insurance contract asset/liability opening to closing
- CSM movement analysis
- Underwriting performance: insurance revenue, insurance service expenses
- Insurance finance income/expense
- Significant judgements: discount rates, risk adjustment, CSM release pattern

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 944): current GAAP uses lock-in assumptions for traditional long-duration contracts; DAC deferred and amortised. FASB ASU 2018-12 (effective 2023) requires remeasurement of certain liabilities annually and updated DAC amortisation — moving closer to IFRS 17 direction but still significantly different.
