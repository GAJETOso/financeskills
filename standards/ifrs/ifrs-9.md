# IFRS-9: Financial Instruments

## What It Addresses
Replaces IAS 39. Establishes principles for the recognition, measurement, impairment, and hedge accounting of financial assets and financial liabilities.

## Scope
All financial instruments except: interests in subsidiaries/associates/JVs; rights and obligations under employee benefit plans; insurance contracts; financial instruments within scope of IFRS 2.

## Key Requirements
- Classification (financial assets): based on business model (hold to collect / hold to collect and sell / other) and contractual cash flow characteristics (SPPI test)
- AC (amortised cost): hold to collect + SPPI; FVOCI (debt): hold to collect and sell + SPPI; FVTPL: all others or FV option
- Equity investments: FVTPL by default; irrevocable election to FVOCI (no recycling) for non-trading equities
- Financial liabilities: AC for most; FVTPL for trading + FV option; own credit risk changes on FV option liabilities → OCI
- Impairment — Expected Credit Loss (ECL) model: Stage 1 (12-month ECL for no significant credit deterioration); Stage 2 (lifetime ECL for significant credit deterioration); Stage 3 (lifetime ECL for credit-impaired; interest on net carrying amount)
- Hedge accounting: fair value hedge, cash flow hedge, net investment hedge; 80-125% effectiveness test replaced by objectives-based test; risk components and net positions eligible

## Key Disclosures
- Financial assets and liabilities by IFRS 9 category
- ECL: risk management policies; quantitative and qualitative disclosures of credit risk; reconciliation of ECL allowance movements
- Hedge accounting: risk management objectives; hedging instruments and hedged items; hedge ineffectiveness
- Modified financial assets; collateral held and called

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 326 CECL, ASC 815, ASC 825): CECL (Current Expected Credit Loss) is a lifetime ECL model from day 1 — no staging concept; IFRS 9 uses 3-stage model. Classification: US GAAP HTM/AFS/trading for debt; IFRS 9 AC/FVOCI/FVTPL. Hedge accounting: US GAAP has additional bright-line tests.
