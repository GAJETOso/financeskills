# IFRS-10: Consolidated Financial Statements

## What It Addresses
Establishes principles for presenting and preparing consolidated financial statements when an entity controls one or more other entities.

## Scope
All entities that are a parent (have control over another entity), except investment entities meeting specific criteria.

## Key Requirements
- Control = power over investee + exposure to variable returns + ability to use power to affect returns
- Power: existing rights that give current ability to direct relevant activities (activities that significantly affect returns)
- Variable returns: dividends, service fees, economies of scale, synergies, residual interests
- Investment entities exception: measure subsidiaries at FVTPL instead of consolidating (with narrow exceptions)
- Consolidation: combine like items of assets, liabilities, equity, income, expenses, cash flows
- Eliminate: intercompany balances, transactions, income/expenses; unrealised profits
- Uniform accounting policies across group; align reporting dates (max 3-month difference)
- NCI: present in equity, separately from parent's equity; share of P&L attributed to NCI separately

## Key Disclosures
- Significant judgements in determining control (structured entities, potential voting rights, agent/principal)
- Changes in ownership interests without loss of control
- Nature and extent of significant restrictions on ability to access or use assets and settle liabilities
- Nature of risks associated with interests in consolidated structured entities

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 810): similar control model for voting interest entities. Variable Interest Entity (VIE) model for structured entities: primary beneficiary consolidates based on power to direct activities AND obligation to absorb losses/right to receive benefits — comparable to IFRS control model but different mechanics.
