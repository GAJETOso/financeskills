# IFRS-2: Share-based Payment

## What It Addresses
Specifies the financial reporting for transactions in which an entity receives or acquires goods or services in exchange for equity instruments or cash amounts based on the price of the entity's shares.

## Scope
All share-based payment transactions: equity-settled, cash-settled, and those with cash or equity settlement choice.

## Key Requirements
- Equity-settled transactions with employees: measure at grant date fair value of equity instruments; recognise over vesting period as expense and equity
- Market conditions (e.g., share price target): include in grant date fair value; do not adjust for whether achieved
- Non-market performance and service conditions: not reflected in fair value; adjust number of instruments expected to vest
- Cash-settled transactions (SARs etc.): remeasure liability to fair value at each reporting date and at settlement; recognise changes in P&L
- Modification of terms: if modification increases fair value, recognise incremental fair value; never decrease expense below original grant date amount
- Group share-based payments: subsidiary recognises expense; parent records equity or liability depending on arrangement

## Key Disclosures
- Description of each type of share-based arrangement, vesting conditions, and maximum term
- Number and weighted average exercise price of options: outstanding, granted, forfeited, exercised, expired
- Weighted average share price at date of exercise
- Fair value measurement: model used, inputs (volatility, expected life, risk-free rate, expected dividends)
- Total expense recognised from share-based transactions

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 718): substantially converged after FASB/IASB joint project. Key remaining differences: US GAAP allows entity-wide accounting policy to estimate forfeitures vs. IFRS requires estimating forfeitures; US GAAP has specific guidance for nonpublic entities; liability classification criteria differ slightly.
