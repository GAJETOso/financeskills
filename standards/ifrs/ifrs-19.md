# IFRS-19: Subsidiaries without Public Accountability: Disclosures

## What It Addresses
Permits eligible subsidiaries to apply IFRS with reduced disclosures, aligning with the IASB's Subsidiaries SME project. Reduces the disclosure burden for subsidiaries that do not have public accountability and whose parent produces consolidated IFRS financial statements.

## Scope
Subsidiaries that: (a) do not have public accountability (not listed; not hold assets in fiduciary capacity for broad group); AND (b) parent produces consolidated IFRS financial statements available to public.

## Key Requirements
- Full recognition and measurement requirements of IFRS still apply — no changes to how amounts are calculated
- Reduced disclosure requirements: entity omits or simplifies specified disclosures across multiple IFRS standards
- If subsidiary elects IFRS 19, must apply consistently for all applicable reduced disclosures
- Disclosure that IFRS 19 has been applied

## Key Disclosures
- Statement that IFRS 19 (reduced disclosures) has been applied
- Name and country of incorporation of parent producing consolidated IFRS statements

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP: no direct equivalent. Private company alternative (FASB PCC standards) provides some measurement simplifications for private companies — goes further than IFRS 19 which only reduces disclosures, not measurement.
