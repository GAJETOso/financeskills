# IFRS-8: Operating Segments

## What It Addresses
Requires an entity to adopt a management approach to disclose information about its operating segments, products and services, geographical areas, and major customers.

## Scope
Public entities (listed debt or equity) and entities in process of being listed. Other entities may use voluntarily.

## Key Requirements
- Management approach: identify segments based on how CODM (Chief Operating Decision Maker) reviews performance
- Operating segment: component that engages in business activities, whose operating results are regularly reviewed by CODM, and for which discrete financial information is available
- Aggregation criteria: similar economic characteristics; similar nature of products/services, production processes, customers, distribution methods, regulatory environment
- Quantitative thresholds (10% tests): revenue ≥10% total; profit/loss ≥10% greater of combined profitable/loss segments; assets ≥10% total assets
- 75% test: sufficient segments identified to account for at least 75% of total external revenue
- Measure segment profit/loss: as reported to CODM (not necessarily IFRS measures)
- Reconcile: segment revenues/profit/assets/liabilities to financial statements totals

## Key Disclosures
- Factors used to identify reportable segments
- Types of products and services from which each segment derives revenues
- Segment revenue, profit/loss, assets, liabilities (if regularly provided to CODM)
- Entity-wide disclosures: revenue by product/service; revenue and non-current assets by geography; major customer information

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 280): substantially converged (both use management approach). Minor differences in CODM definition and interim segment disclosure requirements.
