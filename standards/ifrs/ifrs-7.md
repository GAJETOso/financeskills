# IFRS-7: Financial Instruments: Disclosures

## What It Addresses
Requires entities to provide disclosures in their financial statements that enable users to evaluate the significance of financial instruments for the entity's financial position and performance, and the nature and extent of risks arising from financial instruments.

## Scope
All entities for all financial instruments except: interests in subsidiaries, associates, JVs; employee benefit plan assets (IAS 26); insurance contracts (IFRS 17).

## Key Requirements
- Significance: carrying amounts by IFRS 9 category; items of income/expense/gains/losses by category; fair values; collateral pledged/held
- Nature and extent of risks: credit risk, liquidity risk, market risk (currency, interest rate, price risk)
- Credit risk: maximum exposure; collateral and credit enhancements; credit quality; concentrations; write-offs; credit-impaired assets
- Liquidity risk: maturity analysis of financial liabilities (contractual undiscounted cash flows)
- Market risk: sensitivity analysis for each type of market risk (e.g., how 100bp rate change affects P&L/equity)
- Transfers of financial assets: nature, carrying amounts, risks retained

## Key Disclosures
- IFRS 9 classification and measurement categories
- Fair value hierarchy levels (Level 1, 2, 3) and transfers between levels
- Valuation techniques and inputs for Level 2 and 3 measurements
- Reconciliation of Level 3 instruments
- Quantitative sensitivity analysis for credit, liquidity, and market risks

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 825, ASC 815): similar in spirit. Fair value hierarchy substantially converged (ASC 820). US GAAP also has FASB ASU 2016-01 requiring equity investments at fair value through net income with specific exceptions.
