# IFRS-5: Non-current Assets Held for Sale and Discontinued Operations

## What It Addresses
Specifies accounting for assets held for sale, and the presentation and disclosure of discontinued operations.

## Scope
Non-current assets (or disposal groups) classified as held for sale; discontinued operations presented in financial statements.

## Key Requirements
- Held for sale classification: available for immediate sale in present condition + sale highly probable (committed plan, active programme, listed at reasonable price, expected within 12 months)
- Measurement: lower of carrying amount and fair value less costs to sell; no depreciation after classification
- Present separately in statement of financial position
- Discontinued operation: a component of an entity disposed of or classified as held for sale, representing a separate major line of business or geographical area
- Discontinued operations: present post-tax profit/loss as a single line on face of income statement; restate comparatives
- If held for sale criteria no longer met: remeasure at lower of: (a) carrying amount before HFS adjusted for depreciation that would have been charged; (b) recoverable amount at date of reclassification

## Key Disclosures
- Description of non-current asset/disposal group; expected manner and timing of disposal
- Gain/loss recognised on remeasurement to FVLCTS
- Segment in which asset/disposal group is presented
- For discontinued operations: revenue, expenses, pre-tax profit/loss; related income tax; gain/loss on disposal and tax; cash flows from operating, investing, financing

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 360-10, ASC 230): similar held-for-sale criteria. Discontinued operation definition narrower under US GAAP historically — now substantially converged to separate major line of business or geography test. Comparative restatement requirement for discontinued operations is similar.
