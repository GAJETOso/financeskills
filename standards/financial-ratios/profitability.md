# Profitability Ratios

Measure an entity's ability to generate earnings relative to revenue, assets, equity, or invested capital.

## Gross Profit Margin
**Formula:** Gross Profit ÷ Revenue × 100  
**Interpretation:** Higher is better; benchmarks heavily industry-specific. Declining margins signal pricing pressure or cost increases.  
**Note:** IFRS 15 / ASC 606 — ensure revenue is net of variable consideration (returns, discounts). Principal vs. agent (gross vs. net) affects both revenue and COGS comparability.

## EBITDA Margin
**Formula:** EBITDA ÷ Revenue × 100  
**Where:** EBITDA = Earnings Before Interest, Tax, Depreciation & Amortisation  
**Interpretation:** Proxy for operating cash generation; removes capital structure and depreciation policy differences.  
**IFRS Note:** Under IFRS 16, operating lease payments are replaced by depreciation (on ROU asset) + interest (on lease liability) → EBITDA is higher than pre-IFRS 16 / US GAAP operating lease treatment. Always clarify if EBITDA is pre- or post-IFRS 16.  
**US GAAP Note:** ASC 842 operating leases still show as operating expense → EBITDA lower. Finance leases treated similarly to IFRS 16.

## EBIT Margin (Operating Margin)
**Formula:** EBIT ÷ Revenue × 100  
**Interpretation:** Operating profitability before financing costs and taxes. Useful for comparing entities with different capital structures.

## Net Profit Margin
**Formula:** Net Income ÷ Revenue × 100  
**Note:** Impacted by tax rate, financing costs, and one-off items. Adjust for non-recurring items for underlying margin analysis.

## Return on Assets (ROA)
**Formula:** Net Income ÷ Average Total Assets × 100  
**Interpretation:** Measures asset efficiency. Higher = better asset utilisation. Asset-heavy industries (utilities, banks) naturally lower.  
**Variant:** EBIT ÷ Average Total Assets (removes tax and capital structure effects for comparability).

## Return on Equity (ROE)
**Formula:** Net Income ÷ Average Shareholders' Equity × 100  
**DuPont Decomposition:** ROE = Net Margin × Asset Turnover × Equity Multiplier  
**Interpretation:** Core shareholder return metric. High ROE via high leverage ≠ same quality as high ROE via margin/efficiency.  
**IFRS Note:** OCI items (e.g., revaluation surplus, remeasurement of DB pension, FVOCI instruments) affect equity; ROE may differ from US GAAP depending on OCI composition.

## Return on Invested Capital (ROIC)
**Formula:** NOPAT ÷ Invested Capital × 100  
**Where:** NOPAT = Net Operating Profit After Tax; Invested Capital = Equity + Interest-bearing Debt − Excess Cash  
**Interpretation:** Best measure of core capital efficiency. ROIC > WACC = value creation.

## Return on Capital Employed (ROCE)
**Formula:** EBIT ÷ Capital Employed × 100  
**Where:** Capital Employed = Total Assets − Current Liabilities  
**Interpretation:** Widely used in Europe and Africa; similar to ROIC but less precise on capital definition.

## Common Judgment Areas
- IFRS 16 / ASC 842 lease treatment creates EBITDA/EBIT non-comparability between periods and entities
- Goodwill amortisation (US GAAP private; not IFRS) affects net income and EBIT
- Revaluation of PPE (IAS 16 revaluation model) raises asset base, depressing ROA/ROCE vs. cost model
- Non-recurring items: litigation provisions (IAS 37/ASC 450), impairments (IAS 36/ASC 350/360) — always disclose adjustments
