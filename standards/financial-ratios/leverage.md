# Leverage and Solvency Ratios

Measure the extent of debt financing and an entity's ability to meet long-term obligations.

## Debt-to-Equity (D/E) Ratio
**Formula:** Total Debt ÷ Total Shareholders' Equity  
**Where Total Debt =** Short-term borrowings + Current portion of long-term debt + Long-term debt + Lease liabilities (IFRS 16)  
**Interpretation:** Higher ratio = more financial leverage and risk. Benchmarks vary: 1.0–2.0 typical for industrials; banks routinely > 10.  
**IFRS Note:** Lease liabilities (IFRS 16) are included in debt; creates non-comparability with pre-2019 periods and US GAAP operating lease entities.

## Debt-to-Assets Ratio
**Formula:** Total Debt ÷ Total Assets  
**Interpretation:** < 0.5 generally considered conservative. Proportion of assets financed by creditors.

## Debt-to-EBITDA
**Formula:** Net Debt ÷ EBITDA  
**Where Net Debt =** Total Debt − Cash & Cash Equivalents  
**Interpretation:** Most widely used credit metric by lenders and rating agencies. < 2.0 = strong; 4.0–5.0 = highly leveraged; > 6.0 = distressed territory for investment grade.  
**Note:** Adjusted EBITDA (excluding one-offs, synergies) frequently used in credit agreements — understand what adjustments lenders permit.

## Net Debt-to-Equity
**Formula:** Net Debt ÷ Shareholders' Equity  
**Interpretation:** Reflects net leverage after cash offset. Negative = net cash position.

## Equity Multiplier
**Formula:** Total Assets ÷ Total Shareholders' Equity  
**Interpretation:** Higher = more leverage. Component of DuPont ROE decomposition.

## Solvency Ratio (Insurance/General)
**Formula:** (Net Income + Depreciation) ÷ Total Liabilities  
**Interpretation:** > 0.2 generally healthy. Measures long-term obligation coverage from earnings.

## Common Judgment Areas
- IFRS 16 lease liabilities significantly increase reported debt for retailers, airlines, hospitality → always specify whether leverage ratios include or exclude IFRS 16 leases
- Hybrid instruments (convertible bonds, preference shares): classify as debt or equity per IAS 32 / ASC 480 — material for D/E
- Off-balance-sheet arrangements: operating leases (US GAAP ASC 842 lessees), SPE/VIE — adjust for comparability
- Contingent liabilities (IAS 37/ASC 450) not on balance sheet but material for credit risk
