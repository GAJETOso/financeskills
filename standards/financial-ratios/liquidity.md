# Liquidity Ratios

Measure an entity's ability to meet short-term obligations. Derived from the balance sheet and cash flow statement.

## Current Ratio
**Formula:** Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities  
**Interpretation:** ≥ 2.0 historically considered healthy; industry-dependent. < 1.0 signals potential liquidity stress.  
**IFRS Note:** Current/non-current classification per IAS 1 (IFRS 18 from 2027). Lease liabilities split current/non-current per IFRS 16.  
**US GAAP Note:** Same classification per ASC 210. Restricted cash excluded per ASC 230 (post-ASU 2016-18).

## Quick Ratio (Acid-Test Ratio)
**Formula:** (Cash + Short-term Investments + Receivables) ÷ Current Liabilities  
**Interpretation:** ≥ 1.0 considered sound. Excludes inventories and prepaid expenses as less liquid.  
**Note:** Verify receivable quality — high DSO may overstate liquidity.

## Cash Ratio
**Formula:** (Cash + Cash Equivalents) ÷ Current Liabilities  
**Interpretation:** Most conservative liquidity measure. Benchmarks vary: 0.2–0.5 common for corporates.  
**IFRS Note:** Cash equivalents defined per IAS 7 — original maturity ≤ 3 months, no significant risk of change in value.

## Operating Cash Flow Ratio
**Formula:** Operating Cash Flow ÷ Current Liabilities  
**Interpretation:** > 1.0 indicates current liabilities can be covered by operating cash generation. Preferred over accrual-based ratios for quality analysis.

## Net Working Capital (NWC)
**Formula:** Current Assets − Current Liabilities  
**Interpretation:** Absolute measure; positive NWC = buffer for operations. Trend more informative than absolute level.

## NWC to Revenue
**Formula:** Net Working Capital ÷ Revenue  
**Interpretation:** Normalises NWC by scale. Rising ratio may indicate deteriorating working capital management.

## Common Judgment Areas
- Classification of lease liabilities (IFRS 16 / ASC 842) in current liabilities can significantly affect current ratio
- IFRS contract liabilities (IFRS 15) vs. US GAAP deferred revenue — different balance sheet line
- Restricted cash treatment: include or exclude depending on accessibility
- Overdrafts: offset against cash under IAS 7.33 (if repayable on demand and form part of cash management); separate under US GAAP
