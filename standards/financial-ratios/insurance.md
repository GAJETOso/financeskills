# Insurance-Specific Ratios

## Underwriting Performance

### Loss Ratio
**Formula:** Net Claims Incurred ÷ Net Earned Premiums × 100  
**Interpretation:** < 60% excellent; 60–70% good; 70–80% marginal; > 80% underwriting loss territory.  
**IFRS 17 Note:** "Claims incurred" = insurance service expenses; "earned premiums" = insurance revenue (not written premium).  
**US GAAP Note:** Short-duration: per ASC 944-40 loss reserve development disclosures.

### Expense Ratio
**Formula:** Underwriting Expenses ÷ Net Written (or Earned) Premiums × 100  
**Components:** Acquisition costs (commissions) + Administrative expenses  
**Interpretation:** < 25% efficient; 30–35% typical; > 40% high.  
**IFRS 17 Note:** DAC replaced by acquisition cash flows within insurance contract liability measurement.

### Combined Ratio
**Formula:** Loss Ratio + Expense Ratio  
**Interpretation:** < 100% = underwriting profit; > 100% = underwriting loss (relying on investment income for overall profit).  
**Benchmark:** Combined ratio < 95% excellent; 100–105% borderline; > 110% concerning.

### Operating Ratio
**Formula:** Combined Ratio − Investment Yield  
**Interpretation:** Overall profitability including investment income. < 100% = profitable overall.

## Balance Sheet Solvency

### Solvency Ratio (NAICOM / Solvency II / RBC)
**EU Solvency II:** SCR Coverage = Available Capital ÷ Solvency Capital Requirement (SCR) × 100 — minimum 100%; target ≥ 150%  
**NAICOM (Nigeria):** Minimum paid-up capital requirements by licence category; risk-based supervision framework in transition  
**RBC (NAIC, US):** Risk-Based Capital ratio = Total Adjusted Capital ÷ Authorised Control Level RBC

### Premium-to-Surplus Ratio
**Formula:** Net Written Premiums ÷ Policyholders' Surplus  
**Interpretation:** < 1.5 conservative; 2.0–3.0 acceptable; > 3.0 potentially under-capitalised for premium volume.

### Reserve Adequacy Ratio
**Formula:** Net Loss Reserves ÷ Net Earned Premiums  
**Interpretation:** Adequacy of reserves relative to earned premium volume. Actuary-reviewed.

## Profitability (Insurance)

### Return on Equity (ROE)
**Formula:** Net Income ÷ Average Equity × 100  
**Interpretation:** 10–15% target for well-performing insurers.

### Investment Yield
**Formula:** Net Investment Income ÷ Average Invested Assets × 100  
**Note:** Critical for life insurers and P&C long-tail lines where investment income offsets underwriting losses.

### Contractual Service Margin (CSM) — IFRS 17 specific
**Not a traditional ratio** but key IFRS 17 metric: CSM represents unearned profit in insurance contracts. Released to insurance revenue as services are provided. Monitored as a percentage of insurance contract liabilities.
