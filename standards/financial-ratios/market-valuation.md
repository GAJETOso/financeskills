# Market and Valuation Ratios

Used by investors to assess market pricing relative to fundamental value.

## Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio
**Formula:** Market Price per Share ÷ Earnings per Share (EPS)  
**Interpretation:** Higher P/E = market expects higher growth or lower risk. Negative P/E meaningless (loss-making entity).  
**Variants:** Trailing P/E (last 12 months); Forward P/E (next 12 months consensus); Normalised P/E (cycle-adjusted, e.g., Shiller CAPE)  
**IFRS Note:** EPS per IAS 33 (basic and diluted). US GAAP: ASC 260.

## Price-to-Book (P/B) Ratio
**Formula:** Market Capitalisation ÷ Total Book Equity (or per share)  
**Interpretation:** P/B < 1.0 = trading below book value (potential value or distress signal). P/B useful for asset-heavy industries and banks.  
**IFRS Note:** Book equity affected by revaluation surplus (IAS 16/40) and OCI items (FVOCI, pension remeasurements). Adjust for comparability.

## EV/EBITDA
**Formula:** Enterprise Value ÷ EBITDA  
**Where EV =** Market Cap + Net Debt + Minority Interest + Preferred Shares  
**Interpretation:** Capital structure-neutral valuation multiple. Most commonly used in M&A. Range highly industry-specific: 6–10x typical for industrials; 15–25x for tech/growth.  
**IFRS Note:** EBITDA higher post-IFRS 16 (lease payments replaced by D&A + interest). Use consistent EV and EBITDA definition.

## EV/EBIT
**Formula:** Enterprise Value ÷ EBIT  
**Interpretation:** Similar to EV/EBITDA but includes D&A — better for comparing capital-intensive businesses.

## EV/Revenue
**Formula:** Enterprise Value ÷ Revenue  
**Use:** When EBITDA is negative (early-stage, loss-making); SaaS; high-growth businesses.

## Price/Earnings-to-Growth (PEG) Ratio
**Formula:** P/E Ratio ÷ Expected Earnings Growth Rate  
**Interpretation:** PEG ≈ 1.0 considered fairly valued; < 1.0 potentially undervalued relative to growth; > 1.0 potentially overvalued.

## Dividend Yield
**Formula:** Dividends per Share ÷ Market Price per Share × 100  
**Interpretation:** Income return to shareholders. High yield may signal value or potential cut risk — check payout ratio.

## Dividend Payout Ratio
**Formula:** Dividends per Share ÷ EPS × 100 (or Total Dividends ÷ Net Income)  
**Interpretation:** Sustainability check: > 100% = paying from reserves or borrowings; < 40% = strong reinvestment mode.

## Price-to-Cash Flow (P/CF)
**Formula:** Market Price per Share ÷ Operating Cash Flow per Share  
**Interpretation:** Less susceptible to accrual manipulation than P/E. Useful when earnings diverge from cash flow.

## Tobin's Q
**Formula:** Market Value of Assets ÷ Replacement Cost of Assets  
**Interpretation:** Q > 1.0 = market values assets above replacement cost (incentive to invest); Q < 1.0 = market values assets below replacement cost.

## Common Judgment Areas
- IFRS OCI items in equity (revaluation, pension remeasurements) affect P/B — strip out or include consistently
- Goodwill: not amortised under IFRS; amortised under US GAAP (private companies); creates EPS non-comparability
- Share-based payments (IFRS 2/ASC 718): diluted EPS includes dilutive options; check grant date FV assumptions
- Non-controlling interests: include in EV calculations for consolidated multiples
