# Banking-Specific Ratios

## Capital Adequacy

### Common Equity Tier 1 (CET1) Ratio
**Formula:** CET1 Capital ÷ Risk-Weighted Assets (RWA) × 100  
**Basel III Minimum:** 4.5%; Regulatory buffer adds Capital Conservation Buffer (2.5%) → effective minimum 7.0% for most banks  
**Interpretation:** Core measure of capital strength. Higher = more resilient to losses.

### Tier 1 Capital Ratio
**Formula:** Tier 1 Capital ÷ RWA × 100  
**Basel III Minimum:** 6.0% (8.5% including conservation buffer)  
**Tier 1 = CET1 + Additional Tier 1 (AT1):** AT1 includes hybrid instruments (contingent convertible bonds / CoCos)

### Total Capital Ratio (CAR)
**Formula:** (Tier 1 + Tier 2 Capital) ÷ RWA × 100  
**Basel III Minimum:** 8.0% (10.5% including conservation buffer)  
**CBN Nigeria:** Minimum CAR = 15% (domestic systemically important banks: 16%)

### Leverage Ratio (Basel III)
**Formula:** Tier 1 Capital ÷ Total Exposure (on + off-balance-sheet) × 100  
**Basel III Minimum:** 3.0% (G-SIBs: 3.5% from 2022)  
**Purpose:** Non-risk-based backstop to prevent excessive leverage regardless of RWA calculation methodology

## Liquidity

### Liquidity Coverage Ratio (LCR)
**Formula:** High-Quality Liquid Assets (HQLA) ÷ Net Cash Outflows (30-day stress scenario) × 100  
**Basel III Minimum:** 100%  
**Interpretation:** Can the bank survive a 30-day liquidity stress? ≥ 100% = self-sufficient for 30 days.

### Net Stable Funding Ratio (NSFR)
**Formula:** Available Stable Funding (ASF) ÷ Required Stable Funding (RSF) × 100  
**Basel III Minimum:** 100%  
**Interpretation:** 1-year structural funding: longer-term liabilities must fund longer-term assets.

## Asset Quality

### Non-Performing Loan (NPL) Ratio
**Formula:** Non-Performing Loans ÷ Gross Loans × 100  
**Interpretation:** Lower = better asset quality. < 5% generally acceptable; > 10% elevated concern. ECB threshold: NPL ratio > 5% triggers enhanced scrutiny.  
**IFRS Note:** NPL broadly aligns with Stage 3 (credit-impaired) under IFRS 9 ECL.

### Loan Loss Coverage Ratio
**Formula:** Loan Loss Provisions ÷ Non-Performing Loans × 100  
**Interpretation:** > 100% = provisions exceed NPLs (over-provisioned / conservative). Minimum 60–70% expected by regulators.

### Cost of Risk
**Formula:** Credit Impairment Charge ÷ Average Gross Loans × 100 (bps)  
**Interpretation:** Annualised credit loss rate. < 50 bps = benign credit environment; > 150 bps = elevated losses.  
**IFRS Note:** Under IFRS 9, includes Stage 1/2/3 ECL movements — more forward-looking than incurred loss models.

## Profitability (Banking)

### Net Interest Margin (NIM)
**Formula:** Net Interest Income ÷ Average Interest-Earning Assets × 100  
**Interpretation:** Core spread profitability. 2–4% typical for commercial banks; higher in emerging markets (Nigeria: 6–10%+ historically).

### Cost-to-Income Ratio (Efficiency Ratio)
**Formula:** Operating Expenses ÷ Operating Income × 100  
**Interpretation:** Lower = more efficient. < 50% excellent; 55–65% acceptable; > 70% inefficient.

### Return on Assets (ROA) — Banking
**Formula:** Net Income ÷ Average Total Assets × 100  
**Interpretation:** 1–2% considered strong for banks (asset base very large vs. equity).

### Return on Equity (ROE) — Banking
**Formula:** Net Income ÷ Average Shareholders' Equity × 100  
**Interpretation:** 10–15% target for many banks. High leverage amplifies ROE.
