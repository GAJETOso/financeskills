# Efficiency and Activity Ratios

Measure how effectively an entity uses its assets and manages working capital.

## Accounts Receivable Turnover
**Formula:** Revenue ÷ Average Accounts Receivable  
**Interpretation:** Higher = faster collections. Industry-dependent: FMCG > 12x; construction < 6x.

## Days Sales Outstanding (DSO)
**Formula:** (Average Accounts Receivable ÷ Revenue) × 365  
**Interpretation:** Lower = faster collections. Rising DSO may signal credit deterioration or revenue quality issues.  
**Note:** Use net receivables (after ECL allowance per IFRS 9/ASC 326). Contract assets (IFRS 15) sometimes added for comparability.

## Inventory Turnover
**Formula:** Cost of Goods Sold ÷ Average Inventory  
**Interpretation:** Higher = faster inventory movement. Low turnover may signal obsolescence risk.  
**IFRS Note:** LIFO not permitted (IAS 2); FIFO or weighted average only.  
**US GAAP Note:** LIFO permitted (ASC 330); creates non-comparability. LIFO reserve disclosure required — add back for IFRS comparison.

## Days Inventory Outstanding (DIO)
**Formula:** (Average Inventory ÷ COGS) × 365  
**Interpretation:** Lower = leaner inventory. Part of Cash Conversion Cycle.

## Accounts Payable Turnover
**Formula:** COGS (or Purchases) ÷ Average Accounts Payable

## Days Payable Outstanding (DPO)
**Formula:** (Average Accounts Payable ÷ COGS) × 365  
**Interpretation:** Higher DPO = longer time to pay suppliers. Balance: too high may signal liquidity stress or supplier relationship risk.

## Cash Conversion Cycle (CCC)
**Formula:** DSO + DIO − DPO  
**Interpretation:** Lower (or negative) = more efficient working capital. Negative CCC (e.g., supermarkets, Amazon) = suppliers finance the business.

## Asset Turnover
**Formula:** Revenue ÷ Average Total Assets  
**Interpretation:** Higher = more revenue per unit of assets. Asset-heavy industries naturally lower. Part of DuPont ROE decomposition.

## Fixed Asset Turnover
**Formula:** Revenue ÷ Average Net PPE  
**Interpretation:** Efficiency of fixed asset base in generating revenue.

## Common Judgment Areas
- Contract assets (IFRS 15 / ASC 606): include in DSO for comparable analysis of customer credit exposure
- IFRS 16 ROU assets inflate total asset base → suppresses asset turnover vs. pre-IFRS 16; exclude or flag
- Revaluation surplus (IAS 16): higher PPE book value suppresses fixed asset turnover vs. cost model
- LIFO vs. FIFO inventory cost differences distort DIO comparisons
