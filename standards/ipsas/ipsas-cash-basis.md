# IPSAS-CASH-BASIS: Financial Reporting Under the Cash Basis of Accounting

## What It Addresses
Provides a basis of accounting recognised for governments not yet able to implement full accrual basis — the minimum standard for government financial reporting.

## Scope
PSEs that have not yet transitioned to accrual basis IPSAS (many developing country governments).

## Key Requirements
- Cash receipts, cash payments, and cash balances only reported
- No recognition of assets (except cash), liabilities, revenues, or expenses on accrual basis
- Statement of cash receipts and payments: by category (recurrent, capital, financing)
- Encouraged additional disclosures: external debt; guarantees; property; budget comparison

## Key Disclosures
- Cash receipts and payments by category and entity
- Significant items of disbursements from donors and other third parties
- Departure from cash basis with reason

## Relationship to Private Sector Standard
This is a transitional/minimum standard. Private sector equivalent would be pure cash basis which is not acceptable under IFRS. Acts as stepping stone to full accrual IPSAS.
