# IPSAS-26: Impairment of Cash-Generating Assets

## What It Addresses
Prescribes impairment testing for cash-generating assets (assets operated commercially by government) using discounted cash flow approaches.

## Scope
PSEs holding cash-generating assets (government commercial enterprises, state-owned enterprises operating commercially).

## Key Requirements
- Cash-generating assets: assets held primarily to generate cash flows
- Recoverable amount: higher of FV less costs of disposal and value in use (PV of future cash flows)
- VIU: pre-tax discount rate, terminal value, cash flow projections
- CGU identification: smallest identifiable group with independent cash inflows
- Impairment loss: reduce to recoverable amount; reversal permitted

## Key Disclosures
- Impairment losses by CGU class
- Events and circumstances; recoverable amount basis
- For material CGUs: key assumptions, discount rate, sensitivity

## Relationship to Private Sector Standard
IPSAS 26 mirrors IAS 36 for cash-generating assets. Together with IPSAS 21 (non-cash-generating) provides full IPSAS impairment framework.
