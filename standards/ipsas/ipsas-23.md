# IPSAS-23: Revenue from Non-Exchange Transactions (Taxes and Transfers)

## What It Addresses
Prescribes requirements for the recognition and measurement of revenue from non-exchange transactions — the primary revenue source for most governments (taxes, fines, grants, donations).

## Scope
All PSEs receiving revenue without providing approximately equal value in exchange.

## Key Requirements
- Non-exchange revenue recognised when: control established AND no conditions attached (or conditions satisfied)
- Taxes: recognise when tax event occurs and revenue can be reliably measured; estimated on statistical basis where individual transactions not tracked
- Transfers (grants from other governments, donors): recognise when conditions are met; conditions vs. restrictions distinction
- Conditions: specific actions required before transfer recognised as revenue
- Restrictions: specify how asset must be used after transfer — do not delay recognition
- Services in-kind: recognised at FV when entity controls and can use asset

## Key Disclosures
- Revenue by category: tax, grant, donation, other non-exchange
- Conditions attached to unrecognised transfers
- Services in-kind received and recognised

## Relationship to Private Sector Standard
No private sector equivalent. IFRS 15 covers exchange transactions; IFRS 9 covers grants under IAS 20 analogy. This standard specifically addresses government tax revenue and inter-government transfers.
