# IPSAS-32: Service Concession Arrangements: Grantor

## What It Addresses
Prescribes accounting for service concession arrangements from the grantor's perspective (i.e., the government entity granting a concession to a private operator).

## Scope
Government entities (grantors) granting concessions for public infrastructure (toll roads, airports, water utilities).

## Key Requirements
- Grantor recognises service concession asset when grantor controls what services operator must provide and to whom AND grantor retains significant residual interest at end of arrangement
- Asset initially measured at fair value OR at cumulative payments made if FV not determinable
- Corresponding liability: financial liability (if grantor must pay operator) or deferred revenue/performance obligation
- Non-current assets: depreciate over shorter of concession period and useful life

## Key Disclosures
- Description of concession arrangement; material terms and conditions
- Significant assumptions in accounting for concession
- Carrying amount of service concession assets by class

## Relationship to Private Sector Standard
No direct private sector equivalent — private sector operator uses IFRIC 12 (intangible or financial asset model). IPSAS 32 addresses the government side.
