# IPSAS-42: Social Benefits

## What It Addresses
Prescribes accounting for social benefits — payments to eligible individuals meeting demographic or socioeconomic criteria (pensions, welfare, healthcare subsidies).

## Scope
PSEs that operate social benefit schemes (national pension systems, welfare programmes, unemployment benefits, free healthcare).

## Key Requirements
- Social benefits recognised when: eligibility criteria met AND benefit transaction occurs
- Social benefit expense: amount of benefits paid/payable in the period
- No recognition of long-term liability for future social benefit payments (controversial — obligation approach NOT required but optionally permitted)
- Obligating event: when individual satisfies eligibility criteria for the period
- Disclosure-based approach for long-term social benefit projections

## Key Disclosures
- Description of social benefit scheme
- Expense for current period
- Future obligations: projected payments for 5-year period and beyond (encouraged)

## Relationship to Private Sector Standard
No private sector equivalent. Addresses the most contentious measurement issue in government accounting: should future pension entitlements be recognised as liabilities?
