# IPSAS-39: Employee Benefits

## What It Addresses
Prescribes accounting for employee benefits in public sector including pension obligations, accumulated sick leave, and long-service leave.

## Scope
All PSEs with employees.

## Key Requirements
- Short-term benefits: accrue when service rendered
- Defined contribution plans: expense = contribution
- Defined benefit plans: projected unit credit method; net DB liability/asset on balance sheet
- Actuarial gains/losses: recognised in OCI immediately (not recycled)
- Other long-term benefits (long-service leave, deferred pay): full actuarial measurement
- Termination benefits: recognise at earliest of: offer accepted or restructuring costs recognised

## Key Disclosures
- Funded status of DB plans
- Components of expense: service cost, net interest
- Actuarial assumptions: discount rate, salary growth, mortality
- Sensitivity analysis for key assumptions

## Relationship to Private Sector Standard
Adapted from IAS 19. Significant public sector context: most government employees are in defined benefit plans; unfunded pension obligations are often material to sovereign balance sheets.
