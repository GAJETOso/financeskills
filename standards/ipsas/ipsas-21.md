# IPSAS-21: Impairment of Non-Cash-Generating Assets

## What It Addresses
Prescribes procedures for PSEs to identify whether non-cash-generating assets (assets held for service delivery, not cash generation) are impaired and how to recognise impairment.

## Scope
PSEs holding non-cash-generating assets (e.g., public infrastructure, schools, hospitals, military equipment).

## Key Requirements
- Non-cash-generating assets: primary objective is service delivery not cash generation
- Impairment indicators: significant changes in expected service utility; physical damage; plans to discontinue service
- Recoverable service amount: higher of fair value less costs to sell AND value in use (service potential approach)
- Value in use for NCG assets: depreciated replacement cost, restoration cost, or service units method
- Impairment loss: reduce to recoverable service amount; P&L

## Key Disclosures
- Impairment losses by class
- Events and circumstances leading to impairment
- Service potential of impaired assets

## Relationship to Private Sector Standard
IAS 36 adapted for non-cash-generating assets. Key difference: 'value in use' uses service potential (replacement cost) rather than PV of future cash flows.
