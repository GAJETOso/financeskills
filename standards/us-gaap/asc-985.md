# ASC-985: Software

## What It Addresses
Accounting for costs incurred to develop software for sale, lease, or external deployment; capitalisation thresholds and amortisation.

## Key Requirements
- Software to be sold, leased, or marketed (ASC 985-20): expense costs until technological feasibility established (coding complete and all necessary functions tested); then capitalise until product available for general release; amortise over useful life (greater of ratio of current to total expected revenue OR straight-line)
- Internal-use software: governed by ASC 350-40 (not ASC 985)
- Technological feasibility: completion of detailed program design or working model
- Amortisation: begins when product is available for general release to customers

## Key Disclosures
- Unamortised computer software costs and periods
- Amount charged to expense for maintenance and customer support
- Basis for determining net realisable value for write-downs

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 38: development costs for software (whether for internal use or sale) follow the IAS 38 development phase criteria (technical feasibility as key threshold, but based on six criteria, not 'technological feasibility' concept). No equivalent to ASC 985's revenue-ratio amortisation method under IFRS.
