# ASC-932: Extractive Activities — Oil and Gas

## What It Addresses
Accounting and disclosure for oil and gas producing activities, including successful efforts and full cost methods, reserve disclosures, and asset impairment.

## Key Requirements
- Successful efforts method: only capitalise costs when they lead to proved reserves; exploratory dry holes → expense immediately; development costs → capitalise
- Full cost method: pool all exploration and development costs within a cost centre (country/region); deplete over total proved reserves using unit-of-production
- Proved reserves: SEC definitions apply (1P: proved developed and undeveloped)
- Ceiling test (full cost): carrying amount cannot exceed PV10 of proved reserves using 12-month average price (SEC method) plus lower of cost or FV of properties not included
- Impairment (successful efforts): assess each field; use IAS 36 / ASC 360 approaches

## Key Disclosures
- Costs incurred (acquisition, exploration, development)
- Capitalised costs and accumulated DD&A
- Results of operations for producing activities (supplemental)
- Reserve quantity disclosures (proved developed, undeveloped)
- Standardised measure of discounted future net cash flows (PV10)

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 6: more principles-based; allows continuation of existing accounting policies pre-IFRS adoption. No specific IFRS equivalent to SEC reserve reporting rules. Full cost method not specifically endorsed under IFRS (but permitted if entity adopted before IFRS transition).
