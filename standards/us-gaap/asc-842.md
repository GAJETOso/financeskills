# ASC-842: Leases

## What It Addresses
Accounting by lessees and lessors for lease arrangements; requires balance sheet recognition of ROU assets and lease liabilities for both operating and finance leases.

## Key Requirements
- Lessee: recognise ROU asset and lease liability for ALL leases (except short-term ≤12 months and low-value policy elections)
- Finance lease: front-loaded expense (depreciation + interest); operating lease: single straight-line expense (but asset and liability still recognised)
- Lease classification (lessee): five criteria any one of which results in finance lease (transfer of title; purchase option reasonably certain; lease term ≥75% economic life; PV of payments ≥90% FV; specialised asset)
- Lessor: retains ASC 840-type classification (sales-type, direct financing, operating)
- Practical expedients: portfolio approach; short-term exemption; combining lease/non-lease components

## Key Disclosures
- ROU assets and lease liabilities by type
- Lease cost: finance (depreciation, interest) and operating (single line)
- Maturity analysis of lease liabilities (undiscounted)
- Weighted average remaining lease term and discount rate

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 16: single on-balance sheet model for lessees — ALL leases generate depreciation + interest (no operating lease P&L presentation). ASC 842 retains operating/finance distinction for P&L purposes despite balance sheet convergence. Lessor: both use similar classification.
