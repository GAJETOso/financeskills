# ASC-958: Not-for-Profit Entities

## What It Addresses
Accounting and reporting standards for not-for-profit entities (NPEs), including net asset classification, contributions, and expense reporting.

## Key Requirements
- Net assets: two classes — net assets with donor restrictions; net assets without donor restrictions (replaced old unrestricted/temporarily restricted/permanently restricted)
- Contributions: recognise when received as revenue; conditional contributions recognised only when conditions met
- Expiration of restrictions: reclassify from restricted to unrestricted when time passes or purpose fulfilled
- Functional expense reporting: present expenses by both natural classification (salaries, supplies, etc.) and function (program services, management, fundraising)
- Underwater endowments: disclose; expense from net assets without restrictions (after ASU 2016-14)

## Key Disclosures
- Nature and amounts of donor-imposed restrictions
- Liquidity and availability of financial assets for general expenditure within one year
- Functional expenses in notes or separate statement
- Qualitative information on how entity manages liquidity risk

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS does not have a specific standard for NPEs. NPEs may apply IFRS but with adaptations; IPSAS is often more relevant for government NPEs. IAS 20 addresses government grants; IFRS 15 donor-restricted grants depend on whether donor is a customer.
