# ASC-320: Investments — Debt Securities

## What It Addresses
Accounting for investments in debt securities: classification into held-to-maturity (HTM), available-for-sale (AFS), and trading categories; recognition of unrealised gains/losses.

## Key Requirements
- HTM: intent and ability to hold to maturity; amortised cost; no fair value adjustment
- AFS: not HTM or trading; fair value through OCI; reclassify when sold or impaired
- Trading: fair value through net income; generally financial institutions' portfolios
- Credit losses on AFS and HTM: ASC 326 CECL applies; AFS allowance can be reversed (unlike prior OTTI model)
- Transfers between categories: rare and only in specific circumstances

## Key Disclosures
- Fair value and amortised cost by security type and maturity
- Gross unrealised gains and losses
- Proceeds from sales; realised gains/losses
- Credit quality of HTM securities

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 9 classifies debt instruments as AC, FVOCI, or FVTPL based on business model and SPPI test — less election-based than US GAAP's HTM/AFS/trading categories.
