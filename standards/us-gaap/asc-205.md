# ASC-205: Presentation of Financial Statements

## What It Addresses
Basis of presentation, going concern, liquidation basis, discontinued operations classification, and restatement of prior-period financial statements.

## Key Requirements
- Going concern: evaluate conditions that raise substantial doubt; disclose if substantial doubt exists
- Discontinued operations: component that is a strategic shift or major effect on operations/financial results
- Liquidation basis: adopt when liquidation is imminent; measure assets at expected cash collection and liabilities at expected settlement
- Comparative financial statements: comparative periods required for SEC filers

## Key Disclosures
- Nature and reason for any restatement
- Going concern assessment and plans to alleviate doubt
- Discontinued operations: revenue, expenses, pre-tax income, tax, and cash flows

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 1 is the IFRS equivalent. IFRS requires a 3rd balance sheet when prior periods restated. Going concern disclosure threshold similar but IFRS uses 'material uncertainty.'
