# ASC-410: Asset Retirement and Environmental Obligations

## What It Addresses
Accounting for asset retirement obligations (AROs) and environmental remediation liabilities, including recognition, measurement, and disclosure.

## Key Requirements
- ARO (ASC 410-20): recognise liability at fair value when legal obligation exists; add asset retirement cost to asset; accrete liability using credit-adjusted risk-free rate
- Remeasure ARO when circumstances change significantly (layer accounting for revisions)
- Environmental obligations (ASC 410-30): recognise when probable and estimable (ASC 450 model); measure at best estimate
- Government cost recovery (Superfund): recognise only when contribution is probable

## Key Disclosures
- Description of ARO and associated long-lived assets
- Fair value of assets legally restricted for ARO settlement
- ARO reconciliation: beginning/ending balance, accretion, liabilities incurred/settled, revisions

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 37 governs provisions (including decommissioning). Recognition threshold similar (probable + reliable estimate). IAS 37 uses expected value/most likely outcome; discounts at pre-tax risk-free rate (not credit-adjusted). IFRS 16 impacts lease-related restoration obligations.
