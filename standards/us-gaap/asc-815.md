# ASC-815: Derivatives and Hedging

## What It Addresses
Accounting for derivative instruments and hedging activities, including recognition, measurement, and hedge accounting designations.

## Key Requirements
- All derivatives recognised at fair value on balance sheet
- Changes in fair value: P&L unless in qualifying hedge relationship
- Fair value hedge: derivative at FV; hedged item adjusted for fair value change attributable to hedged risk; both through P&L (partially offsetting)
- Cash flow hedge: effective portion of derivative FV changes → OCI; reclassify to P&L when hedged item affects earnings
- Net investment hedge: effective portion → OCI (CTA); released to P&L on disposal of foreign operation
- Hedge effectiveness: qualitative assessment or quantitative; shortcut method and critical terms match for certain relationships
- Embedded derivatives: bifurcate if host contract not already at fair value and economic characteristics not clearly and closely related

## Key Disclosures
- Objectives and strategies for using derivatives
- Volume of derivative activity
- Fair value amounts by type; location in balance sheet
- Effect of hedging on P&L and OCI; tabular format required

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 9 hedge accounting: similar three types. IFRS 9 replaced 80-125% effectiveness test with objectives-based test. IFRS 9 allows more risk components and net positions to be eligible. No shortcut method under IFRS.
