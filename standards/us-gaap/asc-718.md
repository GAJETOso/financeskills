# ASC-718: Compensation — Stock Compensation

## What It Addresses
Accounting for share-based payment arrangements including stock options, restricted stock, and stock appreciation rights.

## Key Requirements
- Equity-classified awards: measure at grant date fair value; recognise as compensation expense over requisite service period (usually vesting period)
- Liability-classified awards: remeasure to fair value each period through compensation expense
- Forfeitures: entity-wide policy to estimate forfeitures at grant date OR recognise as they occur (policy election — differs from IFRS which requires estimation)
- Performance conditions: probability-weighted; recognise expense only when probable
- Non-employee awards: same as employee awards (ASU 2018-07 convergence)
- Excess tax benefits: recognise in income tax expense (not APIC, post ASU 2016-09)

## Key Disclosures
- Description of plans; vesting terms; exercise price range; maximum contractual term
- Option activity: granted, exercised, forfeited, expired, outstanding
- Fair value assumptions: valuation model, inputs
- Total compensation cost recognised and unrecognised

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 2: requires forfeiture estimation (no policy election). Tax effects: IFRS requires excess/deficiency to OCI if no APIC available (US GAAP always to income tax expense post ASU 2016-09). IFRS 2 has different group share-based payment requirements.
