# ASC-820: Fair Value Measurement

## What It Addresses
Defines fair value; establishes a framework for measuring fair value; requires disclosures about fair value measurements.

## Key Requirements
- Fair value: exit price — received to sell asset or paid to transfer liability in orderly transaction between market participants at measurement date
- Principal market (or most advantageous if no principal market)
- Three-level hierarchy: Level 1 (quoted prices identical instruments, active market); Level 2 (observable inputs other than Level 1); Level 3 (unobservable inputs)
- Highest and best use for non-financial assets
- Liabilities and instruments classified in equity: measured from market participant perspective; consider non-performance risk

## Key Disclosures
- Level in hierarchy for each asset/liability class
- Level 3 rollforward; gains/losses in earnings and OCI; transfers into/out of Level 3
- Valuation techniques and inputs for Level 2 and 3
- Sensitivity analysis for Level 3 measurements

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 13 is the converged equivalent — substantially identical. Both define fair value as exit price; both use same 3-level hierarchy; same valuation approaches.
