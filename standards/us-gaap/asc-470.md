# ASC-470: Debt

## What It Addresses
Accounting for debt obligations including issuance, modification, extinguishment, convertible debt, and debt with equity-linked features.

## Key Requirements
- Debt issuance costs: present as direct deduction from carrying amount of debt (deferred and amortised using effective interest method)
- Debt modifications: distinguish modification from extinguishment (10% test on PV of cash flows)
- Convertible instruments (ASU 2020-06): single liability model for most convertibles (bifurcation of equity component eliminated for most instruments)
- Short-term debt: classify as long-term if refinancing agreed or completed after reporting date but before issuance
- Debt covenant violations: short-term unless waiver obtained before financial statements issued

## Key Disclosures
- Aggregate maturities of long-term debt for each of next 5 years
- Terms of each debt instrument; covenants; collateral
- Convertible debt: terms, conversion features, carrying amounts of liability and equity components

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 32 and IFRS 9: compound instrument bifurcation required for convertibles (liability + equity components separated at inception). US GAAP ASU 2020-06 eliminated bifurcation for most convertibles — moved closer to IFRS but different mechanics.
