# ASC-350: Intangibles — Goodwill and Other

## What It Addresses
Accounting for goodwill and other intangible assets after initial recognition, including impairment testing, amortisation, and internal-use software capitalisation.

## Key Requirements
- Goodwill: not amortised; test for impairment annually or when triggering events occur at reporting unit level
- Goodwill impairment: compare carrying amount of reporting unit (including goodwill) to fair value; impairment = excess of carrying amount over fair value (limited to goodwill carrying amount)
- Private company alternative (ASU 2014-02): may amortise goodwill over 10 years and use triggering event test only
- Indefinite-lived intangibles: not amortised; test for impairment annually
- Internal-use software (ASC 350-40): expense preliminary project stage; capitalise application development stage; expense post-implementation
- Website development costs (ASC 350-50): similar stages approach

## Key Disclosures
- Changes in goodwill carrying amount (including impairment losses)
- Aggregate amortisation expense; estimated future amortisation
- Impairment losses and reason

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 36 and IAS 38: IFRS requires impairment at CGU level (may be smaller than US GAAP reporting unit); IFRS prohibits goodwill amortisation (even for private companies without IFRS 19 framework). US GAAP private company alternative allows amortisation.
