# ASC-830: Foreign Currency Matters

## What It Addresses
Accounting for foreign currency transactions and translation of financial statements of foreign entities into a reporting currency.

## Key Requirements
- Functional currency: determined by primary economic environment indicators (same as IFRS)
- Foreign currency transactions: translate at spot rate; monetary items retranslated at current rate; gains/losses to P&L
- Translation (functional to reporting): assets/liabilities at current rate; revenues/expenses at weighted average; equity at historical rates; translation adjustment → OCI (CTA)
- Highly inflationary economies (cumulative 3-year inflation ≥100%): remeasure as if functional = reporting currency (temporal method)
- Forward contracts designated as hedges of net investment: CTA treatment

## Key Disclosures
- Change in cumulative translation adjustment; amounts transferred to P&L
- Description of highly inflationary economies and effect
- Transaction gains/losses included in net income

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 21: substantially similar translation approach. Key difference: US GAAP uses remeasurement (temporal method) for highly inflationary economies vs. IFRS IAS 29 (restatement using general price index).
