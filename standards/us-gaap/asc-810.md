# ASC-810: Consolidation

## What It Addresses
Criteria for consolidation of subsidiaries; variable interest entity (VIE) model; presentation of non-controlling interests.

## Key Requirements
- Voting interest entity: consolidate when majority of voting interest held
- Variable Interest Entity (VIE): primary beneficiary consolidates — entity that (1) has power to direct activities that most significantly affect VIE's economic performance AND (2) has obligation to absorb losses or receive benefits that could be significant
- VIE consolidation: even without majority voting rights if meets primary beneficiary criteria
- NCI: presented in equity, separate from parent's equity; share of P&L attributed to NCI separately on income statement
- Loss of control: derecognise subsidiary; recognise retained interest at fair value; recognise gain/loss; reclassify from OCI to P&L

## Key Disclosures
- Consolidated VIEs: carrying amounts of assets/liabilities; maximum exposure to loss
- Unconsolidated VIEs: maximum exposure; nature of involvement
- NCI: amounts in equity; income/loss attributable to NCI

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 10 uses single control model (power + variable returns + ability to use power). US GAAP has two separate models: voting interest and VIE. VIE model catches entities that IFRS might control through voting rights. Investment entity exception exists in both.
