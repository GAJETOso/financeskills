# ASC-326: Financial Instruments — Credit Losses (CECL)

## What It Addresses
Current Expected Credit Loss (CECL) model — requires entities to estimate and recognise lifetime expected credit losses on financial assets measured at amortised cost, AFS debt securities, and certain off-balance sheet arrangements at origination/purchase.

## Key Requirements
- CECL model: recognise lifetime ECL at origination/purchase (no staging — contrast with IFRS 9)
- Relevant information: historical loss data + current conditions + reasonable/supportable forecasts of future conditions
- AFS debt securities: allowance approach (can reverse); pool or individual assessment
- Purchased credit-deteriorated (PCD) assets: gross-up approach at acquisition
- Reversion to historical mean: for periods beyond reasonable/supportable forecast horizon

## Key Disclosures
- Allowance for credit loss rollforward by portfolio segment
- Vintage analysis (year of origination) for term loans
- Credit quality indicators by class
- Off-balance sheet credit exposure

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 9 uses 3-stage model: Stage 1 (12-month ECL), Stage 2 and 3 (lifetime ECL) triggered by significant credit deterioration. US GAAP CECL requires lifetime ECL from day 1 — generally results in higher day-1 allowances.
