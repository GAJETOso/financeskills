# ASC-260: Earnings Per Share

## What It Addresses
Computation, presentation, and disclosure of earnings per share (EPS) by public entities.

## Key Requirements
- Basic EPS: net income attributable to common shareholders / weighted average common shares outstanding
- Diluted EPS: add dilutive effect of options (treasury stock method), convertibles (if-converted method after ASU 2020-06), contingently issuable shares
- Anti-dilutive securities: excluded from diluted EPS but disclosed
- Two-class method: required when participating securities exist

## Key Disclosures
- Basic and diluted EPS on face of income statement
- Reconciliation of numerator and denominator
- Antidilutive securities excluded and potential shares
- Effect of contracts that may be settled in cash or shares

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 33 is the IFRS equivalent. Substantially converged. Difference: ASU 2020-06 aligned US GAAP if-converted method for convertibles with IFRS. Two-class method more prevalent in US GAAP.
