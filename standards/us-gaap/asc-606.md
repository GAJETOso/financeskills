# ASC-606: Revenue from Contracts with Customers

## What It Addresses
Establishes principles for reporting revenue that reflect consideration expected in exchange for goods or services transferred to customers. The 5-step model.

## Key Requirements
- Step 1: Identify the contract(s) with a customer
- Step 2: Identify the performance obligations in the contract
- Step 3: Determine the transaction price (including variable consideration, constraint, significant financing, non-cash, payable to customer)
- Step 4: Allocate transaction price to performance obligations (based on SSP)
- Step 5: Recognise revenue when/as performance obligation satisfied (point in time vs. over time)
- Contract costs: capitalise incremental costs of obtaining a contract if expected to recover; amortise on consistent basis

## Key Disclosures
- Disaggregation of revenue
- Contract balances: contract assets, receivables, contract liabilities (deferred revenue)
- Remaining performance obligations (backlog)
- Significant judgements: timing of satisfaction, variable consideration, SSP

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 15 is the identical standard — fully converged. Minor practical differences: US GAAP has some industry-specific guidance; US GAAP allows certain practical expedients that IFRS 15 does not explicitly provide.
