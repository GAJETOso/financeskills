# ASC-310: Receivables

## What It Addresses
Accounting for receivables, including loans, trade receivables, and troubled debt restructurings; impairment and write-off; disclosures about credit risk.

## Key Requirements
- Loans and trade receivables: measured at amortised cost (net of allowance for credit losses under CECL — ASC 326)
- Impaired loans: measure at PV of expected cash flows, observable market price, or fair value of collateral
- Troubled debt restructuring (TDR): recognise as restructuring if creditor grants concession; measure modification; ASU 2022-02 eliminates TDR accounting prospectively
- Non-accrual loans: stop accruing interest when collection of principal or interest is not reasonably assured

## Key Disclosures
- Credit quality indicators for loans
- Allowance for credit losses rollforward
- Past due and non-accrual loans by class
- Collateral-dependent loans

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 9 governs financial instrument classification and ECL impairment. IFRS 9 uses 3-stage ECL model vs. US GAAP CECL (lifetime ECL from Day 1 for all financial assets in scope).
