# ASC-270: Interim Reporting

## What It Addresses
Form and content of interim financial statements; recognition and measurement requirements unique to interim periods.

## Key Requirements
- Discrete period approach: generally account for each interim period as if it were a separate annual period
- Inventory write-downs: temporary LCNRV declines may not be recognised if recovery expected before year end
- Annual costs: allocate proportionately to interim periods (e.g., advertising, property taxes)
- Estimated annual effective tax rate: apply to year-to-date ordinary income

## Key Disclosures
- Sales and net income for current and year-to-date periods
- Basic and diluted EPS
- Seasonal revenue disclosure if applicable
- Material changes from most recent annual report

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 34 is the IFRS equivalent. IFRS uses integral approach more broadly; US GAAP leans more discrete. Both require EAETR for taxes.
