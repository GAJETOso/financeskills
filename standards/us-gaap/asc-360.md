# ASC-360: Property, Plant, and Equipment

## What It Addresses
Accounting for long-lived assets including acquisition, depreciation, impairment, and disposal of property, plant, and equipment.

## Key Requirements
- Capitalise direct costs; expense indirect costs and general overhead
- Depreciation: systematic allocation over useful life; methods include SL, DDB, units of production
- Impairment — two-step: (1) recoverability test: carrying amount > sum of undiscounted expected future cash flows → impaired; (2) measure impairment = carrying amount − fair value
- Once written down, no reversal permitted
- Revaluation model not permitted under US GAAP
- Costs of retirement obligations (AROs): capitalise as part of asset cost; record liability at fair value (ASC 410-20)

## Key Disclosures
- Depreciation expense; accumulated depreciation
- PP&E by major class; useful lives or depreciation rates
- Impairment losses: description, amount, how fair value determined

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 16 allows revaluation model (not available under US GAAP). IFRS uses single-step recoverable amount test (higher of FVLCTD and VIU) — no undiscounted recoverability pre-test. IFRS allows reversal of impairment; US GAAP does not.
