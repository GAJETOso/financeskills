# ASC-480: Distinguishing Liabilities from Equity

## What It Addresses
Determines when financial instruments with characteristics of both debt and equity are classified as liabilities.

## Key Requirements
- Mandatorily redeemable instruments: classify as liabilities
- Obligations to repurchase own shares for cash: classify as liabilities (forward purchases)
- Certain variable settlement instruments: classify as liabilities
- Mezzanine equity: instruments that are not permanent equity (e.g., redeemable preferred stock subject to conditions outside issuer's control) — shown between liabilities and equity
- Convertible preferred stock: typically equity unless meets ASC 480 liability criteria

## Key Disclosures
- Terms of mandatorily redeemable instruments; settlement alternatives
- Mezzanine equity: terms, redemption amounts, redemption period

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 32 requires equity classification only if no contractual obligation to deliver cash. Mezzanine equity concept does not exist under IFRS — instruments are either debt or equity. Puttable instruments: specific IFRS equity classification criteria.
