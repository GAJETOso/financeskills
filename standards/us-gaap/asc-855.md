# ASC-855: Subsequent Events

## What It Addresses
Accounting for and disclosure of events that occur after the balance sheet date but before financial statements are issued.

## Key Requirements
- Recognised subsequent events: provide evidence of conditions that existed at balance sheet date — adjust financial statements
- Non-recognised subsequent events: arise after balance sheet date — disclose in notes if material
- Through date: public companies must evaluate through the date statements are issued; non-public companies through the date statements are available to be issued
- Disclose the date through which subsequent events have been evaluated and whether that date is the issuance date or availability date

## Key Disclosures
- Nature of event
- Estimate of financial effect or statement that estimate cannot be made
- Date through which subsequent events evaluated

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 10: substantially converged. IFRS does not explicitly require disclosure of the date through which subsequent events evaluated (but date of authorisation for issue is required). IFRS: dividends declared after reporting date are non-adjusting; US GAAP same.
