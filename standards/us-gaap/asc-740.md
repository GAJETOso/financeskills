# ASC-740: Income Taxes

## What It Addresses
Accounting for current and deferred income taxes using the asset and liability (balance sheet) method; uncertain tax positions.

## Key Requirements
- Deferred tax: balance sheet method; temporary differences between tax and financial reporting bases of assets/liabilities
- Deferred tax assets (DTAs): reduced by valuation allowance if more likely than not that some/all will not be realised (50%+ threshold)
- Indefinite-lived deferred tax liabilities: generally cannot offset finite-lived DTAs (with some exceptions post ASU 2019-12)
- Uncertain tax positions (UTPs — ASC 740-10): two-step: recognition (more likely than not > 50%) then measurement (largest amount >50% likely to be sustained); interest/penalties policy election
- Tax reform/rate changes: reflect in period of enactment
- Global Minimum Tax (Pillar Two — ASU 2023-09 enhanced disclosures)

## Key Disclosures
- Components of income tax expense (current, deferred, federal, state, foreign)
- ETR reconciliation
- Deferred tax assets and liabilities by type; valuation allowance movement
- Unrecognised tax benefits rollforward; effect if recognised
- Tax years subject to examination; settlements during year

## Key Differences from IFRS
IAS 12 and IFRIC 23: IFRS uses probability-weighted or most likely amount for UTPs (IFRIC 23). IFRS does not have valuation allowance concept — instead uses recognition test (probable future taxable profit). Rate changes enacted vs. substantively enacted differ.
