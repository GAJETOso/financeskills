# ASC-805: Business Combinations

## What It Addresses
Accounting for business combinations using the acquisition method; recognition and measurement of assets acquired, liabilities assumed, goodwill, and non-controlling interests.

## Key Requirements
- Acquisition method only (no pooling of interests)
- Identify acquirer; determine acquisition date
- Recognise identifiable assets/liabilities at fair value
- NCI: measured at fair value (full goodwill method — US GAAP requires this; IFRS allows choice)
- Goodwill: excess of consideration + NCI fair value + previously held equity interest over net identifiable assets at FV
- Acquisition costs: expense as incurred
- Contingent consideration: fair value at acquisition; liability remeasured to fair value through P&L; equity not remeasured

## Key Disclosures
- Description of transaction; acquisition date; percentage acquired
- Fair value of each major class of consideration
- Amounts recognised for each class of acquired assets/liabilities
- Goodwill: amount and qualitative description of factors
- Pro-forma revenue and earnings (SEC filers)

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 3 is substantially identical (jointly developed). NCI: US GAAP requires fair value (full goodwill); IFRS allows proportionate NCI (partial goodwill). US GAAP has more detailed guidance on in-process R&D and deferred tax reversals reducing goodwill.
