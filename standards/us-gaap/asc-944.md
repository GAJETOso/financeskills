# ASC-944: Financial Services — Insurance

## What It Addresses
Accounting and reporting for insurance entities, including recognition and measurement of insurance contract assets and liabilities, deferred acquisition costs, and premium revenue.

## Key Requirements
- Short-duration contracts: unearned premium reserve; loss reserve at best estimate (undiscounted for most)
- Long-duration contracts: benefit reserves; deferred policy acquisition costs (DPAC) amortised over expected policy term
- ASU 2018-12 (effective 2023): update cash flow assumptions at least annually for certain long-duration contracts; DAC amortised on straight-line basis over expected term; market risk benefit at fair value
- Universal life and investment contracts: separate account assets/liabilities; policyholder account balances
- Reinsurance: separate presentation; no netting; recognition of gains on reinsurance

## Key Disclosures
- Disaggregation of insurance revenues and expenses
- Rollforward of liability for future policy benefits; DAC; market risk benefits
- Significant actuarial assumptions; sensitivity to assumptions

## Key Differences from IFRS
IFRS 17 (effective 2023): current value model (CSM, FCF, risk adjustment); revenue = insurance service revenue (not premium); fundamentally different P&L presentation. US GAAP ASC 944 (as amended) moves closer but retains premium revenue presentation and separate DPAC concept.
