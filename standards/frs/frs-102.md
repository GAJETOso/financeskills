# FRS-102: The Financial Reporting Standard Applicable in the UK and Republic of Ireland

## What It Addresses
The main UK GAAP standard for most entities not applying IFRS. A principles-based standard broadly aligned with IFRS for SMEs but adapted for UK/Irish legal framework.

## Scope
Entities applying UK GAAP: most medium and large non-listed companies, charities, pension schemes (with SORPs), housing associations.

## Key Requirements
- Comprehensive standard covering: presentation of financial statements; income statement; balance sheet; cash flow; accounting policies
- Financial instruments: basic financial instruments at amortised cost; other financial instruments at FV
- Fixed assets: cost model or revaluation model (PP&E and investment properties)
- Revenue: recognition based on delivery of goods/rendering of services; aligns broadly with IFRS 15 principles (2025 update)
- Leases: operating/finance distinction (FRS 102 not yet aligned with IFRS 16 — 2025 update introduces on-balance-sheet model)
- Investment property: FV model preferred; changes through P&L
- Business combinations: purchase method; goodwill amortised over useful life (max 10 years if uncertain)
- Pension: full actuarial valuation for defined benefit schemes; multi-employer scheme rules

## Key Disclosures
- Primary statements conforming to FRS 102 requirements
- Accounting policies; estimation uncertainties
- Directors' remuneration (per Companies Act)
- Transactions with related parties; post-balance-sheet events
- Financial instruments: FV disclosure for basic instruments; full IFRS 7-style for complex instruments

## Relationship to IFRS
FRS 102 broadly aligns with IFRS for SMEs (2009 version, updated) and sits below full IFRS in complexity. Key UK GAAP vs. IFRS differences: goodwill amortised (not tested only); leases still operating/finance model (until 2025 update effective); investment property FV changes through P&L (both).
