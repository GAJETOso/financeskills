# FRS-103: Insurance Contracts

## What It Addresses
Prescribes accounting for insurance contracts by UK entities applying FRS 102, providing guidance consistent with former UK GAAP insurance practices.

## Scope
UK insurance entities applying FRS 102 (not applying IFRS 17).

## Key Requirements
- Technical provisions: calculated using prudent actuarial methods
- Deferred acquisition costs: recognise and amortise over policy term
- Equalisation reserves: allowed for certain classes (unlike IFRS which prohibits)
- Unearned premium reserves for short-duration contracts
- Long-term business (life insurance): liability adequacy test required

## Key Disclosures
- Nature of insurance contracts; methods used to determine technical provisions
- Sensitivity to changes in key assumptions
- Unexpired risk provision; deferred acquisition costs

## Relationship to IFRS
Bridges gap between FRS 102 and IFRS 17. UK insurers listed on a stock exchange use IFRS 17; UK unlisted insurers use FRS 103. FRS 103 broadly maintains former UK GAAP insurance accounting.
