# FRS-105: The Financial Reporting Standard Applicable to the Micro-entities Regime

## What It Addresses
Provides a simplified accounting framework for micro-entities (very small companies qualifying for the UK micro-entity regime).

## Scope
UK/Irish entities qualifying as micro-entities: turnover ≤ £632k, balance sheet total ≤ £316k, employees ≤ 10 (at least 2 of 3 criteria).

## Key Requirements
- Simplified presentation: no statement of changes in equity; abbreviated balance sheet; abridged P&L
- Historical cost only — no fair value or revaluation permitted
- Simplified financial instrument treatment: basic amortised cost only
- No requirement to comply with accounting standards beyond FRS 105
- No notes except specific statutory ones required
- Deferred tax not required to be presented

## Key Disclosures
- Abbreviated balance sheet and profit and loss account only
- Very limited notes: advance and credits to directors; financial commitments, guarantees and contingencies

## Relationship to IFRS
Micro-entity regime has no IFRS equivalent. Designed for very small companies for which full FRS 102 would be disproportionately burdensome.
