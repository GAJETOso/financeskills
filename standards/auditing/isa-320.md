# ISA 320: Materiality

## What It Addresses
Establishes how auditors determine materiality for planning purposes and apply it to evaluate the effect of identified misstatements.

## Key Requirements
- **Overall materiality**: Determined at planning; based on appropriate benchmark (usually 5% of pre-tax profit; 0.5–1% revenue; 1–2% total assets; 0.5–1% equity — depends on entity and user focus)
- **Performance materiality**: Set below overall materiality to reduce risk that aggregate uncorrected misstatements exceed overall materiality. Typically 50–75% of overall materiality.
- **Specific materiality**: For items where lower misstatement thresholds apply (related party transactions, compliance matters, key ratios, debt covenants)
- **Trivial threshold**: Below which misstatements not accumulated. Typically 3–5% of overall materiality.
- **Revision during audit**: If new information arises, revise materiality and adjust planned procedures accordingly

## Benchmarks (Common Practice)
| Benchmark | % Range | Typical Use |
|-----------|---------|-------------|
| Pre-tax profit | 5–10% | Profitable entities; investor focus |
| Revenue | 0.5–1% | Low-margin entities; revenue focus |
| Total assets | 0.5–1% | Banks; asset-heavy entities |
| Net assets/equity | 1–2% | Holding companies; investment entities |
| Gross profit | 1–2% | Trading entities |
