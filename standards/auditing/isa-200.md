# ISA 200: Overall Objectives

## What It Addresses
Sets the overall objectives of the independent auditor and the nature of an audit, including the concept of reasonable assurance, professional scepticism, and professional judgement. Foundational to all other ISAs.

## Key Requirements
- **Overall objectives**: Obtain reasonable assurance that FS are free from material misstatement (fraud or error); report as required
- **Reasonable assurance**: High but not absolute level of assurance; reduces audit risk to an acceptably low level
- **Audit risk**: Risk of expressing inappropriate opinion = Inherent Risk × Control Risk × Detection Risk
- **Professional scepticism**: Critical assessment of evidence; alert to conditions indicating misstatement; question inconsistencies
- **Professional judgement**: Applied in planning, procedures, evaluation, and conclusions
- **Compliance with ISAs**: Comply with all relevant ISAs; document departure reasons where exceptional circumstances justify

## Ethical Requirements
- Comply with IESBA Code of Ethics (independence, objectivity, competence)
- ISQM 1 / ISQM 2 govern quality management at firm and engagement level
