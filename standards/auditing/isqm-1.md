# ISQM 1: Quality Management for Firms

## What It Addresses
Establishes a risk-based, proactive approach to quality management at the firm level. Supersedes the old ISQC 1 (compliance-based checklist approach) with a systems-of-quality-management (SOQM) framework.

## Scope
All firms performing audits, reviews, other assurance, and related services.

## Key Requirements
- **Eight components of SOQM**: (1) Governance and leadership; (2) Relevant ethical requirements; (3) Acceptance and continuance; (4) Engagement performance; (5) Resources; (6) Information and communication; (7) Monitoring and remediation; (8) Risk assessment process
- **Risk assessment process**: Central to ISQM 1 — identify quality risks; design responses; evaluate effectiveness
- **Annual evaluation**: Firm must evaluate SOQM at least annually; conclude whether system provides reasonable assurance of quality
- **Remediation**: Deficiencies must be remediated; root cause analysis required
- **Technology**: Explicit consideration of technology tools used in audit (AI, data analytics) within resources component

## Key Differences from ISQC 1
- Risk-based vs. prescriptive rules
- Ongoing monitoring vs. periodic inspection
- Firm leadership accountability vs. compliance function responsibility
- Explicit technology and independence considerations
