# ISAE 3000: General Assurance Standard

## What It Addresses
The overarching standard for assurance engagements that are not audits or reviews of historical financial information. Covers limited assurance and reasonable assurance engagements on subject matters such as sustainability information, internal controls, compliance, and non-financial KPIs.

## Scope
All assurance engagements other than audits/reviews of historical FS. Subject matters include: sustainability/ESG disclosures; internal control effectiveness; compliance with laws/regulations; non-financial performance information.

## Key Concepts
- **Reasonable assurance**: Positive form conclusion ("In our opinion, the subject matter is fairly stated…"). Same assurance level as an audit.
- **Limited assurance**: Negative form conclusion ("Nothing has come to our attention that causes us to believe…"). Less work; more cost-effective.
- **Three-party relationship**: Responsible party (preparer); intended users; practitioner
- **Suitable criteria**: The subject matter must be measured against suitable criteria (IFRS S1/S2; GRI; TCFD; specific KPI definitions)
- **Engagement acceptance**: Consider competence; criteria suitability; subject matter measurability

## ESG / Sustainability Context
ISAE 3000 is the primary standard for sustainability assurance. IFRS S1/S2 and GRI increasingly require or encourage third-party assurance; ISAE 3000 (limited or reasonable) is the standard framework. The IAASB has developed ISSA 5000 (International Standard on Sustainability Assurance) as a dedicated standard — expected effective 2026.
