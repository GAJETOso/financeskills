# ISA 330: Responses to Assessed Risks

## What It Addresses
Requires the auditor to design and implement responses to the risks of material misstatement identified under ISA 315. Links risk assessment to audit procedures.

## Key Requirements
- **Overall responses**: To address pervasive risks (e.g., control environment weaknesses): assign more experienced staff; increase unpredictability; perform more substantive procedures
- **Specific responses per assertion**: For each significant account/disclosure, design substantive procedures and (where applicable) tests of controls
- **Tests of controls**: Required when relying on controls to reduce substantive testing; evaluate operating effectiveness over the audit period
- **Substantive procedures**: Must be performed for all significant risk areas regardless of controls. Two types:
  - *Substantive analytical procedures* (ratio analysis, predictive modelling)
  - *Tests of details* (vouching, confirmations, recalculation, observation)
- **Significant risks**: Substantive procedures must include tests of details — analytical alone not sufficient

## Common Judgment Areas
- Extent of controls reliance vs. substantive approach — risk-based decision
- For highly automated processes (ERP), IT controls testing may allow significant reduction in substantive testing
- Completeness vs. existence assertions require different procedure types
