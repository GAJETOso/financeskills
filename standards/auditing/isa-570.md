# ISA 570 (Revised): Going Concern

## What It Addresses
Sets out the auditor's responsibilities relating to management's use of the going concern basis of accounting and events/conditions that may cast doubt on an entity's ability to continue as a going concern.

## Key Requirements
- **Management's assessment**: Evaluate whether management has performed adequate going concern assessment; review the look-forward period (minimum 12 months from FS date under IFRS/IAS 1)
- **Risk indicators**: Financial indicators (net liability position, borrowing facilities expiry, breach of covenants, negative operating cash flow); Operating indicators (loss of major customer, key management departure, supply disruptions); Other indicators (legal proceedings, regulatory action, catastrophic loss)
- **Procedures**: Review management's cash flow forecasts; obtain corroboration; check post-period-end events; review correspondence with lenders; assess sensitivity of assumptions
- **Conclusions**:
  - Going concern appropriate with no material uncertainty → standard opinion
  - Material uncertainty exists, adequately disclosed → unmodified opinion with Emphasis of Matter (GC section in auditor's report)
  - Material uncertainty not adequately disclosed → qualified or adverse opinion
  - Going concern basis inappropriate → adverse opinion

## IFRS / IAS 1 Linkage
IAS 1.25: Management must assess going concern and disclose material uncertainties. IFRS 18 (2027) continues this requirement.
