# ISA 315 (Revised 2019): Risk Assessment

## What It Addresses
Sets out requirements for identifying and assessing risks of material misstatement at the FS and assertion level. The 2019 revision significantly enhanced requirements for IT general controls (ITGC) and spectrum of inherent risk.

## Scope
All audits of financial statements.

## Key Requirements
- **Risk assessment procedures**: Enquiry; analytical procedures; observation and inspection; IT-specific procedures (new)
- **Understanding the entity**: Business model; industry; objectives/strategies; related risks; financial performance; IT environment; internal control (5 COSO components)
- **Inherent risk factors**: Complexity, subjectivity, change, uncertainty, susceptibility to bias/fraud — assessed on a spectrum (not binary)
- **Significant risks**: Require special audit consideration; no reliance on controls alone; must obtain substantive evidence
- **IT general controls**: Explicitly scoped; identify relevant IT systems; assess ITGCs over those systems
- **Control environment**: Evaluate effectiveness; identify entity-level controls; consider whether controls are designed and implemented

## Common Judgment Areas
- Accounting estimates (IFRS 13 FV; IFRS 9 ECL; IFRS 17 CSM) typically high-inherent-risk
- Related party transactions — often significant risk per ISA 550
- IT environment complexity: automated controls vs. manual overrides; data migration risks
