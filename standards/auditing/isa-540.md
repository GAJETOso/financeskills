# ISA 540 (Revised 2019): Auditing Accounting Estimates

## What It Addresses
Significantly revised standard addressing auditor responsibilities for accounting estimates (including fair value estimates). The 2019 revision introduced the concept of the spectrum of inherent risk and enhanced requirements for auditor scepticism on highly uncertain estimates.

## Key Requirements
- **Spectrum of inherent risk**: Estimates assessed on a scale from low to high risk based on: complexity, subjectivity, estimation uncertainty, and susceptibility to management bias
- **Risk assessment**: Understand how management made the estimate; identify estimation uncertainty; consider whether methods, significant assumptions, and data are reasonable
- **Responses based on risk**: Three possible approaches:
  - *Test how management made the estimate*
  - *Develop an auditor's point estimate or range*
  - *Assess subsequent events that provide audit evidence*
- **Management bias**: Evaluate whether directional bias exists across estimates; aggregate effect may be material
- **Disclosures**: Verify adequacy of disclosures about estimation uncertainty (key sources of estimation uncertainty per IAS 1/IFRS 18)

## High-Risk Estimates (Typically)
- IFRS 9 ECL (especially Stage 2/3 PD, LGD, macro scenarios)
- IFRS 17 CSM and risk adjustment
- IFRS 13 Level 3 fair values (unobservable inputs)
- IAS 36 VIU calculations (DCF; terminal value; discount rate)
- IAS 37 provisions (probability; expected value; best estimate)
- IAS 19 defined benefit obligation (actuarial assumptions)
