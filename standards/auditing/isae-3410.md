# ISAE 3410: GHG Assurance

## What It Addresses
Applies ISAE 3000 principles specifically to engagements providing assurance on greenhouse gas (GHG) statements — Scope 1, Scope 2, and Scope 3 emissions.

## Scope
Assurance on GHG statements prepared against the GHG Protocol, ISO 14064, or other defined criteria. Applies to standalone GHG reports and emissions disclosures within sustainability reports.

## Key Requirements
- **Criteria**: GHG statement must be prepared against suitable criteria (GHG Protocol Corporate Standard; GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance; ISO 14064-1; IFRS S2 requirements)
- **Materiality**: Determined based on likely user decisions; quantitative and qualitative factors
- **Scope 3 challenges**: Most complex — high estimation uncertainty; data from third parties; limited direct verification. Limited assurance more common for Scope 3.
- **Engagement procedures**: Understand GHG inventory methodology; assess completeness and accuracy of emission factors; test source data; assess consistency with organisational boundary

## Limited vs. Reasonable Assurance for GHG
Most current GHG assurance is limited assurance (negative form conclusion). Trend toward reasonable assurance for Scope 1/2 as data quality improves. IFRS S2 and CSRD are driving demand for higher assurance levels.
