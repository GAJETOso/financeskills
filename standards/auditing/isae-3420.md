# ISAE 3420: Pro Forma Assurance

## What It Addresses
Provides guidance for practitioners reporting on pro forma financial information (e.g., acquisition/merger impacts) included in prospectuses or offer documents. Ensures pro forma has been properly compiled based on stated assumptions.

## Scope
Pro forma financial information in investment circulars, IPO prospectuses, M&A deal documents, and regulatory filings.

## Key Requirements
- The practitioner's report covers whether pro forma has been properly compiled based on the stated basis (not whether the assumptions are reasonable — that is management's responsibility)
- Review: adjustments directly attributable to transaction; appropriate accounting policies applied; arithmetic correctness
- Conclusion: positive or negative form depending on engagement terms

## Common Use Cases
- IPO prospectus (required by many stock exchanges including NGX, LSE, JSE)
- Post-acquisition pro forma in IFRS 3 business combination disclosures
- Refinancing or restructuring circulars
