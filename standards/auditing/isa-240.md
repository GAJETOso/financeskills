# ISA 240: Auditor's Responsibilities Relating to Fraud

## What It Addresses
Defines the auditor's responsibilities to identify and respond to material misstatement due to fraud — fraudulent financial reporting and misappropriation of assets.

## Key Requirements
- **Fraud risk factors**: Incentives/pressures, opportunities, attitudes/rationalisations (fraud triangle)
- **Mandatory fraud discussion**: Engagement team discussion of susceptibility to fraud (ISA 315 linkage)
- **Risk assessment**: Presumed significant risks: revenue recognition and management override of controls
- **Responses**: Unpredictability in audit procedures; test journal entries; evaluate accounting estimates for bias; review significant unusual transactions
- **Management override**: Always a significant risk — test appropriateness of journal entries, review estimates for management bias, evaluate significant transactions outside normal business
- **Communication**: Fraud or suspicion to TCWG and appropriate authority where required by law

## Common Judgment Areas
- Revenue recognition fraud is the single largest category of financial statement fraud
- Distinguishing error from fraud requires judgement on intent
- Respond to identified or suspected fraud — report to TCWG; consider legal/regulatory reporting obligations
