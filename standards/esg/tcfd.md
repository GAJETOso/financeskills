# TCFD Recommendations

## What They Address
TCFD (established by FSB in 2015; final recommendations 2017) created the foundational four-pillar framework for climate-related financial disclosures. TCFD is now the basis for IFRS S2, CSRD, SEC climate rule, and virtually all major climate disclosure requirements.

## Four Pillars

| Pillar | What to Disclose |
|--------|-----------------|
| **Governance** | Board oversight of climate-related risks and opportunities; management's role |
| **Strategy** | Climate risks and opportunities over short, medium, and long term; impact on strategy and financial planning; scenario analysis (including 2°C/1.5°C aligned scenario) |
| **Risk Management** | Process for identifying, assessing, and managing climate-related risks; integration with overall risk management |
| **Metrics and Targets** | GHG emissions (Scope 1, 2, 3); climate-related targets; performance against targets |

## Scenario Analysis
TCFD requires scenario analysis to assess resilience of strategy under different climate scenarios including a 2°C or lower pathway. Common scenarios used:
- IEA NZE (Net Zero by 2050)
- IEA SDS (Sustainable Development Scenario)
- NGFS scenarios (central bank network)
- RCP 2.6 / 4.5 / 8.5 (physical risk scenarios)

## Physical vs. Transition Risk
- **Physical risk**: Acute (hurricanes, floods, wildfires); Chronic (sea level rise, temperature increase)
- **Transition risk**: Policy (carbon pricing, regulations); Technology (low-carbon transition); Market (demand shifts); Reputational

## TCFD → IFRS S2
IFRS S2 (effective 2024) is explicitly TCFD-aligned and supersedes TCFD for ISSB-adopting jurisdictions. TCFD disbanded in 2023 as its recommendations were mainstreamed.

## Nigeria / Africa Relevance
- CBN Climate Risk Guidelines for Financial Institutions (2022): TCFD-aligned
- SEC Nigeria Sustainability Framework: TCFD-based
- NNPC climate risk disclosures: TCFD-aligned; Scope 3 Category 11 (use of sold products) material
