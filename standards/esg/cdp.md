# CDP Disclosure System

## What It Addresses
CDP operates the world's largest environmental disclosure platform. Companies, cities, and governments respond to annual CDP questionnaires on climate change, water security, and forests. CDP scoring (A–D–F) is widely used by investors, procurement teams, and regulators.

## Three Questionnaires
1. **Climate Change**: Governance; risks/opportunities; business strategy; emissions accounting (Scope 1/2/3); energy; targets; carbon pricing; engagement
2. **Water Security**: Governance; risks; accounting; targets; watershed engagement; business model resilience
3. **Forests**: Commodity-specific (timber, palm oil, cattle, soy, rubber); supply chain deforestation risk; targets; verification

## CDP Scoring
A (Leadership) → A- → B (Management) → C (Awareness) → D (Disclosure) → F (Failure to disclose)  
Scoring based on disclosure quality, awareness, management, and leadership on each topic.

## CDP ↔ IFRS S2 / GRI
- CDP questionnaire is aligned with TCFD; IFRS S2 companies can largely respond to CDP using IFRS S2 disclosures
- CDP forests questionnaire aligns with TNFD (nature/biodiversity)
- GRI 305 (emissions) and GRI 303 (water) data feeds directly into CDP responses

## Oil and Gas Sector (Nigeria Relevance)
- Scope 3 Category 11 (use of sold products) is typically 85–95% of an O&G company's total emissions
- CDP requests Scope 3 completeness and verification status
- Flaring and methane: CDP climate questionnaire includes dedicated flaring and methane sections
