# TNFD Framework

## What It Addresses
The TNFD provides a risk management and disclosure framework for organisations to report and act on nature-related risks and opportunities — biodiversity loss, ecosystem degradation, land/water/ocean use. Positioned as the "TCFD for nature."

## Four Pillars (TCFD-aligned)
1. **Governance**: Board oversight of nature-related dependencies, impacts, risks, and opportunities (DIROs)
2. **Strategy**: Nature-related DIROs; impact on strategy and financial planning; scenario analysis
3. **Risk and Impact Management**: Process for identifying, assessing, prioritising, and managing nature-related risks/impacts
4. **Metrics and Targets**: Nature-related metrics; targets; performance

## LEAP Approach (Core Methodology)
Locate → Evaluate → Assess → Prepare
- **Locate**: Where does the organisation interface with nature? (Priority locations by biome/sector)
- **Evaluate**: What are the dependencies and impacts on nature?
- **Assess**: What are the nature-related risks and opportunities arising from those dependencies/impacts?
- **Prepare**: Strategy and disclosure response

## Key Metrics Categories
- Land use and conversion
- Water consumption
- Species threatened
- Ecosystem degradation
- Pollution (to soil, water, air)

## Sectoral Relevance for Nigeria
- **Oil and Gas**: Flaring, spills, land disturbance in Niger Delta — high biodiversity impact; NNPC nature risk
- **Agriculture / Agri-commodities**: Deforestation risk (cocoa, palm oil supply chains)
- **Mining**: Land disturbance; water use; artisanal and small-scale mining (ASM) impacts

## TNFD ↔ Other Frameworks
- CSRD ESRS E4 (Biodiversity) is aligned with TNFD
- CDP Forests questionnaire overlaps with TNFD
- GRI 304 (Biodiversity) maps to TNFD disclosures
