# EU CSRD and European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS)

## What It Addresses
The CSRD replaces the Non-Financial Reporting Directive (NFRD) and dramatically expands mandatory sustainability reporting for EU companies and non-EU companies with significant EU activity. Reporting is done under the European Sustainability Reporting Standards (ESRS), issued by EFRAG.

## Scope
- **FY2024 (reporting 2025)**: Large PIEs already under NFRD (~11,700 companies)
- **FY2025 (reporting 2026)**: All large EU companies (balance sheet > €20m; revenue > €40m; > 250 employees)
- **FY2026 (reporting 2027)**: Listed SMEs (opt-out available until 2028)
- **Non-EU companies**: If EU revenue > €150m and ≥ 1 EU subsidiary or branch — sector-specific ESRS from 2028

## Double Materiality
CSRD mandates **double materiality assessment** — disclose on:
1. **Financial materiality**: How sustainability issues affect the company's financial performance (like IFRS S1/S2)
2. **Impact materiality**: How the company's activities impact people and the environment (like GRI)

## ESRS Structure

### Cross-Cutting Standards
- ESRS 1: General Requirements (materiality; value chain; time horizons)
- ESRS 2: General Disclosures (governance; strategy; impact/risk/opportunity management; metrics)

### Environmental
- ESRS E1: Climate Change (aligned with TCFD/IFRS S2; Scope 1/2/3; transition plan)
- ESRS E2: Pollution
- ESRS E3: Water and Marine Resources
- ESRS E4: Biodiversity and Ecosystems (TNFD-aligned)
- ESRS E5: Resource Use and Circular Economy

### Social
- ESRS S1: Own Workforce
- ESRS S2: Workers in Value Chain
- ESRS S3: Affected Communities
- ESRS S4: Consumers and End-Users

### Governance
- ESRS G1: Business Conduct (anti-corruption; lobbying; tax transparency)

## Assurance Requirement
CSRD mandates **limited assurance** from a registered auditor or assurance provider (reasonable assurance targeted for future years). This is the most significant mandatory sustainability assurance requirement globally.

## Relevance for Non-EU Companies (incl. Nigeria)
- Nigerian subsidiaries of EU companies subject to CSRD consolidation requirements
- Nigerian exporters to EU may face CSRD-driven supply chain requests
- NNPC and major Nigerian exporters: EU customers requiring ESRS-aligned value chain data
- EU Carbon Border Adjustment Mechanism (CBAM) interacts with CSRD on emissions data
