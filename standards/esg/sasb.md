# SASB Standards

## What They Address
Industry-specific sustainability accounting standards focused on financial materiality — the sustainability topics most likely to affect financial condition and operating performance for a given industry. 77 industry standards across 11 sectors.

## 11 Sectors / 77 Industries
- Consumer Goods; Extractives and Minerals Processing; Financials; Food and Beverage; Health Care; Infrastructure; Resource Transformation; Services; Technology and Communications; Transportation; Renewable Resources and Alternative Energy

## Structure of Each Standard
1. Disclosure topics (financially material for that industry)
2. Accounting metrics (quantitative and qualitative)
3. Technical protocols (precise definitions and measurement guidance)
4. Activity metrics (normalisation denominators: production volume, employees, revenue)

## Nigeria-Relevant SASB Industries

### Oil and Gas — Exploration and Production
Key SASB topics: GHG emissions (Scope 1/2); methane management; water management; ecological impacts; community relations; reserves valuation; business ethics (FCPA/anti-bribery)

### Commercial Banks
Key SASB topics: Data security; financial inclusion; incorporation of ESG factors in credit; systemic risk management; employee diversity

### Insurance
Key SASB topics: Physical risk exposure in underwriting portfolio; systemic risk management; ESG integration in investments

## SASB ↔ IFRS S2
IFRS S2 uses SASB metrics as the primary industry-based metrics framework for climate disclosures. ISSB has confirmed SASB standards are the preferred source of industry-specific metrics for IFRS S1/S2 implementation.
