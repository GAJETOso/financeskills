# GRI Standards

## What They Address
The most widely used global framework for sustainability reporting — used by ~75% of the world's largest companies. GRI Standards enable organisations to report on their impacts on the economy, environment, and people.

## Structure (2021 Revision)

### Universal Standards
| Standard | Title | Content |
|----------|-------|---------|
| GRI 1 | Foundation | Purpose; using GRI; claims |
| GRI 2 | General Disclosures | Governance; strategy; policies; stakeholder engagement; 40+ disclosures |
| GRI 3 | Material Topics | How to determine material topics; management approach |

### Topic Standards (Sector-specific and universal)
- **Economic**: GRI 201 (Economic Performance); GRI 202 (Market Presence); GRI 203 (Indirect Economic Impacts); GRI 204 (Procurement); GRI 205 (Anti-corruption); GRI 206 (Anti-competitive behaviour); GRI 207 (Tax)
- **Environmental**: GRI 301 (Materials); GRI 302 (Energy); GRI 303 (Water); GRI 304 (Biodiversity); GRI 305 (Emissions — Scope 1/2/3); GRI 306 (Waste); GRI 308 (Supplier Environmental)
- **Social**: GRI 401 (Employment); GRI 402 (Labour Relations); GRI 403 (Occupational H&S); GRI 404 (Training); GRI 405 (Diversity); GRI 406 (Non-discrimination); GRI 408 (Child Labour); GRI 409 (Forced Labour); GRI 411 (Indigenous Rights); GRI 413 (Local Communities); GRI 414 (Supplier Social Assessment); GRI 415 (Public Policy); GRI 416 (Customer Health); GRI 417 (Marketing); GRI 418 (Privacy)

### Sector Standards
GRI has published sector-specific supplements including Oil and Gas (GRI 11), Mining (GRI 14), Agriculture/Fisheries (GRI 13), Financial Services — in development.

## Materiality Assessment (GRI 3)
- **Impact materiality**: Identify actual/potential impacts on economy, environment, and people (double materiality)
- **Prioritise**: Based on severity (scale, scope, irremediability for negative impacts)
- **Management approach**: For each material topic, disclose how it is managed

## GRI vs. IFRS S1/S2
- GRI focuses on **impact materiality** (impacts on world) — "outside-in and inside-out" double materiality
- IFRS S1/S2 focus on **financial materiality** (impacts on entity value) — investor perspective
- EU CSRD requires both (ESRS uses double materiality)

## Nigeria Relevance
- NNPC, GTBank, Dangote, MTN Nigeria all produce GRI-aligned sustainability reports
- SEC Nigeria Sustainability Disclosure Framework (2021) encourages GRI alignment
- NGX (Nigerian Exchange Group) Sustainability Disclosure Guidelines reference GRI
