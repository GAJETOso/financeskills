# SEC Climate Disclosure Rule (2024)

## What It Addresses
Requires SEC-registered companies (domestic and foreign private issuers) to disclose material climate-related risks, governance, strategy, and GHG emissions in annual reports (Form 10-K / 20-F).

## Key Requirements
- **Governance**: Board oversight of climate risks; management's role
- **Strategy**: Material climate-related risks (physical and transition); impact on business, strategy, and financial outlook; scenario analysis (if used in strategy)
- **Risk Management**: Process for identifying, assessing, and managing climate-related risks
- **Metrics**: Scope 1 and 2 GHG emissions (required for large accelerated and accelerated filers); Scope 3 **removed from final rule** due to litigation/industry pressure
- **Financial Statement Disclosures**: Quantitative effects of severe weather events and transition activities on financial statement line items if material (e.g., impairment, capitalized costs, costs expensed)
- **Targets and Transition Plans**: If the company has made climate commitments or has a transition plan — disclose and update progress

## Comparison with IFRS S2

| Feature | SEC Rule | IFRS S2 |
|---------|----------|---------|
| Scope 1/2 emissions | Required (large filers) | Required |
| Scope 3 emissions | Removed from final rule | Required (if material) |
| Scenario analysis | If used | Encouraged; required for financial sector |
| Physical risk financials | Required (material) | Required (material) |
| Assurance | Limited → Reasonable (phased) | Not mandated (national) |
| Materiality | Financial materiality only | Financial materiality only |

## Status (2025)
The SEC climate rule faces legal challenges (8th Circuit). Implementation timeline uncertain. Major US companies continuing voluntary TCFD/IFRS S2-aligned disclosures regardless.

## Nigeria / Africa Relevance
- Nigerian companies listed on US exchanges (OTC: many; NYSE/Nasdaq: limited) subject to SEC rule
- Foreign Private Issuers file on Form 20-F; SEC rule applies
- Major Nigerian banks with US ADR programmes subject to disclosure requirements
