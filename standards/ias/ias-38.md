# IAS-38: Intangible Assets

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting treatment for intangible assets not dealt with specifically in another standard, including criteria for recognition, amortisation, and impairment.

## Scope
All intangible assets except: financial assets; exploration and evaluation assets (IFRS 6); intangible assets arising from insurance contracts (IFRS 17); deferred tax assets; goodwill (IFRS 3); deferred acquisition costs (IFRS 17).

## Key Requirements
- Recognition: identifiable (separable or arising from contractual/legal rights), lack of physical substance, controlled by entity, future economic benefits probable, cost reliably measurable
- Internally generated goodwill: not recognised
- Research phase: expense as incurred (cannot demonstrate technical feasibility)
- Development phase: capitalise when 6 criteria met (technical feasibility, intention to complete, ability to use/sell, probable future benefits, adequate resources, ability to measure expenditure)
- Subsequent measurement: cost model OR revaluation model (only if active market exists — rare in practice)
- Amortisation: finite useful life — amortise systematically; indefinite useful life — do not amortise, test annually for impairment
- Useful life review at each year end

## Key Disclosures
- By class: gross carrying amount, accumulated amortisation, impairment losses
- Reconciliation of carrying amounts
- Useful lives or amortisation rates and methods
- Intangible assets assessed as having indefinite useful life
- Research and development expense recognised in period

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 350, ASC 730): similar treatment for acquired intangibles. R&D expensed as incurred (ASC 730) — no development phase capitalisation for software to be sold (ASC 985-20 allows some capitalisation post-technological feasibility). Internal-use software: ASC 350-40 allows capitalisation of certain development stage costs.
