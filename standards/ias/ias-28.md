# IAS-28: Investments in Associates and Joint Ventures

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting for investments in associates and joint ventures using the equity method.

## Scope
Investments where investor has significant influence (associate) or joint control (joint venture). Excludes venture capital/investment funds that elect IFRS 9 for such investments.

## Key Requirements
- Equity method: investment initially at cost; adjusted for investor's share of post-acquisition profit/loss (P&L) and OCI; reduced by distributions received
- Significant influence: presumed at ≥20% voting power (rebuttable); also board representation, policy participation, material transactions, interchange of managerial personnel, technical information
- Goodwill included in investment carrying amount (not separately recognised)
- Impairment: apply IAS 36; recoverable amount vs. carrying amount
- Upstream and downstream transactions: eliminate unrealised profits to extent of investor's interest
- If share of losses exceeds carrying amount: discontinue at zero unless legal/constructive obligation or payments on behalf of investee

## Key Disclosures
- Fair value of investments in associates/JVs with quoted prices
- Summarised financial information of material associates/JVs
- Nature and extent of significant restrictions on transferring funds
- Unrecognised share of losses (cumulative and current period)

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 323): similar equity method. Differences: US GAAP allows 'basis differences' to be separately tracked and amortised; IFRS includes them in the investment and tests for impairment as a whole. US GAAP has more prescriptive intercompany elimination guidance.
