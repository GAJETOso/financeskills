# IAS-10: Events After the Reporting Period

## What It Addresses
Prescribes when an entity should adjust its financial statements for events occurring after the reporting period, and what disclosures it should make.

## Scope
All entities preparing IFRS financial statements.

## Key Requirements
- Adjusting events: provide evidence of conditions that existed at the reporting date — adjust financial statements
- Non-adjusting events: arise after the reporting date — disclose only (no adjustment)
- Examples of adjusting events: settlement of court case after year end confirming liability at year end; bankruptcy of debtor after year end confirming loss at year end
- Examples of non-adjusting events: major business combination; disposal of major subsidiary; fire destroying major asset after year end
- Dividends declared after reporting period: do NOT recognise as liability; disclose
- Going concern: if conditions arise after reporting date indicating entity is not a going concern, do NOT prepare on going concern basis

## Key Disclosures
- Date financial statements authorised for issue and who gave authorisation
- Material non-adjusting events: nature and estimated financial effect

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 855) is substantially converged. Key difference: US GAAP requires disclosure of the date through which subsequent events have been evaluated.
