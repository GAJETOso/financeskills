# IAS-7: Statement of Cash Flows

## What It Addresses
Requires entities to provide information about historical changes in cash and cash equivalents via a cash flow statement classified as operating, investing, and financing activities.

## Scope
All entities preparing IFRS financial statements.

## Key Requirements
- Cash flows classified into: operating, investing, and financing activities
- Operating activities: two methods permitted — direct (actual cash receipts/payments) or indirect (adjusting profit for non-cash items)
- Investing activities: acquisition/disposal of long-term assets and other investments
- Financing activities: changes in equity and borrowings
- Cash and cash equivalents: cash on hand + demand deposits + short-term highly liquid investments (≤3 months maturity)
- Interest and dividends — choice of classification with consistent application (common: interest paid = financing, interest received/dividends received = investing, dividends paid = financing)
- Foreign currency cash flows translated at rate at date of cash flow

## Key Disclosures
- Components of cash and cash equivalents
- Reconciliation of cash and cash equivalents per cash flow statement to balance sheet
- Significant non-cash transactions (e.g., acquisition of assets by assuming liabilities)
- Restricted cash balances
- Undrawn borrowing facilities

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 230): interest paid and received, dividends received classified as operating; dividends paid may be operating or financing. Direct method encouraged but indirect most used. No option to classify bank overdrafts as cash equivalents where repayable on demand.
