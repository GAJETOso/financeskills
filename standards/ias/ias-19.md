# IAS-19: Employee Benefits

## What It Addresses
Prescribes accounting and disclosure for employee benefits including short-term benefits, post-employment benefits (defined contribution and defined benefit plans), other long-term benefits, and termination benefits.

## Scope
All employee benefits except share-based payment (IFRS 2).

## Key Requirements
- Short-term benefits (wages, salaries, annual leave): recognise as expense when service rendered; accrue unused entitlements
- Defined contribution plans: expense = contribution payable; no actuarial risk
- Defined benefit plans (DB): recognise net defined benefit liability/asset = PV of obligation less fair value of plan assets
- DB obligation: projected unit credit method; discount rate = high-quality corporate bond yield (government bonds where no deep market)
- Remeasurements (actuarial gains/losses + return on plan assets ex expected): recognised immediately in OCI, not recycled
- Service cost and net interest on net DB liability: P&L
- Termination benefits: recognise at earlier of: offer accepted or when restructuring costs recognised (IAS 37)

## Key Disclosures
- DB plans: description of plan, risks, amounts in financial statements
- Sensitivity analysis for each significant actuarial assumption
- Expected contributions to DB plans in next reporting period
- Maturity analysis of defined benefit obligation

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 715): actuarial gains/losses and prior service costs recognised in OCI but amortised to P&L over future periods ('corridor' approach eliminated for IFRS but still used in US GAAP). US GAAP requires separate presentation of funded status on balance sheet.
