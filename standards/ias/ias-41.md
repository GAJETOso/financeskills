# IAS-41: Agriculture

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting treatment and disclosures for agricultural activity — the management of the biological transformation and harvest of biological assets for sale, additional biological assets, or agricultural produce.

## Scope
Biological assets; agricultural produce at the point of harvest; government grants for biological assets at fair value less costs to sell. Excludes: land (IAS 16 or IAS 40); intangible assets (IAS 38).

## Key Requirements
- Biological assets: measured at FAIR VALUE LESS COSTS TO SELL (unless fair value cannot be reliably measured at initial recognition)
- Gains/losses from changes in fair value less costs to sell: recognised in P&L in period in which they arise
- Agricultural produce: measured at fair value less costs to sell AT POINT OF HARVEST; becomes cost for IAS 2 at that point
- Unconditional government grants related to biological assets measured at FVLCTS: recognised in P&L when receivable
- Conditional government grants: recognised when conditions are met

## Key Disclosures
- Aggregate gain/loss from changes in fair value
- Description of each group of biological assets
- Methods and assumptions applied in determining fair value
- Biological assets whose title is restricted; biological assets pledged as security
- Financial risk management strategies related to agricultural activity

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP has no direct equivalent to IAS 41. Agricultural assets generally measured at cost under US GAAP. Fair value through P&L model for living plants and animals generally not applicable.
