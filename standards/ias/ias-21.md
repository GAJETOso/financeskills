# IAS-21: The Effects of Changes in Foreign Exchange Rates

## What It Addresses
Prescribes how to include foreign currency transactions and foreign operations in financial statements, and how to translate financial statements into a presentation currency.

## Scope
Foreign currency transactions; translating results of foreign operations. Excludes hedge accounting of foreign currency items (IFRS 9).

## Key Requirements
- Functional currency: currency of primary economic environment; determined by indicators (sales prices, labour/material costs, financing currencies)
- Foreign currency transactions: initially record at spot rate at date of transaction
- Monetary items (cash, receivables, payables): retranslate at closing rate at each reporting date; exchange differences → P&L
- Non-monetary items at historical cost: translate at historical rate (no retranslation)
- Non-monetary items at fair value: translate at rate when fair value determined
- Foreign operations translation (to presentation currency): assets/liabilities at closing rate; income/expenses at transaction date rates (or average); all resulting differences → OCI (translation reserve); recycled to P&L on disposal

## Key Disclosures
- Amount of exchange differences in P&L
- Net exchange differences in OCI and reconciliation of translation reserve
- Reason if presentation currency differs from functional currency
- Change in functional currency and reason

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 830): substantially converged. US GAAP requires remeasurement of functional currency books to reporting currency using temporal method when books kept in different currency — IAS 21 equivalent. Key difference: US GAAP has specific guidance on highly inflationary economies (remeasure as if functional = reporting currency).
