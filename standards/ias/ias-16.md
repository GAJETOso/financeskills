# IAS-16: Property, Plant and Equipment

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting treatment for property, plant and equipment (PP&E) so users can discern the investment in PP&E and changes therein.

## Scope
All PP&E except: biological assets (IAS 41), mineral rights and reserves (IFRS 6), investment property (IAS 40 — but IAS 16 cost model applies by reference).

## Key Requirements
- Recognition: probable future economic benefits and cost reliably measurable
- Initial measurement: cost (purchase price + directly attributable costs + decommissioning estimate)
- Subsequent measurement: cost model (cost less accumulated depreciation and impairment) OR revaluation model (fair value less subsequent depreciation and impairment)
- Revaluation surplus: OCI (equity); revaluation deficit: P&L (unless reversing prior surplus in equity)
- Depreciation: each significant component depreciated separately over useful life using a systematic method (SL, DDB, units of production)
- Residual value and useful life: reviewed at each year end
- Derecognition: on disposal or when no future economic benefits expected; gain/loss to P&L (not revenue)

## Key Disclosures
- Measurement bases for each class
- Depreciation methods and useful lives per class
- Gross carrying amount and accumulated depreciation at beginning and end
- Reconciliation of carrying amount (additions, disposals, revaluations, depreciation, impairment)
- Restrictions on title; assets pledged as security
- For revaluation model: effective date, independent valuer involvement, carrying amount under cost model

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 360): revaluation model not permitted — cost model only. Componentisation less prescriptive. Impairment testing uses different two-step recoverability test vs. IAS 36 recoverable amount approach.
