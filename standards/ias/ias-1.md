# IAS-1: Presentation of Financial Statements

## What It Addresses
Sets the overall requirements for the presentation of financial statements, guidelines for their structure and minimum requirements for content.

## Scope
Applies to all general-purpose financial statements prepared and presented in accordance with IFRS.

## Key Requirements
- Fair presentation and compliance with IFRS
- Going concern assessment
- Accrual basis of accounting
- Materiality and aggregation
- Offsetting (generally prohibited)
- Frequency of reporting (at least annually)
- Comparative information (minimum one prior period)
- Consistency of presentation

## Key Disclosures
- Going concern uncertainties
- Judgements and estimation uncertainties (especially those with significant risk of material adjustment)
- Capital management objectives and policies
- Proposed or declared dividends

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 205-220) has similar requirements but uses different terminology and presentation formats; no explicit 'other comprehensive income' reclassification requirement under some US GAAP options.
