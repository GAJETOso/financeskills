# IAS-24: Related Party Disclosures

## What It Addresses
Ensures that financial statements contain disclosures necessary to draw attention to the possibility that the financial position and profit/loss may have been affected by related party relationships and transactions.

## Scope
All IFRS financial statements. Government-related entities have partial exemption.

## Key Requirements
- Related party definition: person/entity with control, joint control, significant influence over the reporting entity, or vice versa; KMP of entity or parent; close family member of such persons
- Disclose: nature of related party relationships even if no transactions
- Disclose related party transactions: amounts, outstanding balances, terms, provisions for doubtful debts
- Key management personnel (KMP) compensation: total and by category (short-term, post-employment, other long-term, termination, share-based)
- No offset of related party balances from different parties

## Key Disclosures
- Parent entity and ultimate controlling party (if different)
- KMP compensation by category
- Related party transactions: amount, terms, outstanding balances, guarantees
- Government-related entity: name of government and nature of relationship; significant transactions

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 850): similar disclosure requirements. US GAAP has more specific guidance on common ownership situations. No government-related entity exemption in US GAAP.

## Common Judgment Areas
- Identifying all related parties — particularly complex ownership structures
- Determining whether transactions are on arm's length terms (not required to assert under IFRS, but often stated)
- KMP identification — who has authority and responsibility for planning/directing/controlling
