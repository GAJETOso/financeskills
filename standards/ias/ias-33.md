# IAS-33: Earnings Per Share

## What It Addresses
Prescribes principles for determining and presenting basic and diluted earnings per share to improve performance comparisons between entities.

## Scope
Entities with ordinary shares or potential ordinary shares (options, warrants, convertibles) that are publicly traded or in process of being issued to public.

## Key Requirements
- Basic EPS = (profit attributable to ordinary equity holders) / (weighted average ordinary shares outstanding)
- Diluted EPS: adjust numerator for after-tax effect of dilutive instruments; adjust denominator for weighted average of additional shares assuming conversion/exercise (treasury stock method for options)
- Diluted EPS cannot exceed basic EPS (anti-dilutive instruments excluded)
- Rights issues at below market price: treated as bonus element × market price element
- Retrospective adjustment for capitalisation issues, share splits, reverse splits

## Key Disclosures
- Basic and diluted EPS on face of income statement (even if negative)
- Amounts used as numerators (reconciliation to profit/loss)
- Weighted average shares used as denominator
- Instruments that could dilute EPS in future but excluded from diluted EPS (anti-dilutive)

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 260): substantially converged. Differences in treatment of share-based payment instruments and convertible instruments (FASB's ASU 2020-06 changed if-converted method for convertibles).
