# IAS-8: Accounting Policies, Changes in Accounting Estimates and Errors

## What It Addresses
Prescribes criteria for selecting and changing accounting policies, together with the accounting treatment and disclosure of changes in accounting policies, changes in accounting estimates, and corrections of errors.

## Scope
All entities preparing IFRS financial statements.

## Key Requirements
- Accounting policies: select and apply consistently; change only if required by IFRS or results in more reliable/relevant information
- Change in accounting policy: retrospective application (restate comparatives) unless impracticable
- Change in accounting estimate: prospective application (current and future periods)
- Correction of prior period material errors: retrospective restatement
- Hierarchy for selecting policies when no specific IFRS applies: similar IFRS → Conceptual Framework → other standard setters → accounting literature

## Key Disclosures
- Nature and reason for change in accounting policy
- Effect of change on current and prior periods (line by line)
- For new IFRS not yet adopted: expected impact on financial statements
- Description and amount of correction of prior period error

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 250): similar treatment; however, changes from LIFO to another method require retrospective application. Direct and indirect effects of accounting changes treated differently.
