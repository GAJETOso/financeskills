# IAS-34: Interim Financial Reporting

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the minimum content of an interim financial report and the principles for recognising and measuring amounts in interim financial statements.

## Scope
Entities required or choosing to publish interim financial reports in accordance with IFRS.

## Key Requirements
- Interim report: either complete set of financial statements (IAS 1) or condensed financial statements
- Condensed: condensed balance sheet, condensed income statement, condensed cash flows, condensed changes in equity, selected notes
- Same accounting policies as annual statements (unless policy changed)
- Revenue and expenses: recognised on same basis as annual statements; do not defer/anticipate costs that would not be deferred/anticipated at year end
- Materiality: assessed in relation to interim period data
- Estimates: may require more use of estimation methods than annual financial statements
- Taxes: apply estimated average annual effective tax rate to pre-tax income for the interim period

## Key Disclosures
- Statement that same accounting policies as most recent annual statements
- Seasonal or cyclical nature of operations
- Unusual items and their nature and amount
- Changes in estimates; events after interim period
- Changes in composition of entity (business combinations, disposals)

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 270): substantially similar discrete vs. integral approach debate resolved similarly. US GAAP has more specific guidance on extraordinary items (now abolished), discontinued operations classification at interim dates.

- Estimated annual effective tax rate (EAETR) computation and application
