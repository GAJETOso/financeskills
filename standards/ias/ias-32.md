# IAS-32: Financial Instruments: Presentation

## What It Addresses
Establishes principles for presenting financial instruments as liabilities or equity, and for offsetting financial assets and liabilities.

## Scope
All entities for all types of financial instruments except: interests in subsidiaries/associates/JVs; rights/obligations under IFRS 2, 16, 17; insurance contracts (IFRS 17).

## Key Requirements
- Equity vs. liability: instrument is equity ONLY if no contractual obligation to deliver cash/financial asset AND no obligation to exchange financial instruments under potentially unfavourable conditions
- Compound instruments (e.g., convertible bonds): split into liability and equity components at inception using residual method (liability = PV of future cash flows; equity = residual)
- Treasury shares: deducted from equity; no gain/loss on purchase/sale/cancellation
- Interest, dividends, gains/losses on financial liabilities → P&L; equity instruments → equity (distributions)
- Offsetting: present net only when legally enforceable right to offset AND intention to settle net or simultaneously
- Puttable instruments: classified as equity only if specific criteria met

## Key Disclosures
- Terms and conditions of compound instruments
- Carrying amounts of financial assets and liabilities by category

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 480, ASC 260): more complex rules for mandatorily redeemable instruments; mezzanine equity classification (between debt and equity) exists in US GAAP, not in IFRS. EPS treatment of convertibles differs.
