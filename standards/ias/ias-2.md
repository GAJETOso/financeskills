# IAS-2: Inventories

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting treatment for inventories, including the determination of cost and subsequent recognition as an expense.

## Scope
All inventories except: WIP under construction contracts (IFRS 15), financial instruments (IFRS 9), biological assets (IAS 41), and certain commodity broker-trader inventories.

## Key Requirements
- Inventories measured at lower of cost and net realisable value (NRV)
- Cost includes: purchase costs, conversion costs, and other directly attributable costs
- Cost formulas: FIFO or weighted average cost (LIFO prohibited under IFRS)
- Write-down to NRV when cost exceeds NRV; reversal permitted if NRV subsequently recovers
- When inventories are sold, carrying amount recognised as expense in the period of revenue recognition

## Key Disclosures
- Accounting policies for measuring inventories, including cost formula
- Total carrying amount and carrying amount by classification
- Amount of inventories recognised as expense
- Write-downs and reversals of write-downs
- Inventories pledged as security

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 330) permits LIFO which IFRS prohibits. US GAAP measures at lower of cost and net realisable value (LCNRV) for most inventories; LIFO/retail users compare to market (replacement cost). Reversal of write-downs to NRV is prohibited under US GAAP.
