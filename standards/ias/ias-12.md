# IAS-12: Income Taxes

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting treatment for income taxes, using the balance sheet liability method to recognise deferred tax on temporary differences between the tax base and carrying amount of assets and liabilities.

## Scope
All income taxes (domestic and foreign) including withholding taxes on dividends.

## Key Requirements
- Current tax: amount expected to be paid/recovered for current period, using enacted or substantively enacted tax rates
- Deferred tax liability (DTL): recognised for all taxable temporary differences (with limited exceptions)
- Deferred tax asset (DTA): recognised for deductible temporary differences, unused tax losses and credits to the extent probable that future taxable profit will be available
- Exceptions to deferred tax recognition: initial recognition of goodwill; initial recognition of asset/liability in a transaction that is not a business combination and affects neither accounting nor taxable profit
- Tax rates: use enacted or substantively enacted rate expected to apply when asset is realised or liability is settled
- Offsetting: current tax assets and liabilities offset if legal right to offset and intention to settle net; deferred tax similarly offset by jurisdiction

## Key Disclosures
- Major components of tax expense (current, deferred, prior year adjustments)
- Explanation of relationship between tax expense and accounting profit (ETR reconciliation)
- Deferred tax assets and liabilities by type of temporary difference
- Unrecognised deferred tax assets (losses, deductible differences) and why not recognised
- Tax effects of dividends proposed/declared before authorisation

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 740): similar balance sheet approach but valuation allowance used instead of recognition test. US GAAP requires classification of deferred taxes by jurisdiction. Uncertain tax positions (UTPs) treated under ASC 740-10 two-step recognition/measurement; IFRS uses probability-weighted/most likely amount approach under IFRIC 23.
