# IAS-29: Financial Reporting in Hyperinflationary Economies

## What It Addresses
Prescribes restating financial statements of entities whose functional currency is that of a hyperinflationary economy.

## Scope
Primary financial statements (and any financial information) of entities reporting in the currency of a hyperinflationary economy.

## Key Requirements
- Hyperinflation indicators: general population prefers to keep wealth in non-monetary assets or stable foreign currency; prices quoted in stable foreign currency; credit sales/purchases at prices compensating for expected loss of purchasing power; cumulative inflation rate over 3 years approaching or exceeding 100%
- Restate financial statements: non-monetary items (assets, liabilities, equity, income/expense) restated using a general price index from date of acquisition/incurrence
- Monetary items: not restated (already expressed in current monetary units)
- Gain or loss on net monetary position: in P&L
- Comparative periods: restated in terms of measuring unit at end of current period
- All items in cash flow statement: expressed in measuring unit at end of reporting period

## Key Disclosures
- Fact that restatement has been made
- Price index at reporting date and movement during period
- Gain/loss on net monetary position

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 830): for highly inflationary economies (cumulative 3-year inflation ≥100%), the entity remeasures as if its functional currency were the reporting currency (temporal method) rather than applying general price-level restatement.
