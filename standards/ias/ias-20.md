# IAS-20: Accounting for Government Grants and Disclosure of Government Assistance

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting for, and disclosure of, government grants and other forms of government assistance.

## Scope
Government grants and government assistance. Excludes: government as owner (equity transactions); agricultural grants (IAS 41); deferred tax assets (IAS 12).

## Key Requirements
- Recognition: recognise grants only when there is reasonable assurance that: (a) entity will comply with conditions, and (b) grants will be received
- Matching principle: recognise grant in P&L on a systematic basis over periods in which entity recognises related expenses
- Grants related to assets: presented as deferred income OR deducted from carrying amount of asset
- Grants related to income: recognised in P&L; presented as separate credit or deducted from related expense
- Non-monetary grants (e.g., land): recognised at fair value OR nominal value
- Repayment of grants: account as change in accounting estimate; for asset grants, reduce deferred income or increase carrying amount

## Key Disclosures
- Accounting policy adopted, including presentation method
- Nature and extent of government grants recognised
- Unfulfilled conditions and contingencies attached to recognised grants

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP has no direct equivalent standard for government grants for for-profit entities. ASC 958 covers not-for-profits. For-profits often apply IAS 20 by analogy or ASC 450 (gain contingencies). FASB issued ASU 2021-10 requiring disclosure of government assistance for entities (Topic 832) but not full recognition guidance.
