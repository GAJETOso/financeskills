# IAS-40: Investment Property

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting treatment for investment property (land/buildings held to earn rentals and/or capital appreciation) and related disclosure requirements.

## Scope
Investment property: property held to earn rentals or for capital appreciation, or both. Excludes owner-occupied property (IAS 16) and property held for sale in ordinary course (IAS 2).

## Key Requirements
- Initial measurement: at cost
- Subsequent measurement: CHOICE of fair value model or cost model (apply consistently to all investment property)
- Fair value model: remeasure to fair value at each reporting date; changes recognised in P&L (not OCI); no depreciation
- Cost model: IAS 16 cost model applies; fair value disclosed in notes
- Transfers between investment property, owner-occupied, and inventories: specific rules depending on direction of transfer
- Disposals: difference between net disposal proceeds and carrying amount → P&L

## Key Disclosures
- Whether fair value model or cost model used
- Criteria to distinguish investment property from owner-occupied and inventory
- Methods and assumptions applied in determining fair value
- Amounts recognised in P&L for rental income and direct operating expenses
- Restrictions on disposals; contractual obligations for purchases/development/repairs

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP: no equivalent standard. Investment property is generally accounted for under ASC 360 (PP&E) at cost less depreciation. Fair value model not available for investment property under US GAAP (except investment companies under ASC 946).
