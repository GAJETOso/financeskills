# IAS-37: Provisions, Contingent Liabilities and Contingent Assets

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting for and disclosure of provisions, contingent liabilities, and contingent assets to ensure only genuine obligations are recognised.

## Scope
All provisions, contingent liabilities and contingent assets except those covered by other standards (e.g., financial instruments under IFRS 9, insurance under IFRS 17, construction contracts, income tax). Also covers onerous contracts.

## Key Requirements
- Provision recognised when: present obligation (legal or constructive) as result of past event; probable outflow of resources (>50% likely); reliable estimate can be made
- Measurement: best estimate of expenditure required to settle; for large populations use expected value; for single obligation use most likely outcome
- Time value: provisions discounted where material; unwinding of discount recognised as finance cost
- Onerous contracts: if unavoidable costs of meeting obligations exceed expected benefits, recognise provision for lower of cost of fulfilling vs. penalty for not fulfilling
- Restructuring provision: recognise only when detailed formal plan AND raised valid expectation in affected parties
- Contingent liability: disclose only (not recognised) unless possibility of outflow is remote
- Contingent asset: disclose if inflow probable; recognise only when virtually certain

## Key Disclosures
- For each class of provision: carrying amount opening/closing, additions, used, reversed, unwinding of discount
- Description, expected timing, indication of uncertainties
- For contingent liabilities: brief description, estimate of financial effect, uncertainties, possibility of reimbursement

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 450): recognise when probable AND reasonably estimable (similar threshold). US GAAP does not explicitly recognise constructive obligations in the same way; no discounting requirement for provisions.
