# IAS-27: Separate Financial Statements

## What It Addresses
Prescribes accounting and disclosure for investments in subsidiaries, joint ventures, and associates when an entity prepares separate (non-consolidated) financial statements.

## Scope
Separate financial statements — financial statements of a parent, investor or venturer that elects (or is required) to present separate statements.

## Key Requirements
- Investments in subsidiaries, joint ventures, associates in separate statements: accounted for at COST, or in accordance with IFRS 9, or using equity method (IAS 28 principles)
- Must apply same accounting for each category consistently
- Dividends from investees recognised in P&L when right to receive established
- Impairment test investments at cost or IFRS 9 model as appropriate

## Key Disclosures
- Fact that separate financial statements are prepared
- List of significant investments (subsidiaries, JVs, associates): name, address, proportion of ownership, measurement method
- If parent exempted from preparing consolidated statements: name/location of parent that produces consolidated statements

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP does not require or specifically address separate financial statements for parents in the same way. SEC rules may require subsidiary-level financial statements in some filings.
