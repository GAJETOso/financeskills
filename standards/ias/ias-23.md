# IAS-23: Borrowing Costs

## What It Addresses
Prescribes the accounting treatment for borrowing costs. Requires capitalisation of borrowing costs directly attributable to the acquisition, construction, or production of a qualifying asset.

## Scope
All borrowing costs. Qualifying asset: one that necessarily takes a substantial period to get ready for intended use or sale.

## Key Requirements
- Capitalise borrowing costs directly attributable to a qualifying asset as part of cost
- All other borrowing costs: expense in period incurred
- Specific borrowings: actual borrowing costs less investment income on temporary investments
- General borrowings: apply capitalisation rate (weighted average of borrowing costs) to expenditure
- Commence capitalisation when: expenditure incurred + borrowing costs incurred + activities to prepare asset are in progress
- Suspend capitalisation during extended periods of active development interruption
- Cease capitalisation when substantially all activities to prepare asset for use/sale are complete

## Key Disclosures
- Borrowing costs capitalised during the period
- Capitalisation rate used

## Key Differences from US GAAP
US GAAP (ASC 835-20): similar mandatory capitalisation approach. Difference: US GAAP calculates avoidable interest (hypothetical borrowing needed to finance construction) rather than actual borrowing costs directly attributable. More prescriptive on which borrowings to include.
