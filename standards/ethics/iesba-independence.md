# IESBA Independence Standards (Parts 4A and 4B)

## What It Addresses
Detailed requirements for independence of auditors and assurance practitioners. Independence is a specific application of objectivity — required for audit and assurance to have credibility.

## Two Types of Independence
- **Independence of mind**: The state of mind that permits an opinion without being affected by influences that compromise professional judgement
- **Independence in appearance**: Avoidance of circumstances that would cause a reasonable and informed third party to conclude independence is compromised

## Key Threats to Independence

### Financial Interests
- Direct or material indirect financial interest in audit client: threat too significant — prohibited
- Firm or network firm cannot hold financial interests in audit clients

### Loans and Guarantees
- Loans from financial institution clients: permitted under normal commercial terms
- Other loans to/from audit clients: generally prohibited

### Business Relationships
- Close business relationship with audit client: generally prohibited unless immaterial and in ordinary course of business

### Family and Personal Relationships
- Immediate family members in key positions at audit client: evaluate and apply safeguards or resign

### Employment Relationships (Revolving Door)
- Former audit team member joining audit client in key position: cooling-off period requirements
- Former key client management joining audit firm: evaluate independence; cooling-off period

### Non-Assurance Services (NAS) to Audit Clients
Critical restrictions for audit clients that are public interest entities (PIEs):
| Service | PIE Audit Client | Non-PIE |
|---------|-----------------|---------|
| Bookkeeping | Prohibited | Evaluate threats |
| Preparation of FS | Prohibited | Evaluate threats |
| Valuation | Prohibited if material to FS | Evaluate threats |
| Tax advisory | Permitted (some restrictions) | Generally permitted |
| IT services | Prohibited if hosting financial records | Evaluate threats |
| Internal audit | Prohibited | Evaluate threats |
| Management functions | Prohibited | Prohibited |
| Legal services | Prohibited | Evaluate threats |

## Audit Partner Rotation
- Listed entity PIE audit clients: key audit partner (lead and EQR) rotation every 7 years; 2-year cooling off
- Time-on requirements vary by jurisdiction (EU: 10-year firm rotation; UK: 20 years with regular tender; Nigeria: not yet mandated at firm level)

## Public Interest Entities (PIEs)
- Listed entities; financial institutions; insurance companies; pension funds — stricter independence requirements
- Each jurisdiction may expand PIE definition (CBN: all licensed banks are PIE in Nigeria)
