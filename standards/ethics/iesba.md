# IESBA Code of Ethics for Professional Accountants

## What It Addresses
The global ethics standard for professional accountants — in public practice (auditors, tax advisors) and in business (CFOs, management accountants, internal auditors). Adopted by ICAN, ACCA, ICAEW, CIMA, and most professional bodies globally.

## Structure
- **Part 1**: Complying with the Code; Fundamental Principles; Conceptual Framework
- **Part 2**: Professional Accountants in Business (PAIBs)
- **Part 3**: Professional Accountants in Public Practice (PAPPs)
- **Part 4A**: Independence for Audit and Assurance Engagements
- **Part 4B**: Independence for Assurance Engagements Other Than Audit and Review
- **Part 5**: Sector-specific provisions (Public Sector)

## Five Fundamental Principles

| Principle | Description |
|-----------|-------------|
| **Integrity** | Be straightforward and honest in all professional and business relationships |
| **Objectivity** | Not allow bias, conflict of interest, or undue influence to override professional judgements |
| **Professional Competence and Due Care** | Maintain professional knowledge and skill; act diligently; comply with applicable technical and professional standards |
| **Confidentiality** | Do not disclose information acquired in professional relationships without proper authority; do not use for personal advantage |
| **Professional Behaviour** | Comply with laws and regulations; avoid actions that discredit the profession |

## Conceptual Framework Approach
Rather than prescriptive rules, the Code uses a threats-and-safeguards approach:
1. **Identify threats**: Self-interest; self-review; advocacy; familiarity; intimidation
2. **Evaluate significance**: Would a reasonable and informed third party conclude threats are not at an acceptable level?
3. **Apply safeguards**: Eliminate the circumstances creating threats; apply safeguards to reduce to an acceptable level; decline/resign if cannot reduce

## Conflicts of Interest
- Disclose to appropriate parties; obtain consent; implement safeguards; consider whether to withdraw
- Non-assurance services for audit clients: specific restrictions (Part 4A)

## Professional Accountants in Business (Part 2)
- Pressure from superiors to prepare misleading FS: do not comply; escalate to those charged with governance
- Confidentiality vs. whistleblowing: may disclose to appropriate authority in the public interest
- Financial interests and incentive compensation: evaluate and disclose conflicts

## Nigeria Context
- ICAN Code of Professional Conduct and Ethics is based on IESBA Code
- ANAN (Association of National Accountants of Nigeria): similar alignment
- FRC Act 2011 references adherence to IESBA ethics for FRCN-registered professionals
- Corporate governance failures in Nigerian banking (Intercontinental, Oceanic, Spring Bank) led to stronger IESBA ethics enforcement by ICAN/CBN
