# Japan: JGAAP and J-IFRS Framework

## Adoption Status
- **Listed companies**: choice of JGAAP, J-IFRS (full IFRS as issued by IASB), or US GAAP (for cross-listed)
- **J-IFRS adoption growing**: approximately 240+ companies as of 2024, including Toyota, SoftBank, Sony
- **Unlisted companies**: JGAAP required for statutory purposes

## Key JGAAP vs. IFRS Differences
- **Goodwill**: Amortised over ≤20 years under JGAAP; impairment tested only under IFRS
- **Retirement benefits**: Actuarial gains/losses may be deferred and amortised under JGAAP (corridor approach); IAS 19 requires immediate OCI
- **Revenue**: JGAAP Revenue Standard (2021) substantially aligned with IFRS 15
- **Leases**: JGAAP Finance Lease Standard; on-balance sheet for finance leases; some off-balance-sheet operating leases
- **Research and development**: JGAAP expenses all R&D; IAS 38 capitalises development phase
- **Inventory**: Lower of cost or market; LIFO permitted under JGAAP (historic — now eliminated per 2009 reform); FIFO or weighted average

## J-IFRS vs. Full IFRS
J-IFRS = full IFRS as issued by IASB. No carve-outs. Listed companies adopting voluntarily must use full IFRS.
