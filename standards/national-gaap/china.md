# China: CAS Framework

## Adoption Status
- **Listed companies**: CAS (Chinese Accounting Standards for Business Enterprises) mandatory since 2007 for A-share listed companies; substantially converged with IFRS
- **State-Owned Enterprises (SOEs)**: CAS mandatory; many large SOEs also prepare IFRS statements for international capital markets (H-shares listed in HK)
- **Banks**: PBOC and CBRC (CBIRC) regulations supplement CAS; IFRS 9/CAS equivalent for ECL
- **Government**: Government Accounting Standards (accrual government accounting introduced 2017-2019)

## Key CAS vs. IFRS Differences
- **Revenue**: CAS 14 substantially aligned with IFRS 15 (5-step model)
- **Financial instruments**: CAS 22/23/24 aligned with IFRS 9/7/32 but with some local carve-outs for banking
- **Investment property**: FV model permitted but cost model predominates in practice
- **Impairment**: No reversal of goodwill impairment (consistent with IFRS)
- **Group restructuring**: Common control business combinations — pooling method (different from IFRS 3)
- **Related parties**: Broader definition including government-related entities (significant for SOEs)
- **Government grants**: CAS 16 aligned with IAS 20 but specific rules for tax incentives

## Convergence Status
MoF has been progressively converging CAS with IFRS since 2006. Key outstanding differences:
- Special transactions (e.g., entity restructuring in SOE context)
- Government-related entity related party disclosures
- Debt-to-equity swaps

## IFRS vs. CAS Reconciliation
H-share listed companies in Hong Kong present financial statements under HK GAAP (substantially same as IFRS) with CAS reconciliation. A-share only companies use CAS.
