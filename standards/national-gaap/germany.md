# Germany: HGB Framework

## Adoption Status
- **Listed company consolidated statements**: IFRS (EU-adopted) mandatory since 2005
- **Statutory (standalone/Einzelabschluss) statements**: HGB — required for all companies incorporated in Germany (for legal/tax/dividend purposes)
- **Unlisted group consolidated statements**: choice of HGB or IFRS

## Key HGB vs. IFRS Differences (Significant Divergence)
- **Conservatism (Vorsichtsprinzip)**: Strong prudence principle — losses anticipated, gains only recognised when realised
- **Creditor protection**: HGB balance sheet must be available for dividend distribution and creditor claims
- **Goodwill**: Amortised over useful life (not impairment tested) — aligned with FRS 102, not IFRS/US GAAP
- **Pensions**: German pension reserves (Pensionsrückstellungen): discounted at 10-year average market rate (HGB rate, currently different from IAS 19 discount rate)
- **Hidden reserves (stille Reserven)**: Deliberately permitted in HGB for some items (prudence)
- **Financial instruments**: Generally at cost or amortised cost; fair value limited
- **Deferred taxes**: Tax allocation approach aligned with HGB book-tax differences
- **Revenue**: HGB revenue based on realisation principle — transfer of risk and reward; not 5-step IFRS 15 model

## Tax Linkage (Maßgeblichkeitsprinzip)
HGB accounts form the basis for German trade tax (Gewerbesteuer) and corporation tax (Körperschaftsteuer). Strong linkage between commercial and tax accounts.
