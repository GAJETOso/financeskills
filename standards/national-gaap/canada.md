# Canada: Financial Reporting Framework

## Adoption Status
- **Public companies (TSX/TSX-V listed)**: IFRS as issued by IASB — mandatory since 2011
- **Private enterprises**: ASPE (Accounting Standards for Private Enterprises) — Canadian private company GAAP
- **Not-for-profit organisations**: ASNPO (Accounting Standards for Not-for-Profit Organisations)
- **Pension plans**: Part IV of CPA Canada Handbook
- **Government**: Public Sector Accounting Standards (PSAS) — Canadian equivalent of IPSAS

## ASPE (Private Enterprise GAAP) Key Features
- Simplified standards for private companies; not IFRS-based
- **Goodwill**: amortised over useful life (max 10 years if uncertain) — similar to FRS 102/HGB
- **Financial instruments**: simplified measurement; no ECL model requirement
- **Leases**: operating/finance lease distinction (not yet IFRS 16 aligned)
- **Revenue**: principles-based approach; not full IFRS 15 5-step model
- **Income taxes**: taxes payable method permitted (not deferred tax required)

## IFRS vs. ASPE
Many private companies choose IFRS if they anticipate accessing capital markets. ASPE is typically lower compliance cost.
