# Australia: AASB Framework

## Adoption Status
Australia adopted IFRS for for-profit entities from 1 January 2005:
- **For-profit entities**: AASB standards = IFRS with Australian additions (not-for-profit and public sector paragraphs)
- **Not-for-profit private sector**: AASB standards with NFP modifications
- **Public sector**: AASB standards with public sector modifications (aligned with IPSAS where relevant)

## Key AASB Features
- AASB adds Australian-specific paragraphs to each standard for NFP and public sector
- AASB 1 (First-time Adoption): includes Australian addenda for NFP first-time adopters
- AASB 1054: Australian-specific disclosures required beyond IFRS (e.g., franking credits, statutory reporting)
- **Sustainability**: Australia mandated IFRS S1 and S2 (as AASB S1/S2) from 2025 for large listed entities — one of first mandatory adopters globally
- **Tier 2 (RDR)**: Reduced Disclosure Requirements for non-publicly accountable for-profit entities (analogous to FRS 101/IFRS 19)

## Sustainability Reporting Timeline (AASB S1/S2)
- Group 1 (large listed + financial institutions): from 1 January 2025
- Group 2 (other large entities): from 1 July 2026
- Group 3 (medium entities): from 1 July 2027
