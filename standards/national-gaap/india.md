# India: Ind AS Framework

## Adoption Status
India adopted Ind AS (IFRS-converged standards) on phased basis from 2016:
- **Listed companies and large companies** (net worth ≥ INR 250 crore): Ind AS from 2016/17
- **Banks**: RBI phased approach; currently on Indian GAAP with some Ind AS elements
- **Insurance**: IRDAI guidance; transitioning to Ind AS 117 (aligned with IFRS 17)
- **Smaller companies**: Indian GAAP (AS standards, older framework) still applies

## Key Differences: Ind AS vs. IFRS
- **Ind AS 101** (First-time Adoption): carve-outs from IFRS 1 for Indian context
- **Ind AS 109** (Financial Instruments): RBI guidance on provisioning may override some ECL requirements for banks
- **Ind AS 103** (Business Combinations): common control transactions — pooling method permitted (unlike IFRS 3)
- **Ind AS 19** (Employee Benefits): actuarial gains/losses — same OCI treatment as IAS 19
- **Ind AS 116** (Leases): same as IFRS 16
- **Schedule III** (Companies Act 2013): format of financial statements prescribed by law — some line items mandated differently from IAS 1

## Retained Indian GAAP Elements
- Statement of value added (not required but disclosed by many companies)
- Directors' Report format per Companies Act 2013
- SEBI listing obligations for quarterly/annual filings (LODR Regulations)

## Regulator Requirements
- ICAI issues Ind AS standards (converged with IFRS); MCA notifies via Schedule II
- SEBI: listed company disclosures and quarterly results (Regulation 33)
- RBI: banking sector specific; CBN equivalent
