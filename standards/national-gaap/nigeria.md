# Nigeria: Financial Reporting Framework

## Adoption Status
Nigeria fully adopted IFRS for public interest entities (PIEs) in 2012:
- **Listed companies and significant public entities**: full IFRS from 2012
- **Non-publicly accountable entities**: IFRS for SMEs (permitted); Nigerian SAS replaced
- **Public sector**: IPSAS (accrual basis transitional adoption per IPSAS 33; FGN agencies on phased adoption)
- **Small companies**: FRCN issued SMEGAS (Small and Medium-sized Entity Generally Accepted Accounting Standards)

## Regulator
**Financial Reporting Council of Nigeria (FRC Act 2011)**
- Sets accounting and auditing standards
- Registers and regulates preparers of financial statements
- Oversees adoption of IFRS, IPSAS, and auditing standards (ISA)

## Tax Authority
**Nigeria Revenue Service (NRS)** — formerly the Federal Inland Revenue Service (FIRS), renamed under the **Nigeria Revenue Service (Establishment) Act 2025**, one of four Acts comprising Nigeria's landmark 2024/2025 tax reform legislation.

### Nigeria Tax Reform 2024/2025 — Key Acts
Signed into law by President Bola Tinubu in 2025, the Tax Reform consolidates and modernises Nigeria's tax framework across four Acts:

| Act | What It Does |
|-----|-------------|
| **Nigeria Tax Act (NTA) 2025** | Consolidates CITA, PITA, CGT Act, VAT Act, Stamp Duties Act, and others into a single omnibus tax law |
| **Nigeria Tax Administration Act (NTAA) 2025** | Harmonises tax administration, assessment, and dispute resolution across all taxes |
| **Nigeria Revenue Service (Establishment) Act 2025** | Renames FIRS to NRS; expands mandate to include all federation taxes |
| **Joint Revenue Board (Establishment) Act 2025** | Establishes new board to coordinate federal, state, and LGA tax authorities; replaces JTB |

### Key NTA 2025 Changes Affecting Financial Reporting
- **Companies Income Tax (CIT)**: Standard rate retained at 30% for large companies; SME reliefs expanded
- **VAT**: Rate changes and input credit expansion under review; B2B VAT credit regime improved
- **Withholding Tax Regulations 2024** (already in force pre-NTA): Revised WHT rates; principal/agent distinction for WHT obligations — material for revenue recognition and expense accruals
- **Transfer Pricing**: NRS TP Regulations retained; Country-by-Country Reporting (CbCR) rules under NTA
