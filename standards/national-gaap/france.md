# France: PCG Framework

## Adoption Status
- **Listed company consolidated statements**: EU-adopted IFRS mandatory since 2005
- **Statutory (individual entity) statements**: PCG (Plan Comptable Général) mandatory for all French entities
- **Group consolidated (unlisted)**: choice of PCG-based French GAAP or IFRS

## Key PCG vs. IFRS Differences
- **Codified chart of accounts**: PCG prescribes a uniform chart of accounts with specific account numbers — no equivalent in IFRS
- **Prudence and realisation**: similar to HGB; strong conservatism
- **Goodwill**: historically amortised; French reform (1999) allowed non-amortisation for unlimited life goodwill; EU directive alignment
- **Revenue**: realisation basis (transfer of risk/reward); French reform progressively aligning with IFRS 15
- **Financial instruments**: cost-based for statutory; fair value only in limited circumstances
- **Leases**: operating/finance distinction in statutory accounts; off-balance-sheet operating leases
- **Provisions for charges (provisions pour charges)**: French statutory accounts permit more provisioning flexibility than IAS 37

## Tax Linkage
French statutory accounts form the basis for corporation tax (Impôt sur les sociétés). Strong statutory-tax linkage as in Germany.
