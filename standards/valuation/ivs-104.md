# IVS 104: Bases of Value

## What It Addresses
Defines the standard bases of value used in IVS assignments. The basis of value must be stated in every valuation report; it determines the objective and assumptions underlying the valuation.

## Bases of Value

### Market Value
**Definition:** The estimated amount for which an asset or liability should exchange on the valuation date between a willing buyer and a willing seller in an arm's length transaction after proper marketing and where the parties have each acted knowledgeably, prudently and without compulsion.  
**Use:** Property valuations; IFRS 13 FV measurement; collateral; acquisition pricing

### Investment Value (Worth)
**Definition:** The value of an asset to a particular owner or prospective owner for individual investment or operational objectives.  
**Use:** Capital budgeting; entity-specific hold/sell decisions; synergy quantification

### Fair Value (IVS definition — distinct from IFRS 13)
**Definition (IVS):** The price that would be received to sell an asset or paid to transfer a liability in an orderly transaction between market participants at the measurement date — identical to IFRS 13.  
**Note:** When performing valuations for IFRS 13, use the IFRS 13 definition; IVS 104 aligns with it.

### Liquidation Value
**Definition:** Amount that would be realised when an asset is sold on a piecemeal basis, usually under distress conditions. Always less than market value.  
**Use:** Insolvency proceedings; collateral enforcement; going concern assessment (IAS 1/ISA 570)

### Synergistic Value
**Definition:** Value above market value arising from combining two or more interests. Relevant in business combinations (IFRS 3 purchase price allocation).

## Common Judgment Areas
- Choosing the appropriate basis for the purpose of the engagement is critical — same asset, different bases = significantly different values
- IFRS 13 requires exit price (hypothetical sale) not entity-specific value — ensure basis aligns
