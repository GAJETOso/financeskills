# IVS 210: Intangible Asset Valuation

## What It Addresses
Valuation of intangible assets including: customer relationships; technology/IP; trade names/brands; contracts; non-compete agreements. Critical for IFRS 3 PPA and IAS 38 impairment.

## IAS 38 Separability Criteria
An intangible must be separately recognised if it is: (a) separable (can be sold/transferred independently) OR (b) arises from contractual/legal rights. IVS 210 helps determine which intangibles are separable and how to value them.

## Common Intangible Asset Methods

| Intangible | Primary Method | Notes |
|-----------|---------------|-------|
| Customer relationships | MPEEM or With/Without | WARA test; attrition rate critical |
| Technology / IP | Relief from Royalty | Royalty rate from comparable licences |
| Trade name / brand | Relief from Royalty | Royalty rates 0.5–5% of revenue typically |
| Backlog | MPEEM or excess earnings | Short amortisation life |
| Non-compete | With/Without (income loss prevented) | Probability of competition scenario |
| In-process R&D | Stage-of-completion DCF | Higher risk adjustment |

## WARA Test (Weighted Average Return on Assets)
After PPA, the weighted average return across all identified assets must equal the WACC of the acquired business. Used to validate reasonableness of individual asset values and useful lives.
