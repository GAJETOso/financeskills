# IVS 500: Financial Instrument Valuation

## What It Addresses
Valuation of financial instruments — equity, fixed income, derivatives, structured products. Bridges IVS methodology with IFRS 9 and IFRS 13 Level 2/3 fair value requirements.

## Key Approaches by Instrument

| Instrument | Approach | Key Inputs |
|-----------|----------|-----------|
| Listed equity | Market approach (Level 1) | Quoted price × shares |
| Unlisted equity | Income approach (DCF) or market approach (multiples) | Level 3; WACC; comparables |
| Government bonds | Market approach | Yield curve; credit spread |
| Corporate bonds | Income approach (DCF) | Risk-free rate; credit spread; OAS |
| Interest rate swaps | Income approach | Yield curve; netting |
| Options / warrants | Option pricing models (Black-Scholes; binomial) | Volatility; risk-free rate; expected life |
| Convertible bonds | Binomial lattice or Monte Carlo | Volatility; credit spread; conversion terms |
| Loan portfolios | DCF on expected cash flows less ECL | PD; LGD; EAD per IFRS 9 |

## IFRS 13 Level 3 Disclosures
For Level 3 instruments, IFRS 7/IFRS 13 require: description of valuation technique; significant unobservable inputs; sensitivity analysis; reconciliation of opening/closing balances. IVS 500 assists in documenting these requirements.
