# IVS 200: Business Valuation

## What It Addresses
Requirements for valuing businesses, business interests, and equity securities. Applies to: IFRS 3 purchase price allocations; impairment testing (IAS 36); employee share schemes (IFRS 2); shareholder disputes; M&A; estate/tax.

## Equity vs. Enterprise Value
- **Enterprise Value (EV)**: Total business value to all capital providers (debt + equity). Derived from WACC-discounted FCF or EV multiples.
- **Equity Value**: EV minus net debt. Derived from equity DCF or P/E multiples.
- **Bridge**: EV → Equity: subtract debt, add cash, adjust for minorities, pension deficit, contingent liabilities.

## Key Considerations
- **Capital Structure**: WACC must reflect target/market capital structure, not book values
- **Normalisation**: Remove non-recurring items, owner's compensation adjustments, related party transactions
- **Control Premium / Minority Discount**: Quoted minority trades at a discount to control value; M&A deals include control premium (20–35% typical)
- **DLOM (Discount for Lack of Marketability)**: Private company equity illiquidity discount (10–30% range); empirical studies: restricted stock studies, pre-IPO studies

## IFRS 3 Purchase Price Allocation (PPA)
Upon business combination, IFRS 3 requires:
1. Identify all assets acquired and liabilities assumed at FV
2. Identify and value separable intangible assets (IVS 210)
3. Residual = goodwill
IVS 200 and 210 are the primary valuation standards for this exercise.
