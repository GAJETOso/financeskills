# IVS 105: Valuation Approaches and Methods

## What It Addresses
Sets out the three valuation approaches and specific methods within each. Valuers must consider all three approaches and explain why any is not applied.

## The Three Approaches

### 1. Market Approach
Uses prices from comparable transactions to indicate value.

**Methods:**
- **Guideline Public Company Method (GPC)**: Trading multiples (EV/EBITDA, P/E, EV/Revenue) from comparable listed companies
- **Guideline Transaction Method (GTM)**: Acquisition multiples from completed M&A deals — typically higher than GPC (control premium)
- **Comparable Uncontrolled Transaction (CUT)**: For intellectual property and intangible licensing rates

**Key adjustments:** Size premium; CSAD (control, size, and company-specific adjustments); minority discount; lack of marketability discount (DLOM) for private company equity

### 2. Income Approach
Converts expected future benefits (cash flows or income) into a present value.

**Methods:**
- **Discounted Cash Flow (DCF)**: Project FCF or equity cash flows; discount at WACC or cost of equity; terminal value (Gordon Growth or exit multiple)
- **Capitalisation of Earnings**: Single-period earnings divided by capitalisation rate (r − g); appropriate for stable, mature businesses
- **Dividend Discount Model (DDM)**: PV of expected future dividends; used for financial institutions and dividend-paying equities
- **Relief from Royalty (RfR)**: For intangible assets — PV of notional royalties saved by owning the IP
- **Multi-Period Excess Earnings (MPEEM)**: For primary intangible assets — isolates cash flows attributable to the intangible

### 3. Cost Approach
Determines value based on current cost to replace or reproduce the asset.

**Methods:**
- **Replacement Cost New (RCN)**: Cost to replace with equivalent utility new asset
- **Depreciated Replacement Cost (DRC)**: RCN less physical deterioration and obsolescence — most common for property, plant, and specialised assets
- **Reproduction Cost**: Cost to recreate exact replica (historical buildings, specialised equipment)

## Reconciliation
Where multiple approaches are applied, the valuer must reconcile to a single value conclusion — explain weighting rationale, not simply average.
