# Nigeria Corporate Governance Codes

## SEC Nigeria Code of Corporate Governance (2021)
- Applies to all public companies and listed entities
- Based on OECD Principles and King IV; apply-and-explain approach
- Key requirements: board independence (minimum 1/3 independent directors); audit committee; board evaluation; remuneration policy; whistleblower provisions

## CBN Code of Corporate Governance for Banks (2014, revised 2022)
- Applies to all licensed banks; more prescriptive than SEC code
- CAE functional reporting to board audit committee
- CEO tenure limits; family ownership caps
- Risk management committee mandatory at board level

## NAICOM Corporate Governance Guidelines for Insurers
- Applies to all licensed insurance companies
- Board composition; actuarial function independence; investment committee
- Risk management framework aligned with Solvency II principles

## PENCOM (Pension) Governance
- Corporate governance guidelines for Pension Fund Administrators (PFAs) and Pension Fund Custodians (PFCs)
- Investment guidelines; responsible investment considerations

## Public Sector: OAGF and ICPC
- Office of the Accountant General of the Federation: internal control framework for MDAs (COSO/INTOSAI-aligned)
- ICPC (Independent Corrupt Practices Commission): Institutional ethics and anti-corruption compliance
- EFCC: Financial crimes enforcement
