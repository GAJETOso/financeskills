# COSO ERM 2017

## What It Addresses
Provides principles-based guidance for integrating enterprise risk management into strategy-setting and performance management. Moves beyond the original 2004 "cube" model to a more dynamic, strategy-focused framework.

## Five Components (2017)
1. **Governance and Culture**: Board oversight; operating structures; attraction/development of talent; commitment to core values; accountability
2. **Strategy and Objective-Setting**: Business context; risk appetite; alternative strategies; business objectives
3. **Performance**: Risk identification; risk assessment (likelihood + impact); risk prioritisation; risk response; portfolio view
4. **Review and Revision**: Substantial change; review of ERM performance; pursuit of improvement
5. **Information, Communication, and Reporting**: Risk information; reporting risk, culture, and performance

## 20 Principles
Organised across the five components. Key principles include:
- Principle 6: Define risk appetite — amount of risk willing to accept in pursuit of strategy
- Principle 9: Assess severity of risk — likelihood and impact; inherent vs. residual risk
- Principle 13: Implements risk responses — accept, avoid, pursue, reduce, share
- Principle 15: Assesses substantial change — monitor for disruptive events

## Risk Appetite vs. Risk Tolerance
- **Risk Appetite**: High-level statement of how much risk the entity is willing to accept (board-set)
- **Risk Tolerance**: Acceptable variance around objectives; operational-level application of risk appetite
- **Risk Capacity**: Maximum risk the entity can absorb given its resources

## Relationship to COSO IC
COSO IC focuses on internal control; COSO ERM is broader — encompasses strategy, objectives, and performance. ERM includes IC but extends to strategic and external risks.
