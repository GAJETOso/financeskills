# ISO 31000: Risk Management Guidelines

## What It Addresses
International standard providing principles, framework, and process for managing risk in any organisation — private, public, or not-for-profit. More universally applicable than COSO (which is US-centric and ICFR-focused).

## Three Parts
1. **Principles**: Value creation; integrated; structured and comprehensive; customised; inclusive; dynamic; best available information; human and cultural factors; continual improvement
2. **Framework**: Leadership and commitment; integration; design; implementation; evaluation; improvement
3. **Process**: Scope, context, criteria → Risk Assessment (identification + analysis + evaluation) → Risk Treatment → Monitoring and Review → Communication and Consultation

## Risk Assessment Process
- **Risk Identification**: What could happen? Where? When? How? Why? Causes and potential consequences
- **Risk Analysis**: Likelihood + consequence; qualitative/semi-quantitative/quantitative methods
- **Risk Evaluation**: Compare analysis results against risk criteria; prioritise for treatment

## Risk Treatment Options
- Avoid (decide not to start/continue the risk-creating activity)
- Remove risk source
- Change likelihood
- Change consequences
- Share risk (insurance, contracts)
- Retain risk (by informed decision)

## Comparison with COSO
| Feature | ISO 31000 | COSO ERM |
|---------|-----------|----------|
| Scope | Universal; any risk | Enterprise; strategy-linked |
| Geographic focus | Global | US-origin; globally adopted |
| Prescriptiveness | Principles-based; high flexibility | More structured |
| Control framework | Does not include | Links to COSO IC |
| Certifiable | No (unlike ISO 9001) | No |
