# COSO Internal Control — Integrated Framework (2013)

## What It Addresses
The most widely used framework globally for designing, implementing, and evaluating internal control over financial reporting (ICFR) and operational/compliance controls. Mandatory reference for SOX 404 ICFR assessments.

## Five Components
1. **Control Environment**: Tone at the top; integrity and ethical values; board independence; organisational structure; accountability; commitment to competence
2. **Risk Assessment**: Identify and analyse risks to achieving objectives; consider fraud risk; identify significant change
3. **Control Activities**: Policies and procedures; general IT controls; application controls; segregation of duties
4. **Information and Communication**: Relevant, quality information; internal and external communication; reporting up, across, and down
5. **Monitoring Activities**: Ongoing monitoring; separate evaluations; deficiency reporting and remediation

## 17 Principles
Each component has associated principles (e.g., Principle 1: Demonstrates commitment to integrity and ethical values; Principle 6: Specifies suitable objectives; Principle 10: Selects and develops control activities). All 17 must be present and functioning for the framework to be effective.

## SOX 404 Linkage (US / SEC-registered entities)
- Management's ICFR assessment (SOX 404a) must use a recognised control framework — COSO IC 2013 is the de facto standard
- External auditor attestation (SOX 404b) under PCAOB AS 2201 evaluates management's assessment and independently tests ICFR
- Material weaknesses and significant deficiencies must be disclosed

## IPSAS / Public Sector
INTOSAI GOV 9100 (Guidelines for Internal Control Standards for the Public Sector) is based on COSO IC. Nigerian OAGF internal control framework for MDAs is INTOSAI/COSO-aligned.
