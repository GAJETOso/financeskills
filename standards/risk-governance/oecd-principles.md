# G20/OECD Principles of Corporate Governance (2023)

## What It Addresses
International benchmark for corporate governance policy and practice. Endorsed by G20 leaders. Used by the World Bank, IMF, and regulators globally (including Nigerian SEC) as the reference for evaluating and developing governance frameworks.

## Six Chapters (2023 Revision)
1. **Ensuring the Basis for an Effective Corporate Governance Framework**: Regulatory quality; rule of law; supervisory effectiveness
2. **Rights and Equitable Treatment of Shareholders and Key Ownership Functions**: Share rights; equitable treatment; participation in key decisions; AGMs; markets for corporate control
3. **Institutional Investors, Stock Markets, and Other Intermediaries**: Beneficial ownership; conflicts of interest; proxy advisors; stock market integrity
4. **The Role of Stakeholders in Corporate Governance**: Employee participation; creditor rights; ESG considerations; whistleblower protection
5. **Disclosure and Transparency**: Financial statements (IFRS/GAAP); governance disclosures; related party transactions; sustainability
6. **The Responsibilities of the Board**: Board functions; fiduciary duties; board composition; audit committee; director nominations and remuneration

## 2023 Updates
- Enhanced ESG/sustainability disclosure requirements
- Expanded section on institutional investor stewardship
- Digital and technology governance considerations
- More robust whistleblower and anti-corruption provisions
