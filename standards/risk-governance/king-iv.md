# King IV: Corporate Governance (South Africa)

## What It Addresses
The fourth iteration of South Africa's King Reports on Corporate Governance — the most influential governance code in Africa and among the most advanced globally. King IV is principles-based, apply-and-explain (not comply-or-explain), and outcome-oriented.

## Four Governance Outcomes
1. Ethical culture
2. Good performance
3. Effective control
4. Legitimacy

## 17 Principles
Covering: ethical leadership; organisational ethics; responsible corporate citizenship; strategy and performance; reporting and disclosure; primary governance structures (board, board committees, CEO, management, audit committee); governance functional areas (risk, technology, compliance, remuneration, assurance, stakeholders).

## Key Features
- **Apply and Explain**: Organisations apply all principles; explain how they have done so (not just comply or explain deviation)
- **Integrated Report**: King IV strongly advocates for integrated reporting (IIRC/<IR> Framework) combining financial and non-financial performance
- **Remuneration**: Detailed requirements for remuneration policy and implementation; shareholder vote (SRI voting guidance)
- **Technology and Information Governance**: Board responsible for IT governance; align with COBIT or similar
- **Internal Audit**: Independent; reports to audit committee; risk-based plan approved by board

## African / Nigeria Relevance
- JSE (Johannesburg Stock Exchange) Listings Requirements mandate King IV for listed companies
- Nigerian SEC Code of Corporate Governance (2011, revised 2021) draws heavily on King IV principles
- CBN Code of Corporate Governance for Banks references similar outcomes to King IV
- Many NNPC and Nigerian listed company governance frameworks reference King IV as best practice
