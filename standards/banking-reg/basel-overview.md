# Basel Accords: Overview

## What They Address
The Basel Accords are international regulatory frameworks for bank capital adequacy, stress testing, and market liquidity. Issued by the BCBS; adopted by member jurisdictions (including Nigeria via CBN).

## Evolution

### Basel I (1988)
- First international capital standard; credit risk only
- Minimum 8% total capital ratio against risk-weighted assets (RWAs)
- Simple risk weight buckets: 0% (sovereigns), 20% (banks), 50% (mortgages), 100% (corporate loans)
- Limitation: no differentiation of credit quality within categories; no market or operational risk

### Basel II (2004 / 2006)
Three pillars:
1. **Pillar 1 — Minimum Capital**: Credit risk (Standardised Approach or IRB); Market risk (Standardised or IMA); Operational risk (Basic Indicator, Standardised, AMA)
2. **Pillar 2 — Supervisory Review**: ICAAP (Internal Capital Adequacy Assessment Process); stress testing; supervisor discretion to impose additional buffers
3. **Pillar 3 — Market Discipline**: Disclosure requirements; Pillar 3 report

### Basel 2.5 (2009 — post-GFC immediate response)
- Stressed VaR; incremental risk charge; securitisation capital increases

### Basel III (2010 / phased in to 2019–2025)
See [basel-iii.md](./basel-iii.md) — major reform post-2008 GFC.

### Basel III Finalisation / "Basel IV" (2017 / effective 2025–2028)
See [basel-iii-reforms.md](./basel-iii-reforms.md) — output floor; revised standardised approaches; FRTB.
