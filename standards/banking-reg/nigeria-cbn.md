# CBN Prudential Framework for Nigerian Banks

## Capital Requirements (CBN Basel III Implementation)
- Minimum CET1: 10.5% (includes 2.5% conservation buffer; above Basel III minimum)
- Domestic SIBs (D-SIBs): Additional 1% CET1 surcharge → 11.5% effective minimum
- Total CAR: 15% for domestic banks; 16% for D-SIBs
- Leverage ratio: ≥ 3% (aligned with Basel III)

## Minimum Capital Base (nominal)
Post-CBN recapitalisation directive (March 2024 — 24-month compliance window to 2026):
| Bank Category | Minimum Paid-up Capital |
|---------------|------------------------|
| International authorisation | NGN 500 billion |
| National authorisation | NGN 200 billion |
| Regional authorisation | NGN 50 billion |

## Liquidity
- LCR: ≥ 100% (aligned with Basel III)
- Liquidity ratio: ≥ 30% (CBN-specific; cash + short-term government securities ÷ total deposits)

## Credit Risk / ECL (IFRS 9 + CBN Prudential)
- IFRS 9 ECL is the primary impairment model for regulatory reporting
- CBN Prudential Guidelines (2010, amended): Statutory minimum provisions that may exceed IFRS 9 ECL
  - Substandard (Stage 2 equivalent): Minimum 10% provision
  - Doubtful: Minimum 50% provision
  - Lost: Minimum 100% provision
- Where IFRS 9 ECL < CBN statutory minimum: difference recognised in regulatory risk reserve (equity; not P&L)

## Concentration Limits
- Single obligor limit: 20% of shareholders' funds unimpaired by losses (5% for insiders)
- Sector concentration monitored by CBN macro-prudential framework
