# Basel III: Capital, Leverage, and Liquidity

## Capital Requirements

### Capital Quality — Three Tiers
| Tier | Components | Key Criteria |
|------|-----------|-------------|
| CET1 (Common Equity Tier 1) | Ordinary shares; retained earnings; AOCI | Highest loss-absorbing capacity; no maturity |
| AT1 (Additional Tier 1) | Perpetual contingent convertibles (CoCos); hybrid instruments | Written down / converted on trigger event |
| Tier 2 | Subordinated debt (≥ 5 yr); general provisions | Gone-concern; absorbs losses in liquidation |

### Minimum Ratios
| Ratio | Minimum | With Conservation Buffer | With G-SIB Surcharge |
|-------|---------|------------------------|---------------------|
| CET1 | 4.5% | 7.0% | Up to 9.5% |
| Tier 1 | 6.0% | 8.5% | Up to 11.0% |
| Total Capital | 8.0% | 10.5% | Up to 13.0% |

### Capital Buffers
- **Capital Conservation Buffer (CCB)**: 2.5% CET1; if breached, restrictions on dividends and bonuses
- **Countercyclical Capital Buffer (CCyB)**: 0–2.5% CET1; set by national regulator based on credit cycle; absorbs systemic risk
- **G-SIB Surcharge**: 1.0–3.5% additional CET1 for Global Systemically Important Banks
- **D-SIB Surcharge**: Domestic SIB surcharge set by national regulator (CBN: 1% for D-SIBs in Nigeria)

## Leverage Ratio
- Tier 1 Capital ÷ Total Exposure ≥ 3% (G-SIBs: 3.5%)
- Exposure includes: on-balance-sheet assets + derivatives (SA-CCR) + SFTs + off-balance-sheet items
- Non-risk-based backstop; prevents excessive leverage regardless of RWA optimisation

## Liquidity Standards

### LCR (Liquidity Coverage Ratio)
- HQLA ÷ Net Stressed Outflows (30-day) ≥ 100%
- HQLA Level 1: Cash, central bank reserves, Level 1 sovereign bonds (0% haircut)
- HQLA Level 2A: Covered bonds, sovereign bonds (15% haircut); Level 2B: RMBS, equities (25–50% haircut)
- Stress scenario: Combined idiosyncratic + market-wide stress

### NSFR (Net Stable Funding Ratio)
- Available Stable Funding (ASF) ÷ Required Stable Funding (RSF) ≥ 100%
- Promotes longer-term structural funding alignment

## IFRS 9 Interaction
Basel III capital requirements interact significantly with IFRS 9:
- IFRS 9 Day 1 ECL provisions reduce CET1 at transition (phase-in adjustments permitted)
- Stage 3 provisions fully deductible from CET1; excess general provisions (Stage 1/2) may qualify as Tier 2 up to 1.25% of RWAs (Standardised Approach)
- Dynamic provisioning vs. capital buffers debate ongoing
