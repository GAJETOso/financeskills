# Basel III Finalisation ("Basel IV") and FRTB

## What It Addresses
The 2017 finalisation package addresses RWA variability and credibility issues identified post-2008. Often called "Basel IV" by industry (BCBS prefers "Basel III finalisation").

## Key Reforms

### Output Floor
- Banks using internal models (IRB, IMA) cannot produce RWAs below 72.5% of standardised approach RWAs
- Phased in: 50% (2025) → 55% (2026) → 60% (2027) → 65% (2028) → 70% (2029) → 72.5% (2030)
- Significantly impacts banks with strong internal model benefits — European banks most affected

### Revised Credit Risk Standardised Approach (SA-CR)
- More risk-sensitive than Basel II SA; removes mechanical reliance on external ratings (US requirement)
- Better granularity for retail, SME, real estate, infrastructure

### Revised IRB Approaches
- A-IRB prohibited for large corporates, banks, and sovereigns — F-IRB only
- A-IRB input floors: PD ≥ 0.05%; LGD floors for retail/unsecured exposures

### Revised Operational Risk
- New Standardised Measurement Approach (SMA) replaces all existing op risk models (including AMA)
- Three components: Business Indicator Component + Internal Loss Multiplier

### FRTB — Fundamental Review of the Trading Book
- Replaces market risk framework (VaR-based Basel 2.5)
- **Boundary**: Clear test for Trading Book vs. Banking Book assignment
- **Standardised Approach (SA)**: Sensitivity-Based Method (SBM) + Default Risk Charge (DRC) + Residual Risk Add-On (RRAO)
- **Internal Models Approach (IMA)**: Expected Shortfall (ES, 97.5%) replaces VaR (99%); desk-level approval; P&L attribution test
- Effective date: 1 January 2026 (BCBS); jurisdiction-dependent

## Nigeria / Africa Implementation
- CBN has adopted Basel III capital and liquidity framework progressively since 2015
- Output floor and FRTB adoption timelines: not yet formally announced as of 2025; CBN monitoring implementation
- Nigerian banks generally Standardised Approach for credit and operational risk
