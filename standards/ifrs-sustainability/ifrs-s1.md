# IFRS S1: General Requirements for Disclosure of Sustainability-related Financial Information

## What It Addresses
Sets out the overall requirements for disclosing material sustainability-related financial information across the full range of sustainability risks and opportunities an entity faces. IFRS S1 is the foundation standard; IFRS S2 (climate) and future topical standards build on it.

## Scope
Any entity that prepares sustainability-related financial disclosures in accordance with ISSB standards. Not limited to listed entities — but regulators determine which entities must comply in their jurisdictions.

## Key Requirements
- **Core content areas** — for each material sustainability-related risk and opportunity, disclose:
  - **Governance**: processes, controls, and procedures to monitor and manage sustainability-related risks/opportunities; board oversight; management roles
  - **Strategy**: how sustainability-related risks and opportunities affect business model, strategy, and financial planning; short/medium/long-term time horizons
  - **Risk management**: processes to identify, assess, prioritise, and monitor sustainability-related risks; integration with overall risk management
  - **Metrics and targets**: performance metrics; progress towards targets; comparatives
- **Materiality**: information is material if omitting, misstating, or obscuring it could reasonably influence primary users' decisions
- **Connected information**: link sustainability disclosures to financial statements; quantify financial effects where possible
- **Comparative information**: prior period comparatives required
- **Timing**: disclose at same time as related financial statements
- **Location**: annual financial report (or report containing financial statements)
- **Proportionality**: entities can use available skills and capabilities while working towards full disclosure over time (relief for Year 1)

## Key Disclosures
- Sustainability-related governance structure
- Strategy: anticipated effects of sustainability risks/opportunities on cash flows, access to finance, and cost of capital over short/medium/long term
- Risk management processes: including whether integrated with entity-wide risk management
- Metrics and targets used to monitor sustainability performance
- Scenario analysis (encouraged; required for IFRS S2 climate)
- Industry-based metrics: reference to SASB standards for sector-specific metrics (encouraged)

## Relationship to Other Frameworks
- Built on TCFD (Task Force on Climate-related Financial Disclosures) framework — IFRS S1 generalises TCFD to all sustainability topics
- References SASB industry-based disclosure topics for sector-specific metrics
- Compatible with GRI (which focuses on impacts on society/environment); IFRS focuses on financial materiality
- Being adopted/referenced by: UK (ISSB-aligned standards via FRC), EU (partial alignment via CSRD/ESRS), Australia (mandatory from 2025), Singapore, Canada, Japan, and others
- SEC (US): separate climate disclosure rules (scaled back 2024); partial overlap with IFRS S2

## Implementation Jurisdictions (as of 2024-2025)
- **Australia**: mandatory for large listed entities from 2025 (AASB S1/S2)
- **UK**: ISSB-aligned UK Sustainability Disclosure Standards under development
- **Singapore**: SGX-listed companies mandatory from 2025
- **Japan**: voluntary adoption encouraged; progressive mandatory adoption
- **Nigeria**: SEC Nigeria and FRCN reviewing ISSB adoption framework
- **EU**: CSRD/ESRS takes precedence for EU entities (broader scope; double materiality)
