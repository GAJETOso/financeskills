# IFRS S2: Climate-related Disclosures

## What It Addresses
Prescribes specific requirements for disclosing information about climate-related risks and opportunities, building on the four core content areas of IFRS S1 (governance, strategy, risk management, metrics and targets) as applied to climate.

## Scope
Any entity applying IFRS S2 must also apply IFRS S1. Climate scope includes both physical risks and transition risks and opportunities.

## Key Requirements
- **Governance** (same as IFRS S1 but climate-specific): board climate expertise; climate considerations in compensation
- **Strategy**:
  - Climate-related risks and opportunities that could affect entity over short, medium, and long term
  - Effects on business model and value chain
  - Effects on strategy and decision-making (climate integration into planning)
  - Financial position, financial performance, and cash flows — current and anticipated
  - **Climate resilience**: entity's ability to adjust or adapt strategy in response to climate transition scenarios; use of climate scenario analysis
  - Transition plan if the entity has one
- **Risk management**: climate risk identification process; how risks are prioritised; integration with entity-wide risk
- **Metrics and targets**:
  - **Scope 1 GHG emissions**: direct emissions from owned/controlled sources (required)
  - **Scope 2 GHG emissions**: indirect from purchased electricity/heat/cooling (required)
  - **Scope 3 GHG emissions**: all other indirect emissions in value chain (required if material; cross-industry metric)
  - **GHG intensity metric**: absolute GHG / revenue or other denominator
  - Capital deployment toward climate risks/opportunities
  - Internal carbon price
  - Remuneration linked to climate targets
  - Climate-related targets: absolute or intensity; base year; milestones
- **Industry-based metrics**: cross-reference to SASB industry standards for sector-specific climate metrics

## Physical vs. Transition Risk Framework
- **Physical risks**: acute (extreme weather events — floods, storms, wildfires) and chronic (sea level rise, temperature change, water scarcity) — effects on assets, operations, supply chains
- **Transition risks**: policy/legal (carbon pricing, regulations); technology (clean energy shift); market (changing demand); reputational (stakeholder pressure)
- **Climate opportunities**: resource efficiency; clean energy; products/services; markets; resilience

## Scenario Analysis
- Not prescriptive about which scenarios — but must be relevant and consistent with best available science
- At minimum: one scenario consistent with international agreements to limit warming to 1.5°C and one higher-warming scenario
- Disclose: scenarios used; key assumptions; time horizons; how results used in strategy assessment
- Climate transition plan: near-term and medium-term targets; decarbonisation levers; capital allocation

## GHG Accounting — Metrics Standards Referenced
- Greenhouse Gas Protocol (GHGP) Corporate Standard for Scope 1, 2, 3
- GHG Protocol Scope 2 Guidance for market-based vs. location-based Scope 2
- Paris Agreement alignment metrics

## Key Disclosures
- Scope 1, 2, 3 GHG emissions (tCO2e) and intensity
- Climate-related risks and opportunities: description, time horizon, likelihood/magnitude
- Scenario analysis: scenarios used, key assumptions, qualitative/quantitative results
- Transition plan (if applicable): targets, milestones, actions, investment plans
- Financial effects: quantitative where possible (effects on revenue, capex, opex, assets, liabilities)
- Internal carbon price used in capital allocation decisions
- Percentage of executive remuneration linked to climate targets

## Relationship to Other Climate Frameworks
- **Replaces/subsumes TCFD**: TCFD recommendations are substantially incorporated into IFRS S2
- **CSRD/ESRS E1 (EU)**: EU standard covers similar ground with additional requirements (transition plan detail; biodiversity crossovers; double materiality); entities reporting under both need reconciliation
- **SEC Climate Rule**: US SEC 2024 climate disclosure rule requires Scope 1 and 2 (only) for large accelerated filers; voluntary Scope 3; safe harbour for Scope 3; less comprehensive than IFRS S2
- **CDP**: IFRS S2 disclosures can be used to respond to CDP climate questionnaire

## Sector Highlights (Nigeria/Africa Context)
- **Oil and gas (IFRS 6 / ASC 932 entities)**: Scope 1 and 2 from operations; Scope 3 Category 11 (use of sold products) is often largest emissions category; stranded asset risk; IEA NZE2050 scenario for transition analysis
- **Banking**: Scope 3 Category 15 (financed emissions); climate scenario stress testing (NGFS scenarios); TCFD-aligned loan portfolio analysis
- **Power generation**: Physical risk to infrastructure; transition risk from fossil fuel plants

## Common Judgment Areas
- Materiality assessment for specific climate risks in entity's value chain
- Scope 3 boundary — which categories are material; data quality and estimation
- Scenario selection and assumptions (especially long-horizon economic assumptions)
- Quantifying financial effects of climate risks on balance sheet and P&L
- Transition plan credibility and consistency with stated targets
