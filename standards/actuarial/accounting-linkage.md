# Actuarial ↔ Accounting Standards Linkage

## Quick Reference

| Accounting Standard | Actuarial Input Required | Primary ISAP |
|--------------------|------------------------|-------------|
| [IAS 19](../ias/ias-19.md) — Employee Benefits | DBO; service cost; net interest; actuarial gains/losses | ISAP 1, ISAP 3 |
| [IPSAS 39](../ipsas/ipsas-39.md) — Employee Benefits (Public Sector) | Same as IAS 19; government plan specific considerations | ISAP 1, ISAP 2 |
| [IFRS 17](../ifrs/ifrs-17.md) — Insurance Contracts | Fulfilment cash flows; risk adjustment; CSM; discount rates | ISAP 4 |
| [IAS 37](../ias/ias-37.md) — Provisions | Long-tail claim reserves; ARO; asbestos; product liability | ISAP 1, ISAP 3 |
| [IPSAS 42](../ipsas/ipsas-42.md) — Social Benefits | Actuarial valuation of pension liabilities; social security obligations | ISAP 2 |
| [IFRS 4](../ifrs/) — Legacy Insurance | Technical provisions (being replaced by IFRS 17) | ISAP 3 |
| Solvency II / RBC / NAICOM | Best estimate liability; risk margin; SCR | ISAP 6 |
| [IAS 36](../ias/ias-36.md) — Impairment | DCF (VIU) may use actuarially-modelled cash flows for insurers | ISAP 1 |

## Discount Rate Selection — Africa Context
IAS 19.83: Discount rate = yield on high-quality corporate bonds at balance sheet date. In markets with no deep corporate bond market (Nigeria, most sub-Saharan Africa), use government bond yield.

- Nigeria: FGN Eurobond yield or FGN local bond yield (adjusted for liquidity premium)
- South Africa: SAGBs widely used (deep, liquid corporate bond market exists)
- Actuaries must document rationale for rate selection
