# ISAP 1: General Actuarial Practice

## What It Addresses
Foundational standard applying to all actuarial work. Establishes principles of relevance, completeness, reasonableness, and appropriate communication.

## Key Requirements
- **Scope**: Define the assignment; identify the principal; specify intended use
- **Professional judgement**: Apply appropriate actuarial methods; consider sensitivity to assumptions; flag material limitations
- **Data**: Assess data for completeness, accuracy, and relevance; document data adjustments
- **Assumptions**: Select assumptions appropriate for the purpose; distinguish prescribed vs. best estimate assumptions
- **Communication**: Written report must include: purpose; scope; data summary; methods; assumptions; results; limitations; reliance on others; professional standards applied
- **Uncertainty**: Disclose sources of material uncertainty; sensitivity analysis where appropriate

## Actuarial Assumptions — Key Categories
- **Demographic**: Mortality (life tables); morbidity; retirement rates; lapse rates; disability incidence
- **Economic**: Discount rate; salary inflation; price inflation; investment return
- **Policyholder behaviour**: Lapses; surrenders; conversion options; anti-selection
