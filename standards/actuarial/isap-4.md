# ISAP 4: Actuarial Practice Under IFRS 17

## What It Addresses
Guidance for actuaries preparing or reviewing actuarial inputs for IFRS 17. Covers the three measurement models and the actuarial judgements they require.

## IFRS 17 Measurement Models

### General Measurement Model (GMM)
- **Fulfilment Cash Flows**: Best estimate future cash flows + Risk Adjustment + Contractual Service Margin
- **Best estimate cash flows**: Unbiased, probability-weighted estimate of all future cash inflows and outflows
- **Risk adjustment (RA)**: Compensation for non-financial risk uncertainty; entity-specific; confidence interval method (e.g., 75th percentile) most common
- **CSM**: Unearned profit at inception; released to insurance revenue over coverage period

### Premium Allocation Approach (PAA)
- Simplified model for short-duration contracts (≤ 12 months); liability = unearned premium
- No CSM required; suitable for most P&C short-tail business

### Variable Fee Approach (VFA)
- For contracts with direct participation features (unit-linked, with-profits)
- CSM absorbs investment return changes; reduces P&L volatility

## Key Actuarial Inputs
- Cash flow projection models: premium, claim, and expense cash flows by cohort (annual cohorts mandatory)
- Discount rates: locked-in rate for CSM accretion; current rate for fulfilment cash flows (OCI option available)
- Risk adjustment: statistical methods; confidence interval approach; cost of capital method
- Transition: Modified retrospective or fair value approach for in-force contracts
