# ISAP 3: Actuarial Practice Under IFRS

## What It Addresses
Guidance for actuaries performing work in support of financial reporting under IFRS — primarily IAS 19 (employee benefits), IAS 37 (long-tail provisions), IFRS 4 (legacy insurance), and the transition to IFRS 17.

## IAS 19 Actuarial Requirements

### Defined Benefit Obligation (DBO)
- **Projected Unit Credit (PUC) method**: Mandatory per IAS 19 — each period of service gives rise to additional unit of benefit entitlement
- **Discount rate**: High-quality corporate bond yield (or government bond yield in markets with no deep corporate bond market — relevant for Nigeria, most African markets)
- **Key assumptions**: Salary growth; discount rate; mortality (country-specific life tables); employee turnover; retirement age
- **Actuarial gains/losses**: Recognised in OCI (not recycled to P&L); presented in SOCIE

### Service Cost and Net Interest
- **Current service cost**: Increase in PV of DBO from employee service in current period → P&L
- **Net interest cost**: Interest on DBO net of plan assets → P&L (at discount rate)
- **Past service cost / curtailment gains**: P&L immediately on plan amendment

## IAS 37 Long-Tail Provisions
- Actuarial methods used for: asbestos/mesothelioma claims; product liability; insurance guarantee fund obligations; nuclear decommissioning (ARO)
- Discount rate for provisions: Pre-tax rate reflecting current market assessments of time value and specific risks per IAS 37.47
